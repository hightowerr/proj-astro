- **Stripe processing fee not recoverable on refund** — Stripe does not return processing fees on refunds (standard policy since October 2023). Platform absorbs ~34p (1.4% + 20p for UK cards) per refunded Connect payment. With a 50p flat application fee and ~34p processing cost, the platform nets ~16p per booking. On refund (`stripe-refund.ts:199-201`): `reverse_transfer: true` debits the merchant's connected account (merchant made whole), `refund_application_fee: true` returns the 50p to the customer (customer made whole), Stripe keeps the 34p processing fee (platform absorbs). The spec (13-refund-reverse-transfer.md:50) documents this as deliberate: "neither party should be penalised. The platform absorbs the fee loss." Root cause (Moral Hazard): complete risk separation — customers and merchants bear zero cost for cancellations, the platform absorbs all processing fee losses. At low volume this is a feature (generous refunds reduce £15+ chargebacks, improve trust, simplify operations). At scale it becomes structurally unsustainable — **break-even refund rate is ~32%** at standard conditions (16p margin / 50p total = refunds must stay below 32% to remain profitable). Root cause (Margin of Safety): the 50p flat app fee creates an **inverted margin structure** — higher deposits have THINNER margins because Stripe's percentage-based processing scales with deposit amount but the flat fee doesn't. A £10 deposit = 34p processing = 16p margin (32% break-even). A £20 deposit = 48p processing = 2p margin (**6% break-even**). International cards (2.9% + 20p) on £10 = 49p processing = 1p margin (**3% break-even**). The refund cost is a symptom; the flat fee model's mismatch with percentage-based processing is the structural vulnerability. **Decision: accept at current scale, add monitoring via progressive tripwires (parked in roadmap.md).** No fee structure changes, no cancellation fees, no merchant pass-through. The current policy is correct for growth phase — premature fee friction harms adoption more than 34p per refund at low volume. Four tripwires defined (see roadmap.md): (1) visibility — measure the fee loss when first Connect refund occurs, (2) 10% refund rate — investigate what's driving it (quarterly at 5+ merchants), (3) 20% refund rate — start shaping cancellation fee infrastructure (quarterly at 10+ merchants), (4) per-merchant outlier — individual rate > 2× platform average for 2+ months (monthly at 10+ merchants). **Not chosen**: customer cancellation fee (premature — introduces friction in growth phase; build when Tripwire 3 fires). **Not chosen**: merchant pass-through fee (unfair — merchants don't control customer cancellation behavior). **Not chosen**: percentage-based app fee (correct long-term but significant pricing change; flag as pricing review when high-deposit verticals onboard, not as a refund fix). **Not chosen**: reduce refund window (`cancelCutoffMinutes` is merchant-configurable; platform shouldn't override). No existing monitoring: no refund rate metric, no fee loss tracking, no per-merchant refund volume. The dashboard shows "Retained" and "Refunded" totals (`getMonthlyFinancialStats()`) but not the platform's processing fee loss. Not surfaced to merchants (deliberate — platform cost of business). **Cross-dependency**: "Partial refunds with Connect" in roadmap.md would change the fee reversal behavior — must decide: proportional fee return, full return on any partial, or no return on partials. **Analysis**: Mental models report (Moral Hazard, Tripwire, Margin of Safety — all converge) at `mcp-go/Mental Models/WorkSpace/26-07-01_06-27-45_stripe_processing_fee_not_recoverable_refund/analysis_report.md`.
