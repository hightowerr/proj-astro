# Recoupling

## Description
Recoupling is the response strategy used by established companies when faced with decoupling. When others break apart the customer value chain, incumbents attempt to glue the activities back together by requiring customers to consume them jointly, even if customers prefer to fulfill only some activities with the incumbent and others with the decoupler. Companies achieve recoupling through various means: contracts with customers, reducing product compatibility, controlling platform standards, using legal measures to enforce terms of use, and closing software systems. This defensive strategy aims to preserve the traditional business model when parts of the value chain are being performed by competitors.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
Recoupling should be avoided when it goes against the grain of customer desires, as this often leads to customer dissatisfaction and potential market exit. Use caution when recoupling efforts are expensive to implement and maintain, potentially reducing profitability. This strategy should be avoided if it risks regulatory action for anticompetitive behavior. Also avoid recoupling when the market has fundamentally shifted and customers have already embraced decoupled services. Be cautious if recoupling efforts might damage brand reputation or relationships with customers.

## Thinking Steps
1. Assess whether customers really want the activities to be coupled together
2. Evaluate the cost of implementing recoupling measures versus their likely effectiveness
3. Analyze the legal risks associated with different recoupling strategies
4. Consider how customers might work around your recoupling efforts
5. Calculate the potential impact on customer satisfaction and retention
6. Explore less confrontational alternatives to complete recoupling
7. Plan for the possibility that recoupling efforts may fail in the long term
8. Consider how competitors might respond to your recoupling efforts
9. Evaluate the sustainability of your recoupling strategy over time
10. Prepare for potential regulatory challenges to your recoupling approach

## Coaching Questions
- How are customers currently decoupling your services, and what benefits do they get?
- What would happen if you allowed customers to continue decoupling?
- Are your recoupling efforts aligned with customer interests or just business interests?
- What legal risks are associated with your proposed recoupling strategy?
- How would customers likely respond to different recoupling measures?
- What alternatives could you offer that would achieve your goals without forcing coupling?
- How are competitors using recoupling strategies in your industry?
- What would happen to your business if all recoupling efforts failed?
- How might customers find ways to work around your recoupling measures?
- Are there ways to maintain customer relationships without forcing complete recoupling?
