# Start With The Why

## Description
Start With The Why is a principle that emphasizes beginning problem-solving and communication by establishing the purpose or motivation behind our actions. This approach, based on Simon Sinek's Golden Circle model, suggests that we should start by explaining why we do what we do (our purpose, cause, belief), then how we do it (our specific approach, values), and finally what we do (our products, services, results). This model posits that people don't buy what you do, they buy why you do it, and that understanding the fundamental purpose behind actions is crucial for motivation and effective communication.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This approach should be used with caution when working with audiences who are primarily interested in concrete details rather than purposes. Avoid using this when under tight time constraints where only results matter. It may not be appropriate in highly technical fields where specific procedures are more important than underlying purposes. Use caution when the "why" is controversial, complex, or not well-established within your organization. Also avoid when the audience has little interest in the purpose behind an action and only cares about outcomes.

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify your core purpose, cause, or belief that drives your actions
2. Clarify how you fulfill your purpose through your specific approach
3. Articulate what you do as a manifestation of your purpose and approach
4. Communicate the purpose first to establish connection and meaning
5. Explain your approach as a way of executing your purpose
6. Present your products, services or results as outcomes of your purpose-driven approach
7. Evaluate whether your current communications start with purpose
8. Assess if your organization's actions align with its stated purpose
9. Consider how your "why" connects with your audience's values
10. Test whether your "why" is compelling enough to drive action

## Coaching Questions
- Why do you do what you do?
- What is the purpose behind your organization's existence?
- How do your products or services manifest your core purpose?
- What drives your passion for your work?
- What cause or belief do you represent?
- How would you explain your purpose to someone who doesn't understand your industry?
- What would happen if you stopped focusing on your purpose?
- How does your "why" differentiate you from competitors?
- How do you ensure your team understands and connects with the "why"?
- How do you align your actions with your stated purpose?
