### Description

The Barbell Strategy is a way to move from fragility toward antifragility by combining two extremes: (1) a conservative, high-safety base that protects against ruin, and (2) a smaller allocation to high-upside, bounded-downside opportunities. It rejects the “golden mean” when the middle creates hidden fragility.

---

### When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)

- When you cannot clearly bound the downside on the speculative side.
- When the “safe” side is only apparently safe (e.g., depends on leverage or fragile institutions).
- When the problem domain requires continuous medium-risk engagement (rare, but possible).

### Thinking Steps

1. **Define the ruin boundary**
   What outcome is unacceptable and irreversible (bankruptcy, health collapse, reputational ruin)?
2. **Build the “safe” side first**
   Allocate the majority of resources to the most robust options: redundancy, cash/runway, simple reliability.
3. **Design the speculative side as options**
   Choose small bets where you can lose a known amount but the upside is large and open-ended.
4. **Eliminate the fragile middle**
   Identify “moderate risk” positions that look prudent but concentrate hidden downside (especially under fat tails).
5. **Ensure independence**
   Keep speculative bets as uncorrelated as possible; diversify across distinct sources of upside.
6. **Rebalance toward safety after wins**
   Convert realized upside into increased robustness rather than increasing fragility via lifestyle/commitment creep.
7. **Periodically re-audit hidden downside**
   Check that the speculative side hasn’t accreted leverage, obligations, or path-dependent traps.

### Coaching Questions

- What’s the one outcome you must avoid at all costs?
- If your plan goes wrong, where does the damage become irreversible?
- What is the smallest bet that could still materially change outcomes if it works?
- Which “reasonable compromise” is actually a hidden tail-risk position?
