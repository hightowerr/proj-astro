## Mental Model = Efficiency vs Adaptability (Yin–Yang Tension)

**Category = Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
Efficiency and adaptability often trade off. Efficiency optimizes performance under current conditions; adaptability preserves viability under changing and uncertain conditions. In evolving systems, sustainability is a dynamic balance: updating structures requires updating functions, and vice versa, across nested scales.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When conditions are stable and the time horizon is short (efficiency can dominate).
- When you’re using “adaptability” as a vague excuse to avoid hard trade-offs.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Resilience; brittleness; sustainability; evolution; optimization vs robustness.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Define the time horizons (short vs long) and what counts as success.
2. Identify what efficiency is optimizing (throughput, cost, energy, time).
3. Identify adaptability requirements (diversity, slack, optionality, learning).
4. Map where efficiency reduces adaptability (brittle monocultures, lock-in).
5. Choose a balance: preserve critical adaptability while improving efficiency where safe.

**Coaching Questions:**
- What are we optimizing for the short run, and what might we be sacrificing long term?
- Where do we need slack/diversity to survive change?
- Which efficiencies create irreversible lock-in?
