## Mental Model = Energy Metabolism

**Category = Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
Energy metabolism focuses on the energy throughput of a society (or subsystem) required to maintain structures and perform functions. It emphasizes that energy is not just priced input; it is a physical flow that enables activity, complexity, and growth—and therefore interacts strongly with rebound and constraint dynamics.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When energy is not a relevant constraint for the decision at hand.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Energy demand; energy intensity; societal metabolism; transition feasibility; growth constraints.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Define the boundary and the energy flows (primary energy, losses, end-use).
2. Map energy use to functions (transport, industry, households, services).
3. Track totals and intensities over time; look for structural shifts.
4. Evaluate interventions for rebound/backfire pathways.
5. Check feasibility and viability (including EROI where relevant).

**Coaching Questions:**
- What functions does energy enable here, and what expands when energy gets cheaper?
- Are totals rising even as intensity falls?
- What energy constraints bind first: supply, quality, or environmental sinks?
