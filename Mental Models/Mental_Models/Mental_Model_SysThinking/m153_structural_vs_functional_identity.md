## Mental Model = Structural vs Functional Identity

**Category = Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
Structural identity focuses on the local “how/what” (the structure and its construction). Functional identity focuses on the larger-scale “why/what” (the function in context). The same function can be realized by different structures (airplane vs balloon), and the same structure can serve different functions depending on context.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When structure and function are tightly coupled and alternatives are irrelevant.

**Keywords for Situations:**
System design; technology selection; trade-offs; requirements; identity; purpose.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. State the function (why/what) in its context.
2. List candidate structures (how/what) that could realize it.
3. Evaluate constraints at both scales (local feasibility vs contextual fit).
4. Check for category errors: are we measuring structure while claiming function?
5. Keep room for multiple realizations (avoid single-solution lock-in).

**Coaching Questions:**
- Are we arguing about what it *is* or what it *does*?
- What alternative structures could deliver the same function?
- Which constraints are local, and which are contextual/system-level?
