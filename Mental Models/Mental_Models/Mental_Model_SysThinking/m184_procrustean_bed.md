### Description

The Procrustean Bed is the error of forcing reality to fit a simplified model, category, or plan—cutting off what doesn’t fit and stretching what does. It is especially damaging in nonlinear, complex systems where the simplification is not merely imperfect but actively misleading, often creating fragility by suppressing variability and adaptation.

---

### When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)

- When you need standardization for interoperability but can maintain local adaptation at the edges.
- When the system is engineered and truly linear within the operating range (rare in human systems).

---

### Keywords for Situations

Oversimplification, linearization, standardization, complex systems, variability, “comfort trap,” model mismatch.

---

### Thinking Steps

1. **Name the forced fit**
   What model/category is being imposed, and what parts of reality are being cut or stretched?
2. **Check for nonlinearity**
   Does the system have thresholds, saturation, or “less is more” zones where linear rules fail?
3. **Identify what variability carries**
   What information or adaptation does normal variability provide?
4. **Preserve heterogeneity**
   Allow local variation where it reduces fragility (different solutions across contexts).
5. **Prefer subtractive fixes**
   Remove the harmful constraint or policy rather than adding more complexity to “manage” it.
6. **Re-test under stress**
   Evaluate the model’s fit under shocks, not only in average conditions.

---

### Coaching Questions

- What are we forcing to fit our plan that doesn’t naturally fit?
- Where does the linear rule break (thresholds, reversals, saturation)?
- What adaptation are we destroying by smoothing everything?
- Which constraint could we remove to restore the system’s natural fit?
