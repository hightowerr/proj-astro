## Mental Model = Tragedy of Change

**Category = Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
The tragedy of change is the predicament that an evolving social system must become a different system while maintaining its individuality—like changing an airplane while flying it. Sustainability decisions require choosing what to preserve and what to sacrifice under uncertainty, disagreement, and power relations.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When incremental change is insufficient and a controlled shutdown/rebuild is possible (rare at societal scales).
- When “tragedy” framing becomes fatalism rather than a prompt for wiser trade-offs.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Transitions; sustainability; identity; trade-offs; uncertainty; governance.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Define what must remain continuous (identity, critical functions).
2. Define what must change for viability (constraints, environment, resources).
3. Surface stakeholder narratives and conflicts about “what to preserve”.
4. Identify feasible transition paths with feedback and learning loops.
5. Choose changes that keep the system operational while reducing long-run risk.

**Coaching Questions:**
- What do we refuse to lose—and what might we need to lose to survive?
- What constraints make change urgent?
- What is the smallest change that tests feasibility without crashing the plane?
