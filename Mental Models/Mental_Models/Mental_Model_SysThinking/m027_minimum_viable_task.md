**Category:** Systems Thinking  

---

**Description:**  
The *Minimum Viable Task (MVT)* is the smallest actionable unit that meaningfully advances progress toward a larger goal. It borrows the logic of “Minimum Viable Product” from lean methodology but applies it to personal productivity and project management. The model recognizes that progress often stalls when goals are too large or abstract, so it reframes work as a sequence of minimal, executable steps that yield immediate feedback and build momentum. Each MVT is simple, focused, and achievable within a short time window—reducing cognitive load while reinforcing a sense of progress.

---

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**  
- When strategic clarity is missing — breaking work into smaller pieces won’t help if you don’t yet know the destination.  
- When interdependencies between tasks are high — excessive fragmentation can cause coordination costs.  
- When the task requires sustained deep work — micro-tasks can interrupt flow or lead to superficial output.  
- When teams misuse it as an excuse for “busyness” instead of meaningful progress.  

---

**Keywords for Situations:**  
- Procrastination or task paralysis  
- Large projects with unclear starting points  
- Perfectionism blocking momentum  
- Goal decomposition / incremental progress  
- Lean productivity / habit formation  

---

**Thinking Steps:**  
1. **Define the goal:** Clearly state the overarching outcome you’re aiming for.  
2. **Identify the bottleneck:** What’s currently stopping progress? Complexity, fear, or lack of clarity?  
3. **Reduce scope:** Ask, *“What’s the smallest next step that still creates movement or feedback?”*  
4. **Set a constraint:** Add a time limit (e.g., 10–15 minutes) or a single deliverable boundary.  
5. **Execute immediately:** Start and finish the MVT in one sitting.  
6. **Reflect and iterate:** Evaluate what you learned, then define the next MVT based on feedback.  

---

**Coaching Questions:**  
- What’s the *next smallest* action that would make this project move forward?  
- If I only had 10 minutes, what progress could I make right now?  
- What’s preventing me from starting — and how can I make the first step smaller?  
- How will I know this small action is actually moving me closer to the goal?  
- What feedback can I get from this step before committing further effort?
