## Mental Model = Holon

**Category = Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
A holon is an entity that is simultaneously a whole and a part. It has constraints from the macroscopic view (as a whole) and from the microscopic view (as a part). Many real systems (social, ecological, biological) are best understood as nested holons with dual identities.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When the system is flat/non-hierarchical and part–whole dynamics are weak.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Nested hierarchies; part–whole; system boundaries; roles; identity; governance.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Identify the holon and the level where it is a “whole”.
2. Identify the larger system where it is a “part”.
3. List constraints and objectives at both levels.
4. Surface conflicts between local and global optimization.
5. Design interventions that respect both identities.

**Coaching Questions:**
- At which level is this thing a “whole”, and at which is it a “part”?
- What constraints dominate locally vs at the larger scale?
- Which metrics are appropriate for each level?
