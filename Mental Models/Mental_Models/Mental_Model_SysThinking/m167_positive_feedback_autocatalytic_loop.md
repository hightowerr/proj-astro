## Mental Model = Positive Feedback (Autocatalytic Loop)

**Category = Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
Positive feedback is a reinforcing loop where outputs feed back as inputs to amplify the process (e.g., reinvesting surplus to generate more surplus). Such loops can drive rapid growth but cannot proceed unchecked for long in finite environments; without damping/control they overshoot constraints and fail.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When you’re modeling long-run dynamics without explicit constraints.
- When you mistake short-run exponential growth for a permanent regime.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Compounding; runaway growth; overshoot; carrying capacity; autocatalysis; boom-bust.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Identify the reinforcing loop and what is being reinvested/amplified.
2. Identify constraints/carrying capacity and the likely limiting factors.
3. Identify damping/control loops (regulation, depletion, saturation).
4. Estimate time-to-constraint under exponential assumptions.
5. Design controls that prevent overshoot while preserving useful growth.

**Coaching Questions:**
- What is the “more leads to more” mechanism here?
- What constraint will bind first, and how do we know?
- What control loop can damp runaway growth without killing the system?
