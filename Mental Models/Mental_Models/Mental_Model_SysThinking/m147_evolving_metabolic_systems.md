## Mental Model = Evolving Metabolic Systems

**Category = Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
Evolving metabolic systems (living systems, societies, ecosystems) persist by maintaining flows of energy and materials. They are open, adaptive, and historically dependent. Because structure and function change over time, any single fixed model of “efficiency” or “optimality” tends to fail outside a narrow context.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When you actually have a stable, closed, engineered system with fixed goals.
- When you need only a narrow local estimate and can safely ignore adaptation.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Society-as-metabolism; sustainability; open systems; thermodynamics; long-run policy; evolution.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Define the system boundary and the critical throughput flows.
2. Identify nested levels (households → sectors → society; cells → organs → organism).
3. Separate structural identity (how) from functional identity (why) at each level.
4. Anticipate adaptation: how flows and behaviors change after interventions.
5. Prefer scenario-based robustness over point prediction.

**Coaching Questions:**
- What flows (energy/materials) must exist for this system to persist?
- Which parts are open to adaptation, and what do they optimize?
- What changes when you scale the intervention from micro to macro?
