## Mental Model = Societal Metabolism

**Category = Systems Thinking / Economics**
**Description:**
Societal metabolism treats an economy as a metabolic system: it requires continuous inflows of energy and materials and produces outflows of wastes. It shifts analysis from purely monetary indicators to physical throughput, making constraints and rebound dynamics visible at the system level.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When the question is purely local and physical throughput is irrelevant.
- When system boundaries are unclear (results can become “true but unhelpful”).

**Keywords for Situations:**
Energy consumption; CO2; resource depletion; throughput; biophysical constraints; sustainability.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Choose the boundary (city, nation, sector) and time horizon.
2. Identify key throughput flows (energy, water, materials) and wastes.
3. Link flows to activities/functions (mobility, food, housing).
4. Track totals and per-capita/per-GDP intensities (but don’t confuse them).
5. Evaluate interventions for both intensity and total throughput effects.

**Coaching Questions:**
- What are the dominant inflows and outflows for this society/system?
- Are we improving intensity while totals still rise?
- What would it take for totals to fall (not just intensity)?
