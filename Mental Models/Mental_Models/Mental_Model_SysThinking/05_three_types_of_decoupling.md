# Three Types of Decoupling

## Description
The book identifies three distinct types of decoupling that startups use to disrupt markets by breaking the links between customer value chain activities. Value-creation decoupling involves businesses that break the links between two or more value-creating activities, where the decoupler offers one of these activities while the incumbent retains another. Value-eroding decoupling includes businesses that break the links between value-eroding and value-creating activities, allowing customers to avoid the unpleasant activity. Value-charging decoupling includes businesses that decouple value-creating and value-charging activities, breaking the link between buying and paying for services. Understanding these types helps identify how disruption might occur in any industry.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This framework should be used with caution when the types of decoupling aren't clearly distinct in a given industry, as some companies may exhibit characteristics of multiple types. Avoid overfitting the framework to situations where the primary disruption isn't based on activity decoupling. Use caution in heavily regulated industries where legal constraints may prevent certain types of decoupling. Also be careful not to assume that all market changes follow these three patterns, as some may result from other factors like technology improvements or cost reductions.

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify which activities in your market create value for customers
2. Identify which activities in your market erode value for customers
3. Identify which activities in your market capture value for businesses
4. Assess which combinations of activities might be prime candidates for separation
5. Determine which type of decoupling would most likely succeed in your market
6. Evaluate if customers would benefit from separating activities of each type
7. Consider how technology could enable each type of decoupling in your industry
8. Analyze potential responses from incumbents to each type of decoupling
9. Identify which type of decoupling your competitors might pursue
10. Develop strategies to either leverage or defend against each type of decoupling

## Coaching Questions
- Which activities in your industry create value but could potentially be separated from other activities?
- What value-eroding activities could a competitor eliminate while still providing core services?
- How might value-charging activities be separated from value-creating activities in your industry?
- What would happen if customers could experience your value-creating activity without your value-charging activity?
- Which type of decoupling would have the biggest impact on your business model?
- How might customers in your market benefit from each type of activity separation?
- What technological capabilities would be required for each type of decoupling to succeed?
- How would incumbents likely respond to each type of decoupling in your industry?
- Which type of decoupling is already happening in adjacent industries?
- How might you leverage one of these types of decoupling to your advantage?
