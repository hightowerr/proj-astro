## Mental Model = Scale-Dependent Narratives

**Category = Systems Thinking / Information**
**Description:**
In complex systems, quantification is not purely “substantive”; it depends on procedural choices about boundaries, scales, and purposes. Different narratives can yield non-equivalent but legitimate representations (e.g., what “north-facing” means depends on whether you use a globe or a flat map). This is why “efficiency” can have multiple valid definitions that produce different conclusions.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When you need a single definitive metric without stating boundary conditions.
- When you treat one narrative as the whole truth across all scales.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Modeling; indicators; boundary choice; stakeholders; epistemology; sustainability.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. State the purpose: what decision is the model supporting?
2. Choose boundary and scale explicitly (spatial + temporal).
3. Identify alternative legitimate narratives used by other stakeholders.
4. Check how conclusions change across narratives/scales.
5. Report results as conditional on narrative/boundary, not “the truth”.

**Coaching Questions:**
- What scale makes this question meaningful?
- Which stakeholders use a different narrative, and why?
- Which definitions of “efficiency” are being mixed or conflated?
