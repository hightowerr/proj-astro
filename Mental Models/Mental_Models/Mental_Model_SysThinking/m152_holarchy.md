## Mental Model = Holarchy

**Category = Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
A holarchy is a nested hierarchy of holons. Each level is a “whole” relative to lower levels and a “part” relative to higher levels. An intervention that improves performance at one level can destabilize identity or constraints at another, which is central to sustainability and governance problems.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When hierarchy is weak and the system is better modeled as a network at one scale.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Nested systems; multi-scale analysis; governance; resilience; sustainability.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Map the nested levels (micro → meso → macro).
2. Define identities/functions at each level.
3. Identify constraints that flow downward (macro) and dependencies that flow upward (micro).
4. Evaluate how changes propagate across levels.
5. Prefer interventions that improve coherence across levels, not only one level.

**Coaching Questions:**
- Which level are we optimizing, and which levels pay the cost?
- What must remain stable for this holarchy to keep its identity?
- Where are the strongest cross-level feedback loops?
