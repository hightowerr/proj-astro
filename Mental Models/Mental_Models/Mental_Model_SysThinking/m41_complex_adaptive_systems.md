## Mental Model = Complex Adaptive Systems

**Category = Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
Complex Adaptive Systems (CAS) exhibit behavior that is not reducible to their parts. Individual components may be simple, but their interactions can produce nonlinear, emergent outcomes. CAS adapt to their environment and to interventions; they are path-dependent (“have memory”), and often self-organize via a few basic rules rather than centralized control. City traffic is a classic example: a single car explains little; interactions among many cars produce congestion patterns, waves, and adaptation.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When you’re treating the system as predictable and deterministic.
- When you assume “more control” is always better than shaping rules/constraints.
- When you infer global behavior directly from a single part.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Interdependence; nonlinear effects; self-organization; emergence; adaptation; feedback loops; path dependence; markets; ecosystems; traffic; organizations.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Identify the agents/parts and what they optimize locally.
2. Map interactions and feedback loops (including delays).
3. Look for constraints/rules that shape behavior (incentives, protocols, norms).
4. Identify emergent properties you care about (stability, throughput, resilience).
5. Anticipate adaptation: how will agents respond to your intervention?
6. Prefer interventions that adjust rules/constraints and reduce harmful feedback.

**Coaching Questions:**
- What are the agents, and what do they respond to?
- Which feedback loops (reinforcing/balancing) dominate the outcome?
- If we “fix” X locally, where does the system push the cost next?
- How might the system adapt to neutralize our intervention?
- What rule/constraint change would shift behavior more robustly than a local patch?
