## Mental Model = Malthusian Instability

**Category = Systems Thinking / Economics**
**Description:**
Malthusian instability describes the tendency of self-reproducing metabolic systems to expand under favorable conditions until they exceed environmental carrying capacity. Improvements can increase surplus, which fuels expansion, which then recreates scarcity/stress at a larger scale.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When the system has enforceable constraints that prevent overshoot (hard caps, strong governance).
- When you’re analyzing short-run local dynamics without capacity limits.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Carrying capacity; overshoot; population; resource depletion; growth limits; selection pressure.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Identify the reproduction/expansion mechanism in the system.
2. Identify carrying capacity and limiting resources/sinks.
3. Analyze how surplus feeds expansion (positive feedback).
4. Identify where/when overshoot occurs and what resets the system.
5. Design constraints to keep expansion below capacity (budgets/caps/governance).

**Coaching Questions:**
- What is reproducing/expanding here, and what fuels it?
- What is the carrying capacity, and how uncertain is it?
- What would keep growth from translating into overshoot?
