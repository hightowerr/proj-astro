# Iterative Process

## Description
Iterative Process is the understanding that strategy development is not a linear, one-time activity but rather a continuous, cyclical process where insights at one stage may require revisiting choices made at other stages. This mental model acknowledges that strategy is dynamic and requires constant refinement as new information emerges, market conditions change, and the implications of strategic choices become clearer. It emphasizes feedback loops and continuous adjustment rather than static, long-term planning.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This model should be used with caution in highly regulated industries where strategy changes are constrained by external requirements. It may be problematic in organizations with cultures that resist change or in situations requiring immediate, decisive action with no opportunity for iteration. Avoid this approach if stakeholders expect static, long-term plans and resist adjustments, or if the cost of frequent strategic adjustments is prohibitive. Also be cautious in crisis situations where immediate strategic decisions are required without time for iterative refinement.

## Thinking Steps
1. Establish clear feedback mechanisms to learn from strategy execution
2. Schedule regular reviews of strategic choices and their effectiveness
3. Create systems to gather market and competitive intelligence
4. Plan for how insights in one area will trigger review of other choices
5. Design processes to quickly implement strategic adjustments
6. Ensure organizational capabilities to adapt to strategic changes
7. Establish metrics that reveal when strategies need adjustment
8. Plan how to communicate iterative changes to stakeholders
9. Balance the need for consistency with the need for adaptation
10. Build organizational tolerance for strategic adjustments and learning
