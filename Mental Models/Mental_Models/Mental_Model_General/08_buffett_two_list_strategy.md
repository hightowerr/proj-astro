# Buffett Two List Strategy

## Description
The Buffett Two List Strategy is a prioritization model attributed to Warren Buffett. The approach involves writing down your top 25 career or life goals, then circling your top 5 priorities from that list. The critical insight is that the remaining 20 goals become your "avoid list" - items you should avoid at all costs. This strategy emphasizes that success comes not just from focusing on what to do, but also from being intentional about what not to do. The model is based on the principle that your most significant accomplishments will come from concentrating all your efforts on your top priorities while actively avoiding the temptation to pursue other good opportunities that don't align with your core objectives.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
Avoid this approach when you're in the early stages of a career or business where exploration across multiple areas might be beneficial. Don't use if you're in a role or situation where broad exposure is more important than depth. Be cautious if you're in a highly dynamic environment where priorities change frequently. Avoid if you work in a role where you're required to handle many concurrent responsibilities across different areas. This approach may be problematic if your organization or role doesn't afford you the luxury to focus deeply on only a few priorities.

## Thinking Steps
1. List your top 25 goals, projects, or priorities without filtering initially
2. Review the list and identify which 5 goals are your absolute top priorities
3. Circle or separate these 5 goals from the remaining 20 items
4. Place the top 5 goals on your "Focus" list where you'll invest your primary energy
5. Place the remaining 20 goals on your "Consciously Avoid" list
6. Commit to avoiding the items on the avoid list until you've accomplished your top priorities
7. Regularly review and reassess your top 5 priorities as circumstances change
8. Be intentional about declining opportunities that don't fit your top 5 priorities
9. Monitor how you're spending your time and energy vis-à-vis your priorities
10. Adjust your lists periodically as goals are completed or circumstances change
