# Focus and Trade-offs

## Description
Focus and Trade-offs is the strategic discipline of making deliberate choices about what to do and what not to do, recognizing that effective strategy requires narrowing options and creating distinct positioning. This mental model emphasizes that attempting to serve everyone or do everything typically results in mediocrity. True strategic advantage comes from making explicit trade-offs that enable superior performance in chosen areas while accepting limitations in others. It requires the courage to say "no" to attractive opportunities that don't fit the strategic framework.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This model should be used with caution in rapidly changing markets where maintaining flexibility is more important than focus. It may be problematic for organizations in highly uncertain environments where the right focus is unclear. Avoid this approach if your market rewards broad coverage over specialization, or if your business model requires scale across many areas to be viable. Also be cautious if your organization operates in a market where customer demands are extremely diverse and consolidated into a narrow focus would lose too many customers.

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify all the potential opportunities and activities your organization could pursue
2. Assess which activities align with your strategic choices and which do not
3. Evaluate the opportunity cost of each potential activity
4. Determine which trade-offs are necessary to create distinct positioning
5. Consider the implications of not pursuing certain opportunities
6. Design systems that reinforce your focused approach
7. Communicate trade-offs clearly to stakeholders
8. Establish metrics that align with your chosen focus
9. Plan how to maintain focus over time despite pressures to expand
10. Test whether your current level of focus is appropriate for your market

## Coaching Questions
- What opportunities would you have to give up to maintain your current strategic focus?
- How do you decide which customer segments or needs to prioritize?
- What would happen if you tried to serve everyone instead of focusing?
- How do you say "no" to attractive but strategically misaligned opportunities?
- What trade-offs does your strategy require you to make?
- How do you communicate these trade-offs to your team and stakeholders?
- What metrics do you use to measure the success of your focused approach?
- How do you maintain focus when competitors expand into new areas?
- What would cause you to reconsider your current focus and trade-offs?
- How do you balance focus with the need for organizational growth?
