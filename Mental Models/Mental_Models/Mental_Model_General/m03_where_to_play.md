# Where to Play

## Description
Where to Play represents the set of choices that narrow the competitive field by defining where a company will compete. These choices include decisions about geography (which countries/regions), product types (what products/services), consumer segments (which customers), distribution channels (how to reach customers), and vertical stages of production (where along the value chain). The key is to select a playing field where the company can win, recognizing that choosing where to play also means choosing where not to play, creating necessary focus and trade-offs.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This framework should be used with caution when an organization is not ready to make trade-offs and focus on specific areas. It may be problematic for organizations that have a deeply ingrained "do everything" culture or those that are overly optimistic about their capabilities. Avoid using this approach if the organization is unwilling to exit markets or segments where they're currently operating but not winning. Also be cautious if the organization lacks deep consumer insights or market understanding to make informed where-to-play choices.

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify all potential geographies where you could compete
2. Determine which product types/services align with your capabilities and consumer needs
3. Segment your potential customers and identify which segments you can serve best
4. Evaluate available distribution channels and their alignment with your strategy
5. Map out the value chain and decide which stages to participate in
6. Assess the competitive landscape in each potential area of play
7. Evaluate your capabilities against the requirements of each potential playing field
8. Consider which areas offer the best potential for sustainable competitive advantage
9. Make explicit choices about where NOT to play as well as where to play
10. Test how your where-to-play choices align with your winning aspiration and capabilities

## Coaching Questions
- What geographies offer the best opportunities for your company to win?
- Which customer segments align best with your capabilities and value proposition?
- What products or services should you focus on to maximize your competitive advantage?
- Which distribution channels best serve your target customers and strategy?
- Where along the value chain can you create the most value and maintain competitive advantage?
- What markets or segments should you explicitly choose not to pursue?
- How do competitors define their playing fields and where are the opportunities?
- What consumer needs are not being met in your potential areas of play?
- How do your where-to-play choices reinforce your overall strategy?
- What would happen if you tried to be everything to everyone?
