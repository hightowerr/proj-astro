# Attacking Lines of Least Resistance

## Description
Attacking Lines of Least Resistance is the strategic approach of identifying and pursuing opportunities where competitors are weakest or where they have not focused their defenses. Rather than attacking competitors' strongholds directly, this approach seeks to find unoccupied or under-contested marketspaces where a company can establish a position and build strength before facing the main competition. It involves looking for unexpected directions, underserved segments, or overlooked applications of capabilities where a company can gain initial traction with less resistance.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This model should be used with caution in markets where first-mover advantage is not sustainable or where early entry is actually disadvantageous. It may be problematic when your capabilities align most naturally with contested market areas or when you have strong competitive advantages in the main market. Avoid this approach if you lack the resources to explore multiple market opportunities or if your organization struggles to execute in non-obvious market areas. Also be cautious in markets where the "obvious" direction actually offers the best long-term potential despite being competitive.

## Thinking Steps
1. Map out the current competitive landscape and identify where competitors are strongest
2. Identify underserved or overlooked customer segments or needs
3. Assess your capabilities to find where they might create advantage in unexpected areas
4. Evaluate less obvious ways to apply your capabilities to market needs
5. Analyze what competitors are not doing or doing poorly
6. Consider alternative ways to serve customer needs that are currently underexplored
7. Plan how to gain initial traction with minimal direct competition
8. Design your approach to build strength and scale before facing main competition
9. Identify how to transition from the line of least resistance to larger opportunities
10. Monitor the competitive response as you establish your position

## Coaching Questions
- What areas of the market are competitors NOT focusing on?
- Where do you have capabilities that competitors lack?
- What customer needs are not being well served by current offerings?
- How could you apply your strengths in unexpected ways?
- What would your competitors find difficult to copy or respond to?
- Where could you gain initial traction with less competitive resistance?
- What market segments are underserved by current players?
- How do you build scale and strength before attracting major competition?
- What would happen if you attacked where competitors are strongest?
- How do you transition from a position of less resistance to larger market opportunities?
