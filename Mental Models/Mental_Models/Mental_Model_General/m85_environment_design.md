## Mental Model: Environment Design

### Description
Environment Design is the practice of structuring your surroundings to make good habits more obvious and likely to occur while making bad habits invisible and difficult. Since behavior is a function of both the person and their environment (B = f(P,E)), changing your environment can automatically shift your behavior without requiring additional willpower. The most powerful of all human sensory abilities is vision, so visual cues in your environment have a profound impact on your behavior. This approach involves redesigning spaces to increase exposure to positive cues and reduce exposure to negative ones.

### When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
- When you have no control over your environment (e.g., shared living/working spaces).
- When the benefits of changing the environment don't outweigh the effort required.
- When you need to build adaptability to varied environments rather than relying on a specific setup.
- When environmental changes might inconvenience others unnecessarily.

### Keywords for Situations
- Space optimization
- Cue management
- Behavioral architecture
- Visual triggers
- Context modification
- Choice architecture

### Thinking Steps
1. Identify your current habits and the environments where they occur.
2. Determine which cues in your environment lead to good or bad habits.
3. Make cues for good habits obvious and visible in your environment.
4. Remove or hide cues for bad habits from your environment.
5. Create dedicated spaces for specific activities to build associated habits.
6. Prime your environment by preparing for future actions in advance.
7. Continuously evaluate and adjust your environment based on results.

### Coaching Questions
- What visual cues in your environment currently trigger your good habits?
- What cues prompt your bad habits?
- How can you rearrange your space to make positive behaviors more obvious?
- What items can you remove from your environment to eliminate bad habits?
- How can you create distinct spaces for different activities?
- What environmental changes would make your desired habits easier to perform?
- How will you maintain your designed environment over time?
