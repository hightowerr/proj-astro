# Where Not to Play

## Description
Where Not to Play is the strategic discipline of making explicit choices about where an organization will not compete, which is as important as deciding where to play. This mental model emphasizes that a clear strategy requires saying "no" to certain markets, segments, products, or geographies to create focus and enable superior performance in chosen areas. By explicitly defining where not to play, organizations can better allocate resources, avoid distractions, and maintain strategic coherence. The practice forces difficult conversations about what to abandon or never enter.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This model should be used with caution when in rapidly evolving markets where the boundaries of opportunity are unclear. It may be problematic for organizations in the early stages of market exploration when too much focus might cause them to miss important opportunities. Avoid this approach if your market is consolidating and broad coverage is necessary for survival. Also be cautious if your business model requires diversification across areas to maintain viability or if you're in a situation where expanding into new areas is necessary to support your core business.

## Thinking Steps
1. List all the areas where your organization currently operates
2. Identify potential areas where you could operate but don't currently
3. Evaluate which areas are not aligned with your strategic choices
4. Assess the resource drain of operating in non-strategic areas
5. Consider the competitive consequences of exiting certain areas
6. Plan the process for exiting or not entering certain markets
7. Design systems that prevent re-entry into non-strategic areas
8. Communicate where not to play decisions to stakeholders
9. Establish metrics to measure the success of your focus
10. Review and update where-not-to-play choices as market conditions change

## Coaching Questions
- What markets, segments, or products should your organization explicitly not pursue?
- How do you decide which opportunities to turn away?
- What would be the consequences of exiting certain current areas of operation?
- How do you say "no" to opportunities that seem attractive but don't fit your strategy?
- What areas are you currently operating in that might be diluting your focus?
- How do you prevent your organization from drifting into non-strategic areas?
- What conversations do you need to have about exiting certain markets?
- How do you balance the risk of missing opportunities with the benefits of focus?
- What would happen if you tried to compete in all possible areas?
- How do you ensure your where-not-to-play decisions remain firm over time?
