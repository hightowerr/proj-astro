# Competing Against the Best

## Description
Competing Against the Best is the practice of focusing strategic efforts on winning against the very best competitors in your space, rather than just traditional rivals or average competitors. This approach involves identifying the strongest competitor in your specific area of competition and understanding what they do better than you. Rather than focusing inward on your own products and innovations, this model directs attention outward to learn from and ultimately surpass the top performers in your field, even if they're from unexpected quarters.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This model should be used with caution in markets where the "best" competitor is protected by regulatory advantages or other non-replicable factors. It may be problematic when the best competitor operates under fundamentally different business models that don't apply to your situation. Avoid this approach if your market is so diverse that there is no single "best" competitor, or if the cost of trying to match or exceed the best is prohibitively high relative to potential returns. Also be cautious if your organization lacks the capabilities to ever realistically compete with the best in your field.

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify who the very best competitor is in your specific area of competition
2. Look beyond traditional competitors to find the best in your space
3. Analyze what the best competitor does strategically and operationally that is better
4. Assess where the best competitor outperforms your organization
5. Determine which of the best competitor's practices are worth emulating
6. Identify opportunities to surpass the best competitor in specific areas
7. Plan how to close performance gaps with the best competitor
8. Establish metrics based on the best competitor's performance
9. Consider how to compete differently rather than just better
10. Regularly reassess who the best competitor is as markets evolve

## Coaching Questions
- Who is the very best competitor in your specific area of competition?
- What does the best competitor do that you don't or can't do?
- How do you measure your performance against the best, not just average competitors?
- What could you learn from the best competitor that would improve your performance?
- How do you avoid simply copying the best competitor without creating differentiation?
- What would it take to surpass the best competitor in your field?
- Are you looking at the right competitors, or focusing too narrowly on traditional rivals?
- How do you balance learning from the best with maintaining your unique approach?
- What prevents your organization from competing at the level of the best?
- How do you stay motivated when competing against seemingly insurmountable leaders?
