# Nested Cascades

## Description
Nested Cascades is the understanding that strategic choices exist at multiple organizational levels (corporate, sector, category, brand, function, etc.) and these choices are interconnected and influence each other. Each level has its own strategic choice cascade (winning aspiration, where to play, how to win, core capabilities, management systems) that must align with and support the cascades above and below it. This creates a system of integrated strategies across the organization rather than disconnected strategies at different levels.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This model should be used with caution in small organizations where the complexity of multiple cascades is unnecessary. It may be problematic in rapidly changing environments where maintaining alignment across levels is too time-consuming. Avoid this approach if your organization lacks the discipline to maintain alignment between levels, or if the cost of coordinating strategies across levels is prohibitive. Also be cautious in organizations where different business units operate in completely unrelated industries with no synergies.

## Thinking Steps
1. Map out the different levels of strategy in your organization
2. Define the strategic choices at each level (aspirations, where to play, how to win, etc.)
3. Identify how choices at one level influence choices at other levels
4. Assess where cascades are aligned and where gaps exist
5. Plan how to maintain alignment as strategies evolve
6. Establish communication processes between levels
7. Design governance mechanisms to coordinate cascades
8. Consider how cascades reinforce and support each other
9. Plan for situations where cascades might need to change simultaneously
10. Create systems to monitor alignment between cascades over time

## Coaching Questions
- How do the strategic choices at your level align with those above and below you?
- What happens when choices at different levels are misaligned?
- How do you ensure your functional strategy supports the business strategy?
- What mechanisms exist to coordinate strategy across different organizational levels?
- How do conflicting priorities at different levels get resolved?
- What would happen if all cascades were completely independent?
- How do you balance local optimization with overall organizational strategy?
- What processes are in place to maintain alignment between cascades?
- How do you handle situations where lower-level cascades need to change?
- How do cascades at different levels reinforce each other?
