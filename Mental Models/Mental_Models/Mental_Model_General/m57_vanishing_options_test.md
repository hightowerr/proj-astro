
# Vanishing Options Test

**Description**: A simple but powerful technique to combat Narrow Framing by forcing you to generate new alternatives. You pose the question: "You cannot choose any of the current options you're considering. What else could you do?" This constraint acts like the old saying, "Necessity is the mother of invention." It forces your mental spotlight to move elsewhere, often for the first time, revealing viable options you were previously blind to.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)**: This is a brainstorming tool, not an evaluation tool. It is meant to generate possibilities, not to decide among them. It is less useful if you already have a wide variety of strong, distinct options. Its primary function is to break a logjam when you feel stuck with one or two choices.

**Keywords for Situations**: Feeling stuck, brainstorming, problem-solving, limited choices, breaking a deadlock, narrow framing, binary choices.

**Thinking Steps**:
1.  Identify the current, limited set of options you are considering (often just one or two, as in a "whether or not" decision).
2.  Pose the core question to yourself or your team: "If a genie appeared and made it impossible for us to choose any of these current options, what would we do?"
3.  Brainstorm genuine alternatives that would become necessary in that scenario. Don't just think of minor variations of the vanished options; think about fundamentally different approaches.
4.  Add the newly generated options to your list of possibilities for full evaluation.

**Coaching Questions**:
*   "It sounds like we're stuck between Option A and Option B. Let's try the Vanishing Options Test. Imagine neither is possible. What would we have to do then?"
*   "You're asking 'whether or not' to take this job. If that offer suddenly disappeared, what would your next step be? What other paths would you explore?"
*   "Let's pretend our top two choices are off the table. Force ourselves to come up with something completely different."
*   "What if we were forbidden from choosing any of the options we've discussed so far? What's our Plan C?"
