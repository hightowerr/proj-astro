## Mental Model: Implementation Intentions

### Description
Implementation intentions are specific plans you make beforehand about when and where to act. The format is: "I will [BEHAVIOR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION]." This strategy leverages the two most common cues for habits: time and location. Rather than having vague goals like "I want to work out more," implementation intentions transform them into a concrete plan of action. Research shows people who make specific plans for when and where they will perform a new habit are more likely to follow through.

### When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
- When your daily schedule is highly unpredictable or variable.
- When external circumstances frequently change, making fixed times/places unreliable.
- When the planned time or location becomes consistently unavailable.
- When over-planning leads to inflexibility in adapting to new opportunities.

### Keywords for Situations
- Goal achievement
- Behavior change
- Time management
- Specific planning
- Scheduled actions
- Habit formation

### Thinking Steps
1. Identify the specific behavior you want to perform.
2. Choose a specific time of day when you'll do it.
3. Select a specific location where you'll do it.
4. Write your implementation intention in the format: "I will [BEHAVIOR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION]."
5. Consider potential obstacles and plan how to overcome them.
6. Review and adjust your implementation intention as needed based on results.
7. Track your consistency with the planned behavior.

### Coaching Questions
- What specific behavior do you want to perform?
- At what time of day will you do this behavior?
- In what specific location will you perform this behavior?
- What obstacles might prevent you from following through?
- How will you adjust your plan if circumstances change?
- How will you track whether you're following your implementation intention?
- What will you do if you miss your planned time/location?
