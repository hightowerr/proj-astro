# Core Capabilities

## Description
Core Capabilities represent the range and quality of activities and competencies that critically underpin specific where-to-play and how-to-win choices. These are the key activities that a company must excel at to execute its strategy successfully. Core capabilities are more than just competencies—they're capabilities that are central to winning in the chosen arena, difficult for competitors to replicate, and directly connected to the company's ability to deliver unique value to customers. They work together as an interconnected system rather than as isolated capabilities.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This model should be used with caution when an organization doesn't have a clear strategy yet, as capabilities should flow from strategic choices rather than define them. It may be problematic if the organization tries to develop too many capabilities simultaneously without focusing on those that are truly core to winning. Avoid this approach if the organization is unwilling to make the investments necessary to build or maintain critical capabilities, or if they're not prepared to divest capabilities that no longer align with their strategy.

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify the capabilities required to execute your where-to-play choices
2. Determine the capabilities needed to execute your how-to-win approach
3. Map out your current capability portfolio and assess their quality
4. Identify which capabilities are truly differentiating and which are merely required
5. Evaluate which capabilities are most difficult for competitors to replicate
6. Assess how your capabilities work together as an integrated system
7. Identify gaps between your required capabilities and current capabilities
8. Prioritize which capabilities to invest in based on strategic importance
9. Plan the development or acquisition of critical capabilities
10. Create systems to measure and maintain capability effectiveness over time

## Coaching Questions
- What capabilities are absolutely essential for your chosen way to win?
- Which of your capabilities are most difficult for competitors to replicate?
- How do your capabilities work together as an integrated system?
- What capabilities do you currently have that are not core to your strategy?
- What gaps exist between your required capabilities and current capabilities?
- How do your capabilities provide sustainable competitive advantage?
- What investments are necessary to maintain or enhance your core capabilities?
- How do you measure the effectiveness of your core capabilities?
- What would happen if a competitor developed superior capabilities in your area?
- How do you ensure your capabilities continue to evolve with changing market conditions?
