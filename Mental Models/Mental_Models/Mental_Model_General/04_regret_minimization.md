# Regret Minimization

## Description
Regret Minimization is a mental model that involves projecting yourself into the future and making present decisions based on what would minimize regrets at that time. The framework asks you to imagine yourself at age 80 looking back at your life, then deciding whether your present decision would be one you'd regret or be happy about. This approach helps focus on the long-term consequences of decisions rather than just immediate impacts. It's based on the principle that the most important questions are about which regrets you might have later in life, which enables you to make decisions that align with your long-term values and aspirations rather than short-term pressures.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
Avoid this model when facing time-critical decisions that require immediate action without long-term consideration. Use caution in situations where long-term projections are unreliable or when facing highly uncertain futures. Don't use this approach when you're making decisions for others who may have different life perspectives or time horizons. Avoid when short-term consequences are critical and immediate action is required regardless of long-term implications. Also be cautious when your long-term perspective is clouded by current emotions or stress.

## Thinking Steps
1. Project yourself into the future (e.g., age 80) and look back on your current situation
2. Consider how you would feel about your current decision from that future perspective
3. Identify what would cause the most regret if you didn't take a particular action
4. Assess what would happen if you chose the safer or easier option
5. Evaluate whether the current decision aligns with your core values and long-term aspirations
6. Consider how your future self would evaluate the risks versus opportunities
7. Think about whether you'd regret not having tried or having failed at trying
8. Compare how your future self would view action versus inaction
9. Determine if your current fears are likely to be important in the long-term future
10. Make your decision based on what would minimize regrets from a future perspective

## Coaching Questions
- How will you feel about this decision when you're 80 years old?
- What would you regret more - trying and failing, or not trying at all?
- If you fail at this endeavor, would you still be satisfied that you attempted it?
- What are you afraid of losing if you don't take this opportunity?
- What would you tell your younger self about taking this risk?
- How would you explain your decision to someone you respect?
- What would happen if you waited five more years to make this decision?
- What are the potential consequences of not making this decision?
- How would your decision change if you knew you couldn't fail?
- What story would you want to tell about this moment in your life?
