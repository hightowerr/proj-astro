# Hard Choice Matrix

## Description
The Hard Choice Matrix is a decision-making framework that helps evaluate choices by analyzing them across two dimensions: (1) importance/impact of the decision and (2) comparability of the options. This creates a 2x2 matrix with four types of decisions: No-Brainers (easy to compare, low impact), Apples/Oranges (hard to compare, low impact), Big Choices (easy to compare, high impact), and Hard Choices (hard to compare, high impact). The framework helps determine how much time and energy to invest in each decision type. No-Brainers deserve quick action, Apples/Oranges require deciding what matters most, Big Choices need proper analysis, and Hard Choices require serious contemplation.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
Avoid this model when decisions have time constraints that don't allow for careful analysis. Use caution when the impact or comparability of options is difficult to assess. Don't use this approach when decisions involve life-threatening situations that require immediate action. Avoid when the options and their impacts are too complex to fit into a simple matrix framework. Be cautious when cultural context or personal values significantly affect the decision process in ways the model doesn't account for.

## Thinking Steps
1. Assess the potential impact of your decision - what are the consequences if you choose poorly?
2. Evaluate how easily you can compare the options - are the pros and cons clearly visible?
3. Plot your decision on the matrix by determining its level of impact and comparison difficulty
4. For No-Brainer decisions: make a quick decision and move on to more important matters
5. For Apples/Oranges decisions: focus on what matters most to you personally and decide accordingly
6. For Big Choice decisions: invest time in analysis but don't overthink since the choice is clear
7. For Hard Choice decisions: allocate significant time and resources to evaluate thoroughly
8. Consider what information would help you move your decision to an easier category
9. Identify the specific elements that make your options difficult or easy to compare
10. Plan how to approach your decision based on which quadrant it falls into

## Coaching Questions
- What category does your decision fall into on the Hard Choice matrix?
- How significant are the potential consequences of this decision?
- How difficult is it to compare the options available to you?
- For this decision, are you spending the right amount of time and energy?
- What information do you need to make the comparison easier?
- How might you simplify this decision if it's currently in the Hard Choice quadrant?
- Are there ways to reduce the impact of a high-impact decision to make it easier to handle?
- What would happen if you made the opposite of your initial inclination?
- How do you know when you're over-analyzing a decision?
- What would be the impact of postponing this decision?
