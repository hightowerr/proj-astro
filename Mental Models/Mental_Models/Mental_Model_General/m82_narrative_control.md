# m82_narrative_control.md

## Description
Narrative control refers to the ability to shape how events, people, or situations are understood and remembered by controlling the story that is told about them. It involves framing events in a particular way to influence perception and interpretation. Those who control the narrative can direct attention to certain aspects while downplaying others, ultimately shaping the meaning that people assign to events. This model recognizes that facts alone don't determine understanding - it's the story we tell about those facts that matters most in shaping beliefs and decisions.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
- When transparency and honesty are more important than strategic advantage
- In situations requiring ethical disclosure of information
- When building long-term trust relationships
- When the controlled narrative could cause harm to others
- In scientific, medical, or safety contexts where accuracy is paramount
- When the audience has access to independent verification
- When the truth is ultimately more powerful than the narrative

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify who currently controls the narrative around your situation
2. Assess the current story being told and its impact on stakeholders
3. Determine what aspects of the story you want to emphasize or de-emphasize
4. Consider what evidence or facts support your preferred narrative
5. Identify what details or context might be missing from the current story
6. Plan how to present information to create your desired narrative
7. Evaluate potential counter-narratives and how to address them
8. Consider the long-term implications of narrative control for trust and relationships

## Coaching Questions
- Who is currently controlling the narrative around this situation?
- What story is being told about these events, people, or decisions?
- How is this narrative shaping people's understanding and reactions?
- What aspects of the truth are being emphasized or downplayed?
- What would I prefer the narrative to be?
- What evidence or context would support my preferred narrative?
- How can I influence the narrative without being deceptive?
- What are the potential negative consequences of controlling this narrative?
- Are there stakeholders who need access to the complete unfiltered truth?
