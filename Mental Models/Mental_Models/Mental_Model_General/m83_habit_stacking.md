## Mental Model: Habit Stacking

### Description
Habit stacking is a special form of an implementation intention that pairs a new behavior with an existing one. Rather than linking a new habit to a particular time and location, you pair it with a current habit. The formula is: "After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]." This method allows you to take advantage of the natural momentum that comes from one behavior leading into the next. It's a way to design an obvious cue for nearly any habit by stacking your new behavior on top of an old one.

### When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
- When the existing habit is inconsistent or doesn't occur with enough frequency to support the new habit.
- When the cue habit happens too infrequently to match your desired new habit frequency.
- When the existing habit occurs in a chaotic environment where you're unlikely to follow through.
- When the trigger is too vague rather than specific and immediately actionable.

### Keywords for Situations
- Building new habits
- Creating behavior chains
- Leveraging existing routines
- Cue-based behavior change
- Sequential actions
- Behavior layering

### Thinking Steps
1. Identify a current habit you do each day without fail.
2. Write down the new habit you want to perform.
3. Create your habit stack using the formula: "After [current habit], I will [new habit]."
4. Make sure the cue is highly specific and immediately actionable.
5. Start with simple, small habits that are easy to complete.
6. Consider chaining additional habits together once the initial stack is automatic.
7. Evaluate the effectiveness of your stack after a few weeks and adjust if needed.

### Coaching Questions
- What is a current habit you perform every day without thinking?
- How can you connect your new desired behavior to this existing habit?
- Is your cue specific enough to act on immediately?
- What time and place will this habit stack occur?
- How will you ensure consistency in performing this stack?
- What obstacles might prevent you from completing the stack, and how will you address them?
