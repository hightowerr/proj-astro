# Strategic Choice Cascade

## Description
The Strategic Choice Cascade is a framework for developing an integrated strategy based on five interconnected choices: winning aspiration, where to play, how to win, core capabilities, and management systems. These choices form a cascade where each level influences and supports the others, creating a coherent and unified strategy. The framework emphasizes that strategy is not a vision, plan, or optimization of the status quo, but rather an integrated set of choices that position a firm uniquely to create sustainable advantage and superior value relative to competition.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This model should be avoided when an organization lacks the commitment to make difficult trade-offs and clear choices. It may not be suitable for organizations that prefer to keep many options open simultaneously, or those that are not ready to define what winning means for them. The framework is also challenging to implement in organizations that struggle with alignment or have a culture that resists making explicit "not-to-do" choices.

## Thinking Steps
1. Define your winning aspiration - What does winning look like in your context and for your stakeholders?
2. Determine where to play - Which markets, segments, channels, and products will you compete in (and not compete in)?
3. Decide how to win - What will be your distinct way of creating value that differentiates you from competitors?
4. Identify core capabilities needed - What unique activities and competencies must you excel at to execute your strategy?
5. Design management systems - What systems, processes, and measures will support and reinforce your strategic choices?
6. Test the integration - Do your choices reinforce each other and create a coherent strategy?
7. Consider the cascade effect - How do choices at one level influence choices at other levels?
8. Validate with stakeholders - Do your choices align with your organization's capabilities and market realities?

## Coaching Questions
- What does winning mean for your organization, specifically and concretely?
- Where are you currently playing that you should stop playing, and where should you start playing?
- What is your unique way of creating value that competitors cannot easily replicate?
- What capabilities do you need to build or strengthen to execute your strategy?
- How do your management systems currently support or hinder your strategic choices?
- What difficult trade-offs must you make to have a coherent strategy?
- How will you ensure alignment across your organization around these strategic choices?
- What would need to change in your business environment for this strategy to no longer work?
- How do you measure success at each level of the cascade?
- What are the potential negative consequences of the strategic choices you're considering?
