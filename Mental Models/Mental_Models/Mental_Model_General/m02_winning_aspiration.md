# Winning Aspiration

## Description
Winning Aspiration is the foundational element of strategic thinking that defines what success looks like for an organization. It goes beyond simple financial metrics to encompass what the organization truly aims to achieve for its stakeholders. The aspiration must be specific, meaningful to employees and consumers, focused on those who matter most, and aligned with winning against the very best competition. It serves as the guiding purpose that sets the context for all other strategic choices in the cascade.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This concept should be used with caution when an organization is not prepared to commit to winning or when leadership is unwilling to define what winning actually means in their context. It may also be problematic if the aspiration is set too low (playing to participate rather than win) or if it's disconnected from the realities of the market and the organization's capabilities. Avoid using this framework if the organization is not ready to make the tough choices and investments that a true winning aspiration requires.

## Thinking Steps
1. Define what winning means in your specific context, beyond financial metrics
2. Identify who matters most - your key consumers, customers, and stakeholders
3. Determine who the very best competitors are in your space
4. Assess whether you are aspiring to win or simply participate in the market
5. Ensure your aspiration connects to the deeper purpose of your organization
6. Align your aspiration with consumer needs rather than internal capabilities
7. Make sure your aspiration is ambitious but achievable
8. Test whether your aspiration will inspire action and investment from your team
9. Validate that your aspirations are consistent across different organizational levels
10. Regularly reassess your aspiration as market conditions change

## Coaching Questions
- What does winning actually look like for your organization?
- Who are the stakeholders that matter most to your success?
- Who are the very best competitors you should be measuring yourself against?
- Are you playing to participate or to win in your market?
- How does your winning aspiration connect to the needs of your consumers?
- What investments would you need to make to achieve your winning aspiration?
- How will your employees understand and connect with this aspiration?
- What would happen if you don't set a clear winning aspiration?
- How does your aspiration align with your company's core purpose?
- How will you measure progress toward your winning aspiration?
