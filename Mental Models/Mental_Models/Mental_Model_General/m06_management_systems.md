# Management Systems

## Description
Management Systems are the systems that foster, support, and measure the strategy. These include the processes, systems, and measures that ensure the strategic choices are communicated throughout the organization, employees are trained to deliver on choices and leverage capabilities, plans are made to invest in and sustain capabilities over time, and the efficacy of choices and progress toward aspirations are measured. Management systems must be purposefully designed to support the strategic choices and capabilities, creating reinforcement throughout the organization.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This model should be used with caution when an organization is implementing strategy changes too rapidly without allowing systems to stabilize. It may be problematic if the organization doesn't have strong leadership to drive system changes, or if there's significant resistance to change within the organization. Avoid this approach if the organization can't commit to aligning all systems with the strategy, as misaligned systems will undermine strategic success. Also be cautious if resources are insufficient to properly design and implement supporting management systems.

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify existing management systems that support or hinder your strategy
2. Design new systems to support your strategic choices and capabilities
3. Plan how to communicate strategic choices throughout the organization
4. Develop training programs to help employees deliver on strategic choices
5. Create measurement systems that track progress toward strategic objectives
6. Establish planning processes that invest in capabilities over time
7. Design reward and recognition systems that reinforce strategic choices
8. Plan how management systems will reinforce each other
9. Consider how to adapt management systems as strategy evolves
10. Test whether your management systems support your strategic choices

## Coaching Questions
- What management systems currently support or hinder your strategy?
- How will you communicate your strategic choices throughout the organization?
- What training programs are needed to help employees deliver on your strategy?
- How do you measure progress toward your strategic objectives?
- Are your reward and recognition systems aligned with your strategic choices?
- What planning processes are needed to invest in capabilities over time?
- How do your various management systems reinforce each other?
- What systems might be working at cross-purposes to your strategy?
- How will you adapt your management systems as your strategy evolves?
- What resources are needed to properly implement your management systems?
