# How to Win

## Description
How to Win defines the unique recipe for success within the chosen playing field. It specifies how the company will create unique value and sustainably deliver that value to customers in a way that is distinct from competitors. This choice must be intimately connected to the where-to-play choices, as how you win differs depending on where you choose to compete. The key is to identify activities and approaches that competitors cannot easily replicate and that create sustainable competitive advantage in the chosen arena.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This model should be used with caution when an organization lacks the necessary capabilities to execute their intended winning approach. It may be problematic if the company doesn't have a clear understanding of their competitive position or if they're trying to win in too many different ways across various markets. Avoid using this approach if the organization is not willing to make the investments necessary to develop the capabilities required to win in their chosen way.

## Thinking Steps
1. Define how you will create unique value for customers in your chosen arena
2. Identify what makes your approach different from competitors
3. Assess whether your approach is difficult for competitors to replicate
4. Ensure your how-to-win approach aligns with your where-to-play choices
5. Determine what capabilities you need to develop to execute your approach
6. Evaluate whether your approach is sustainable over the long term
7. Test if your approach creates real value for customers rather than just internal efficiencies
8. Consider how competitors might respond to your approach
9. Validate that your approach is connected to superior financial returns
10. Plan the investments and resources needed to execute your approach

## Coaching Questions
- What unique value do you create for customers that competitors cannot match?
- How does your approach to winning differ from your competitors?
- What makes your winning approach difficult for others to copy?
- How does your how-to-win strategy align with your chosen playing field?
- What capabilities must you build or strengthen to execute your approach?
- How will you sustain your competitive advantage over time?
- What would happen if competitors tried to replicate your approach?
- How does your approach connect to superior financial returns?
- What investments are necessary to execute your winning approach?
- How will you measure if your winning approach is successful?
