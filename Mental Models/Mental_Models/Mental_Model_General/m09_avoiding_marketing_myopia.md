# Avoiding Marketing Myopia

## Description
Avoiding Marketing Myopia is the practice of defining your business by the customer needs you satisfy rather than by the products you make. Marketing Myopia occurs when companies define themselves too narrowly by their current products or technology rather than by the broader benefit or job they provide for customers. This leads to a focus on internal capabilities and technical achievements rather than customer needs, making companies vulnerable to disruption by competitors who better understand the customer's true requirements.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This concept should be used with caution in highly specialized technical markets where the product complexity itself is the primary value proposition. It may be problematic for companies in B2B markets where technical specifications are more important than customer experience. Avoid this approach if your market is dominated by regulatory requirements that define product parameters regardless of customer preference. Also be cautious in markets where the customer has limited choice due to external factors.

## Thinking Steps
1. Articulate how your business is currently defined (by products vs. customer needs)
2. Identify the broader customer needs your products or services address
3. Consider how technology or market changes might affect customer needs
4. Evaluate whether your current definition is too narrow or product-focused
5. Assess what business you're really in from the customer's perspective
6. Identify adjacent opportunities that address the same customer needs
7. Examine how successful competitors define their business
8. Consider what business you might be in in the future
9. Test your business definition against customer feedback
10. Align organizational thinking around the proper business definition

## Coaching Questions
- How does your organization currently define the business it's in?
- What customer needs does your business actually satisfy?
- Are you focused on products/technology or on customer problems?
- What would happen if your current products became obsolete?
- How do customers think about the job your products do for them?
- What other ways could these customer needs be satisfied?
- Are you missing opportunities because of a narrow business definition?
- How do your competitors define their business differently?
- What would change if you redefined your business around customer needs?
- How does your business definition influence your strategic choices?
