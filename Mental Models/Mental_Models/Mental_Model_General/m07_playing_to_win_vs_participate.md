# Playing to Win vs Participate

## Description
Playing to Win vs Participate is a mindset that distinguishes between organizations that set out to truly win in their markets versus those that simply aim to participate or compete. Organizations that play to win define clear winning aspirations, make tough strategic choices, and invest accordingly to achieve sustainable competitive advantage. Those that play to participate often set modest goals, avoid difficult trade-offs, and ultimately achieve mediocre results. The winning mindset drives fundamentally different strategic decisions and resource allocation choices.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This concept should be used with caution in markets that are declining or where the cost of pursuing a winning position is prohibitively high relative to potential returns. It may be problematic if the organization operates in a market where being number one is not significantly more profitable than being number two or three. Avoid this approach if the organization lacks the resources or capabilities to credibly pursue a winning position, or if the competitive environment makes winning infeasible regardless of strategy. Also be cautious in markets where the definition of winning is constantly changing.

## Thinking Steps
1. Assess whether your organization currently plays to win or just to participate
2. Define what winning means in your specific market context
3. Evaluate if you have the necessary resources and capabilities to win
4. Determine what investments are required to achieve a winning position
5. Identify what trade-offs are necessary to focus on winning
6. Assess the competitive landscape and whether winning is feasible
7. Consider whether the rewards of winning justify the required investments
8. Plan the strategic changes needed to shift from participating to winning
9. Design metrics to track progress toward winning rather than just competing
10. Align your organization's culture and systems around winning aspirations

## Coaching Questions
- Is your organization currently playing to win or just to participate?
- What would change in your strategy if you truly set out to win?
- What investments would you need to make to achieve a winning position?
- How does your current strategy reflect a winning vs participating mindset?
- What trade-offs would you need to make to focus on winning?
- Are there markets where you're currently just participating that should be exited?
- How do your resource allocation decisions reflect a winning versus participating mindset?
- What would happen if you committed fully to winning in your chosen markets?
- How would your competitors react to your shift from participating to winning?
- How do you measure and reward progress toward winning rather than just competing?
