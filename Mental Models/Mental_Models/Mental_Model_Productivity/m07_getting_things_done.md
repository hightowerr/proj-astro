# Getting Things Done

## Description
Getting Things Done (GTD) is a productivity methodology developed by David Allen that redefines how you approach your life and work. It's based on five core steps: Capture, Clarify, Organize, Reflect, and Engage. The method involves systematically collecting all tasks, commitments, and ideas that compete for your attention, clarifying what they mean and what actions they require, organizing them effectively, regularly reviewing your system, and engaging with your priorities with confidence. The core principle is that you should externalize all the "stuff" that occupies your mind into an organized system, allowing you to focus on actual work rather than remembering what needs to be done.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This model should be used with caution if you already have an effective personal system that works for you. It may be overwhelming for people who prefer intuitive, unplanned approaches to work. Avoid using it if you're experiencing major life changes where your priorities shift frequently, or when you have limited time to invest in learning and implementing the system. It may also be excessive for very simple, routine tasks that don't require systematic organization. Be cautious if you're already experiencing burnout, as adding another system might increase rather than decrease stress.

## Thinking Steps
1. Capture everything that has your attention - tasks, ideas, commitments, reminders - into a trusted external system
2. Clarify each item by asking what it means and what specific action is needed
3. Organize clarified actions into appropriate categories and contexts
4. Reflect regularly on your system to ensure priorities are aligned with goals
5. Engage with confidence knowing your system captures what matters
6. Determine which actions to take based on priority, context, time available, and energy levels
7. Process your inbox regularly to keep the system current
8. Set up appropriate organizational structures for different types of actions
9. Schedule regular reviews to maintain system effectiveness
10. Adjust your approach based on changing needs and circumstances

## Coaching Questions
- What's currently occupying your mind that you need to capture and process?
- How do you systematically organize your tasks and responsibilities?
- What system do you use to clarify vague ideas into actionable steps?
- How often do you review and adjust your priorities?
- What contexts or locations do you work in, and how do you organize tasks accordingly?
- How do you ensure nothing falls through the cracks?
- What triggers cause you to feel overwhelmed by your responsibilities?
- How do you determine which task to work on next?
- What systems do you have in place to track commitments and follow through?
- How do you separate urgent tasks from important tasks in your system?
