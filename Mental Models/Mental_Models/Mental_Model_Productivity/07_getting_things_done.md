# Getting Things Done (GTD)

## Description
Getting Things Done (GTD) is a productivity methodology developed by David Allen that serves as a personal productivity approach redefining how people approach their life and work. The methodology involves five key steps: Capture, Clarify, Organize, Reflect, and Engage. It requires systematically collecting everything that competes for your attention (tasks, projects, commitments, ideas), clarifying what each means and what actions are needed, organizing them effectively in your chosen system, regularly reflecting on your lists and commitments to ensure alignment with your goals, and engaging with your priorities confidently knowing your system captures what matters. The core principle is to externalize all the "stuff" that occupies your mind into an organized system, allowing you to focus on actual work rather than remembering what needs to be done.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This model should be used with caution if you already have an effective personal system that works for you. Avoid if you're experiencing significant life changes where priorities shift frequently, making systematic organization difficult to maintain. Don't use when you're in a highly unpredictable work environment where your system might require constant, disruptive changes. Avoid if you prefer intuitive, unplanned approaches to work and would find the systematic nature burdensome. Be cautious if you're experiencing burnout, as adding another system might increase rather than decrease stress. Don't implement if you lack the discipline to maintain the system consistently.

## Thinking Steps
1. Capture everything that has your attention - tasks, ideas, commitments, reminders - into a trusted external system
2. Clarify each item by asking what it means and what specific action is needed
3. Organize clarified actions into appropriate categories and contexts
4. Reflect regularly on your system to ensure priorities align with your goals
5. Engage with confidence knowing your system captures what matters
6. Determine which actions to take based on priority, context, time available, and energy levels
7. Process your inbox regularly to keep the system current
8. Set up appropriate organizational structures for different types of actions
9. Schedule regular reviews to maintain system effectiveness
10. Adjust your approach based on changing needs and circumstances

## Coaching Questions
- What's currently occupying your mind that you need to capture and process?
- How do you systematically organize your tasks and responsibilities?
- What system do you use to clarify vague ideas into actionable steps?
- How often do you review and adjust your priorities?
- What contexts or locations do you work in, and how do you organize tasks accordingly?
- How do you ensure nothing falls through the cracks?
- What triggers cause you to feel overwhelmed by your responsibilities?
- How do you determine which task to work on next?
- What systems do you have in place to track commitments and follow-through?
- How do you separate urgent tasks from important tasks in your system?
