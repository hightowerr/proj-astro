# m71_balance.md

## Description
Balance is the arrangement of elements to create visual stability and harmony. It's the use of artistic elements such as line, texture, color, space, and form in the creation of artworks in a way that renders visual stability. Balance can be conceptualized as harmony. It involves using the space in a way that renders the entire piece easily observable. There is no prescription or formula for what makes a work balanced and therefore harmonious, but artists often experiment to achieve the "just so" effect. Balance requires considering left to right, up to down, and foreground to background relationships.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
- When you want to deliberately create tension or unease
- In abstract art where imbalance serves artistic purpose
- When the subject matter requires asymmetry for effect
- In modern art that challenges traditional harmony
- When the goal is to disturb or challenge the viewer
- When asymmetrical compositions communicate the intended message better

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify the key elements that need to be balanced in your composition
2. Assess visual weights of different elements (size, color, contrast, texture)
3. Consider the relationship between different parts (left/right, up/down, foreground/background)
4. Evaluate how the viewer's eye moves through the composition
5. Test whether the arrangement creates stability and harmony
6. Consider how cultural preferences for symmetry affect the perception
7. Adjust elements to achieve proper visual equilibrium
8. Verify that the balance supports the intended message or function

## Coaching Questions
- What elements are competing for attention in this composition?
- How do the visual weights of different elements compare?
- Where does the viewer's eye naturally travel through this design?
- Are there areas that feel too heavy or too light visually?
- How does this balance support the overall purpose of the work?
- What would happen to the composition if I shifted elements around?
- How do cultural expectations about symmetry affect this design?
- Is the current balance creating harmony or tension, and is that intentional?
- What elements could be adjusted to improve the visual equilibrium?
