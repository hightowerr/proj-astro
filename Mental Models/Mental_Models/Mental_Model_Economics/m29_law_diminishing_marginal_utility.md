# Law of Diminishing Marginal Utility

**Description:**
The Law of Diminishing Marginal Utility states that as a person increases consumption of a product while keeping consumption of other products constant, there is a decline in the marginal utility that person derives from consuming each additional unit of that product. In simple terms, the more you have of something, the less satisfaction you get from each additional unit. This principle explains why people diversify consumption and why the value of money changes based on how much someone already has.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When expecting the same satisfaction from additional units of the same thing
- When making economic decisions without considering diminishing returns
- When assuming that more of something is always better
- When not accounting for the declining satisfaction from additional consumption
- When making consumption decisions based on initial satisfaction levels

**Keywords for Situations:**
Consumer behavior, investment decisions, resource allocation, economic policy, satisfaction, returns, diversification, consumption, utility, preferences.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. **Consider Current Satisfaction:** Evaluate your current level of satisfaction with a product or activity.
2. **Anticipate Diminishing Returns:** Recognize that additional units will provide less satisfaction.
3. **Diversify Consumption:** Consider spreading across different products or activities for continued satisfaction.
4. **Optimize Allocation:** Distribute resources across different uses to maximize total satisfaction.
5. **Recognize Satiation:** Understand when you've reached optimal consumption of a single item.
6. **Plan for Changes:** Consider how your satisfaction may change over time.

**Coaching Questions:**
* "Am I expecting the same satisfaction from additional units?"
* "How might diversifying across different activities affect my satisfaction?"
* "At what point will additional consumption provide diminishing returns?"
* "How can I optimize my resource allocation considering this principle?"
* "Am I focusing too much on one source of satisfaction instead of diversifying?"
* "How does my current satisfaction level affect my decision-making?"
