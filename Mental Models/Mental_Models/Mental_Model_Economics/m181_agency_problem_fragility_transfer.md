### Description

The agency problem is the risk created when the decision maker (agent) is not the true owner or bearer of consequences (principal). The agent can pursue strategies that look good cosmetically—often with hidden tail risks—because the upside accrues to them while the downside is transferred to others. This is a major engine of systemic fragility.

---

### When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)

- When you overcorrect by eliminating delegation entirely; instead, improve alignment and visibility.
- When you assume the principal can fully monitor the agent; hidden risk often lives in foundations.

---

### Keywords for Situations

Incentives, governance, hidden tail risk, moral hazard, accountability, opacity, asymmetry, systemic fragility.

---

### Thinking Steps

1. **Identify principals and agents**
   Who decides, and who bears the long-run consequences?
2. **Map the payoff asymmetry**
   Where does the agent get rewarded, and where can losses be offloaded?
3. **Locate hidden-risk surfaces**
   Find places where risk can be concealed: complexity, leverage, long delays, accounting games.
4. **Align incentives**
   Add skin in the game: deferred compensation, clawbacks, shared downside, and long-horizon metrics.
5. **Constrain ruin**
   Put hard limits on exposures that can create catastrophic loss (position limits, redundancy, fail-safes).
6. **Improve visibility**
   Use independent audits and simple, antifragile heuristics over fragile quantitative narratives.

---

### Coaching Questions

- If this fails badly, who pays?
- What risks can the agent hide from the outside?
- What would change if the agent had to bear the downside personally?
- Which constraints prevent tail-risk accumulation even if monitoring fails?
