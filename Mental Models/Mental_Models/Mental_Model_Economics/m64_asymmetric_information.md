# m64_asymmetric_information.md

## Description
Asymmetric information occurs when one party in a transaction or interaction has more or better information than the other party. This imbalance in knowledge can lead to market failures and inefficiencies, as the party with less information may make suboptimal decisions. The party with superior information may exploit this advantage, leading to adverse selection (where bad risks are more likely to seek insurance or credit) or moral hazard (where one party changes their behavior after a transaction because the other party cannot observe this behavior). This model helps explain many market phenomena and institutional arrangements.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
- When the goal is to create perfectly transparent market conditions
- In situations where information sharing is legally required
- When the information gap is too large to be ethically bridged
- When the cost of obtaining better information is too high
- In regulated industries with strict disclosure requirements
- When information asymmetry involves illegal or unethical activities
- When both parties need equal access to information for safety reasons

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify the information held by each party in the interaction
2. Assess the degree and type of information asymmetry present
3. Determine how the information gap affects decision-making
4. Evaluate potential adverse selection problems before the transaction
5. Consider moral hazard issues after the transaction occurs
6. Analyze how the information gap creates potential for exploitation
7. Consider mechanisms to reduce the information gap or protect the disadvantaged party
8. Assess the costs and benefits of reducing information asymmetry

## Coaching Questions
- What information does each party have or lack in this situation?
- How does this information gap affect the decision-making process?
- Is the party with more information using it for advantage?
- Could adverse selection be affecting this market or relationship?
- Are there moral hazard issues that emerge after the agreement?
- How could information asymmetry be reduced ethically?
- What mechanisms exist to protect the disadvantaged party?
- What are the costs of obtaining better information?
- How does information asymmetry affect the efficiency of this market?
