# Externalities

**Description:**
Externalities are costs or benefits that affect a party who did not choose to be involved in the transaction or activity that caused them. They represent the spillover effects of economic activities on third parties who were not part of the original decision-making process. Positive externalities benefit third parties, while negative externalities impose costs on them without compensation. Externalities occur when the full costs or benefits of activities are not reflected in market prices.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When making decisions without considering their impact on others not directly involved
- When market prices don't reflect the true social costs or benefits of activities
- When ignoring the broader consequences of individual or business actions
- When not accounting for spillover effects in investment or business decisions
- When private costs and benefits differ significantly from social costs and benefits

**Keywords for Situations:**
Market failures, environmental policy, public goods, pollution, congestion, economic policy, corporate responsibility, investment decisions, business strategy, social costs.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. **Identify Third Parties:** Consider who else might be affected by an activity besides the direct participants.
2. **Assess Spillover Effects:** Evaluate the positive and negative impacts on those not directly involved.
3. **Quantify Externalities:** Attempt to measure the cost or benefit imposed on third parties.
4. **Internalize Costs:** Support mechanisms that make decision-makers account for external costs and benefits.
5. **Consider Regulation:** Evaluate the need for policies to address harmful externalities.
6. **Account for Full Impact:** Include external effects when evaluating the true cost of activities.

**Coaching Questions:**
* "Who else might be affected by this activity beyond the direct participants?"
* "Are there systems in place to prevent overuse of this shared resource?"
* "Would I continue this behavior if everyone else did the same?"
* "What are the long-term consequences of current usage patterns?"
* "How can I help create or support systems that promote sustainable use?"
* "Am I considering the collective impact of individual actions?"
