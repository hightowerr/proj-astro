## Mental Model = Energy Return on Investment (EROI)

**Category = Economics / Science**
**Description:**
EROI is the ratio of energy delivered by an energy-gathering process to the energy required to build, operate, and maintain that process over its life cycle. It is a viability lens: a low-EROI energy source may be feasible but can constrain societal surplus and the ability to sustain complex functions at scale.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When system boundaries are unclear (EROI can be gamed by excluding inputs).
- When the decision is local and price/availability dominates near-term choices.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Energy transitions; life-cycle analysis; net energy; viability; unconventional fossil fuels.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Define the boundary and time horizon for energy inputs (construction, maintenance, storage, integration).
2. Compute/estimate gross energy output and total invested energy.
3. Compare EROI across alternatives under consistent assumptions.
4. Translate EROI into implications for net societal surplus and scaling limits.

**Coaching Questions:**
- What inputs are we excluding that would change EROI materially?
- Does this source remain viable when scaled (integration, storage, infrastructure)?
- How much net energy surplus does society need for critical functions?
