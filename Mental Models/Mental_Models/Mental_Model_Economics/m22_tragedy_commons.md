# Tragedy of Commons

**Description:**
The Tragedy of Commons is a situation in which individuals, acting in their own self-interest, deplete or spoil a shared resource even though it's in everyone's long-term interest to preserve it. Each individual benefits from using the common resource while the costs of its degradation are shared by all users. This creates a dynamic where rational individuals make choices that collectively lead to an outcome that is worse for everyone than if they had cooperated.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When using shared resources without considering the collective impact
- When individual incentives conflict with collective long-term benefits
- When there are no governance systems to manage shared resources
- When short-term personal gains outweigh long-term collective benefits
- When failing to recognize that individual actions aggregate to collective outcomes

**Keywords for Situations:**
Environmental resources, public spaces, shared infrastructure, organizational resources, sustainability, collective action, environmental policy, economics, game theory, cooperation.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. **Identify Common Resources:** Recognize resources that are shared and subject to overuse.
2. **Consider Collective Impact:** Think about how individual usage affects the resource for everyone.
3. **Look for Governance:** Determine if there are systems in place to manage the resource sustainably.
4. **Align Individual and Collective Interests:** Support systems that make sustainable use rational for individuals.
5. **Advocate for Rules:** Support regulations or norms that prevent overuse of shared resources.
6. **Personal Responsibility:** Reduce personal usage when possible and encourage others to do the same.

**Coaching Questions:**
* "What shared resources am I using and how does my use affect others?"
* "Are there systems in place to prevent overuse of this shared resource?"
* "Would I continue this behavior if everyone else did the same?"
* "What are the long-term consequences of current usage patterns?"
* "How can I help create or support systems that promote sustainable use?"
* "Am I considering the collective impact of individual actions?"
