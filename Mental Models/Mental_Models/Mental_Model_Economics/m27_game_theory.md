# Game Theory

**Description:**
Game Theory is the study of mathematical models of strategic interaction among rational decision-makers. It analyzes situations where the outcome for each participant depends not only on their own actions but also on the actions of others. This framework helps understand conflict and cooperation between intelligent, rational decision-makers and explains phenomena like the Prisoner's Dilemma, coordination problems, and strategic decision-making in competitive situations.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When assuming all participants will behave rationally
- When not considering how others' strategies will affect your outcomes
- When ignoring the strategic nature of interactions
- When making decisions without anticipating others' responses
- When assuming zero-sum thinking in potentially positive-sum situations

**Keywords for Situations:**
Negotiations, competitive strategy, auctions, market competition, political strategy, conflict resolution, decision making, strategic planning, competitive dynamics.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. **Identify Players:** Determine who the key decision-makers are in the situation.
2. **Understand Incentives:** Analyze what motivates each player.
3. **Predict Responses:** Anticipate how others might respond to your actions.
4. **Consider Equilibria:** Look for stable outcomes where no one wants to change their strategy unilaterally.
5. **Look for Cooperative Solutions:** Consider win-win scenarios where cooperation benefits all.
6. **Plan for Multiple Rounds:** Consider how current actions affect future interactions.

**Coaching Questions:**
* "Who else has decisions to make that will affect my outcomes?"
* "What are the incentives and motivations of other players?"
* "How might others respond to my potential actions?"
* "Are there cooperative opportunities I haven't considered?"
* "What outcomes would be stable where no one wants to change strategy?"
* "How do current decisions affect the game's future iterations?"
