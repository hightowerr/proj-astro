# Business Model Innovation

## Description
Business model innovation refers to the process of changing how a firm creates value (and for whom) and how it captures value (and from whom). A business model specifies how the firm operates - how it creates value and to whom; how it captures value and from whom. This innovation has become a profoundly disruptive force in the digital economy, occurring much more quickly than in previous decades and separating winners from losers in many industries. Business model innovation often proves more powerful than technological innovation in creating market disruption. The book emphasizes that while many assume technology drives disruption, it's actually business model innovation that often provides the competitive advantage.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
Business model innovation should be approached with caution when the market is highly regulated and changes are restricted by law. It should also be avoided when the company lacks the operational capabilities to execute the new model effectively. Be cautious when the customer base is not ready for the proposed changes or when the innovation would alienate existing profitable customer segments. Additionally, avoid business model innovations that depend on technologies or partners that are not yet mature or reliable.

## Thinking Steps
1. Analyze your current business model by identifying exactly how you create value and for whom
2. Examine how you currently capture value and from whom
3. Research successful business model innovations in your industry and adjacent industries
4. Identify customer pain points in your current model that could be addressed with a different model
5. Consider how technology could enable a different way of creating or capturing value
6. Evaluate the feasibility of implementing the new business model given your capabilities and resources
7. Assess how competitors might respond to your business model innovation
8. Design experiments to test the viability of the new business model with select customer segments
9. Plan the transition from the old model to the new, considering impact on existing customers and stakeholders
10. Establish metrics to track the success of the new business model and adapt as needed

## Coaching Questions
- How does your current business model create value for customers and capture value for your company?
- What customer needs are not being met by current business models in your industry?
- How do customers in your market pay today, and how might they prefer to pay in the future?
- What constraints in your current business model prevent you from serving certain customer segments?
- How might technology enable a completely different way of delivering value to customers?
- What business model innovations in other industries could be adapted to your market?
- If you had to change your business model, which customer segment would be most receptive?
- What resources and capabilities would you need to execute a different business model?
- How would your competitors respond if you implemented a new business model?
- What would happen if you eliminated your current revenue stream and created a new one?
