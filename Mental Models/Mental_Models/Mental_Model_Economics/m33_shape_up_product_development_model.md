## Description: Core concept explanation
Shape Up is a product development model that ensures teams deliver meaningful outcomes within fixed timeboxes by doing the real thinking **before** engineering begins. It replaces backlogs, estimate-driven planning, and mid-project resets with a disciplined sequence:

- **Shaping:** Define the problem, set an appetite (time budget), outline a viable solution, and remove major risks.
- **Betting:** Leadership commits to shaped projects using a fixed-time, variable-scope mindset.
- **Building:** Small, autonomous teams deliver vertical slices end-to-end within a six-week cycle.
- **Cool-Down:** A two-week period for maintenance, reflection, and preparing the next set of pitches.

The model works by reducing uncertainty upfront, enforcing clear boundaries, and giving teams full ownership during execution. Fixed time replaces fixed scope, and finished work means “in production,” not “code complete.”

---

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
- **High dependency environments:** Cross-team coordination needs break Shape Up’s autonomy assumptions.  
- **Weak technical or design depth in shaping:** Risky pitches reach teams, causing mid-cycle surprises.  
- **Ambiguous ownership:** No clear shaper or accountable leader.  
- **Continuous-delivery micro-release cultures:** Cycle-based batching conflicts with daily shipping.  
- **R&D or unknown-feasibility work:** Shaping can’t meaningfully bound true research.  
- **Reactive, support-heavy teams:** Interruptions undermine cycle integrity.  
- **Very small teams (<5 people):** The overhead isn’t worth it at early-stage scale.

## Thinking Steps (Deeper tool here - [[m34_shape_up_step_by_step_playbook]])
### 1. Frame
- Validate the problem: whose pain, which workflow, and why now.
- Bound it: avoid grab-bags or vague “redesigns.”

### 2. Shape
- Set the appetite (1–2 weeks or 6 weeks).
- Rough out the solution via breadboarding or fat-marker sketches.
- Identify unknowns and rabbit holes.
- Define no-gos and produce a clear pitch.

### 3. Bet
- Review shaped pitches in cool-down.
- Select a small number of commitments based on strategy, impact, and feasibility.
- Assign teams and guarantee uninterrupted cycle time.

### 4. Build
- Start with one small vertical slice to validate architecture early.
- Work asynchronously; minimise meetings.
- Track progress with hill charts.
- Hammer scope to fit the appetite.

### 5. Finish & Ship
- Create a punch list late cycle.
- Tighten, polish, and deliver into production.
- Hold cool-down for maintenance and new shaping.

## Coaching Questions (Deeper tool here - [[m34_shape_up_step_by_step_playbook]])
### Framing
- What problem are we solving, and for whom?
- What is the smallest meaningful context for this problem?
- Why does this matter now?
### Shaping
- How much time is this worth?  
- What must fit inside the appetite?  
- What risks or unknowns could explode mid-cycle?  
- What’s explicitly out of scope?
### Building
- What’s the smallest vertical slice we can demo?  
- Where are we stuck on the hill?  
- What scope can we cut or defer?  
### Finishing
- What must be true to call this done?  
- Does the outcome solve the framed problem?  
- What learning should feed into future shaping?
