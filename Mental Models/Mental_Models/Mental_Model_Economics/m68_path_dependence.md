# m68_path_dependence.md

## Description
Path dependence is a process where past decisions significantly influence future options and outcomes, making some outcomes inevitable once a particular course has been taken. It describes situations where the sequence of events matters, and small decisions made at early stages can have large consequences later. Once a particular path is established, it becomes increasingly difficult and costly to change course due to accumulated investments, infrastructure, or institutional knowledge. The concept explains why suboptimal systems sometimes persist despite the availability of better alternatives.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
- When you need to make a fresh start without regard to prior commitments
- In situations where the current path is clearly suboptimal or harmful
- When the cost of changing path is less than continuing the current trajectory
- When the initial conditions that created the path have fundamentally changed
- When innovation and new approaches are more important than efficiency
- When the path was established under different circumstances
- When the path dependence creates monopolistic or anti-competitive conditions

## Keywords for Situations
- Technological standards (QWERTY keyboards, Windows OS)
- Infrastructure development (rail gauge, electrical sockets)
- Organizational structures and processes
- Educational curriculum
- Legal systems and precedents
- Urban planning and development
- Investment and financial decisions
- Career trajectories
- Political institutions
- Cultural traditions and practices
- Manufacturing processes
- Market positions and customer habits

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify the key early decisions that established the current path
2. Assess how much has been invested in maintaining the current path
3. Determine whether early conditions still apply to current circumstances
4. Evaluate the costs and benefits of changing versus maintaining the path
5. Analyze the feedback loops that reinforce the current path
6. Consider whether the current path is optimal for current needs
7. Identify potential alternative paths and their implementation costs
8. Assess the feasibility and timing of changing path if needed
9. Plan for building in flexibility to avoid lock-in to suboptimal paths

## Coaching Questions
- How did we get to this point and what early decisions established the current path?
- What investments have been made that make changing difficult?
- Have the conditions that led to this path changed significantly?
- What would be the cost of switching to a different path?
- Are we maintaining this path simply because we've always done it this way?
- What alternative paths might be available and what would it take to switch?
- How might we build in flexibility to avoid getting locked into suboptimal paths in the future?
- What are the consequences of continuing on this path?
- How can we recognize when path dependence is limiting our options?
