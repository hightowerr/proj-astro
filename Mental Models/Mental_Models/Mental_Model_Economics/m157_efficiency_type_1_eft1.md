## Mental Model = Efficiency of Type 1 (EFT1)

**Category = Economics / Science**
**Description:**
Efficiency of Type 1 (EFT1) is the ratio of output to input. It captures “how much output per unit input” (e.g., miles per gallon). It ignores the time required to produce the output and is closely tied to resource/energy throughput and environmental loading.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When time/throughput is the critical constraint (EFT2 may dominate).
- When system-level adaptation means higher EFT1 can still increase total consumption (rebound/backfire).

**Keywords for Situations:**
Fuel economy; energy efficiency; throughput; entropy; resource depletion.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Define output and input units clearly (avoid category errors).
2. Check whether the decision is about throughput reduction (EFT1 relevant).
3. Identify trade-offs with speed/throughput (EFT2).
4. Evaluate system-level effects: does higher EFT1 enable more total activity?

**Coaching Questions:**
- What is the output, what is the input, and are they measured consistently?
- If EFT1 rises, what expands in scale?
- What do we lose in time/throughput performance if we maximize EFT1?
