# Gresham's Law

**Description:**
Gresham's Law states that "bad money drives out good" - when two forms of money are in circulation but one is intrinsically more valuable, the valuable money will be hoarded while the less valuable money remains in circulation. More broadly, this principle applies to situations where inferior quality items or practices crowd out superior ones when both are treated as equivalent. This occurs when people cannot easily distinguish between quality levels or when regulations force equal treatment of unequal items.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When expecting high-quality items or behaviors to naturally prevail without proper incentives
- When creating systems that treat unequal things as equivalent
- When not monitoring for quality degradation over time
- When failing to distinguish between appearance and reality
- When not considering how incentives may promote inferior alternatives

**Keywords for Situations:**
Market failures, quality standards, currency systems, business practices, information quality, incentive systems, regulation, competition, selection mechanisms.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. **Identify Quality Differences:** Recognize when different quality options are competing.
2. **Check for Artificial Parity:** Look for regulations or systems that treat unequal things as equal.
3. **Assess Information Flow:** Determine how well consumers can distinguish quality differences.
4. **Evaluate Incentives:** Understand whether systems reward inferior or superior options.
5. **Consider Monitoring:** Ensure quality standards are maintained and enforced.
6. **Support Good Practices:** Create incentives that favor superior options.

**Coaching Questions:**
* "Are inferior options being artificially supported or subsidized?"
* "Can consumers distinguish between good and poor quality options?"
* "What incentives reward inferior practices over superior ones?"
* "How can I ensure quality standards are maintained?"
* "Am I being influenced by systems that treat unequal things as equivalent?"
* "What systems would better reward quality options?"
