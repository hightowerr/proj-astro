## Mental Model = Efficiency of Type 2 (EFT2)

**Category = Economics / Science**
**Description:**
Efficiency of Type 2 (EFT2) is the pace of generating output per unit time (e.g., miles per hour). It captures “time efficiency” or throughput performance, while ignoring how much input is consumed to achieve that pace. In many real systems, increasing EFT2 reduces EFT1 (higher speed consumes more fuel per mile).

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When environmental/resource constraints are binding (EFT1 becomes critical).
- When higher EFT2 drives scale expansion that worsens total throughput and impacts.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Speed; power; throughput; growth; time-to-market; productivity race.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Define the output per unit time being optimized.
2. Make input costs explicit (energy/materials) to reveal EFT1 trade-offs.
3. Identify whether competition rewards speed (why EFT2 pressure exists).
4. Stress-test system impacts: does higher EFT2 increase total throughput and wastes?

**Coaching Questions:**
- What inputs are being ignored while we optimize speed?
- Where is the EFT1/EFT2 trade-off curve in this system?
- If we get faster, what new behaviors or scale increases follow?
