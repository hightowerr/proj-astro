  - Builder Liability Principle

  - The builder/operator has more knowledge about hidden risks than inspectors

### Description

Hammurabi risk management is the idea that the best way to manage hidden risk is to align incentives with the party who can conceal it. When a builder knows far more than an inspector about what lies in the foundations, inspection cannot substitute for symmetry of consequences. The remedy is to ensure meaningful liability and downside for those who build and decide.

---

### When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)

- When literal liability is infeasible; use structural substitutes (insurance structures, bonding, strict constraints).
- When the rule is used for revenge rather than ex-ante risk reduction.

---

### Keywords for Situations

Hidden risk, delayed collapse, accountability, incentive alignment, tail risk, moral hazard, foundations.

---

### Thinking Steps

1. **Find the foundations**
   Identify where risks can be hidden and revealed only after long delays.
2. **Locate the knowledge advantage**
   Who knows more about those hidden risks than outsiders?
3. **Attach downside to the knower**
   Create liability, bonding, or exposure mechanisms that make hiding risk irrational.
4. **Design for tail events**
   Ensure incentives address rare but catastrophic outcomes, not just frequent small ones.
5. **Simplify enforcement**
   Prefer clear rules that still work when monitoring fails or metrics are gamed.

---

### Coaching Questions

- Where can the real risk be hidden from auditors?
- Who has the strongest informational advantage over the foundations?
- What penalty or exposure would prevent risky shortcuts up front?
- Are we optimizing for frequent small issues while ignoring rare catastrophic ones?
