## Mental Model = Peak Oil

**Category = Economics / Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
Peak oil is the point where the pace of discovering new reserves falls below the pace of depleting existing reserves, leading to a peak (and eventual decline) in production rates. The key issue is not “running out tomorrow” but the tightening of supply rates, rising marginal costs, and cascading system impacts via prices and substitution.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When the relevant planning horizon is short and supply constraints are not binding.
- When you treat peak as a single date rather than a regime shift with uncertainty.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Depletion; energy supply risk; price signals; transition planning; constraints; substitution.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Separate stocks (reserves) from flows (production rate).
2. Compare discovery rates to depletion rates over time.
3. Identify how marginal supply quality/cost changes post-peak.
4. Model system impacts: prices, substitution, demand response, and policy constraints.
5. Plan for resilience under supply volatility, not a single-point forecast.

**Coaching Questions:**
- Are we planning for stock size or flow rate?
- What happens to the economy if marginal supply gets more expensive?
- Which substitutions are feasible, and which are limited by EROI or infrastructure?
