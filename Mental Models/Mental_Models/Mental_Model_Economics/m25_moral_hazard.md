# Moral Hazard

**Description:**
Moral Hazard occurs when one party takes more risks because someone else bears the cost of those risks. This happens when there's a separation between the party taking the risk and the party responsible for the consequences. The risk-taker is insulated from the negative outcomes of their actions, leading to behavior that might not occur if they faced the full costs. This concept is prevalent in insurance, finance, and situations where people have asymmetric information or are shielded from outcomes.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When designing systems without proper accountability or incentive alignment
- When people are shielded from consequences of their actions
- When compensation systems reward risky behavior
- When monitoring and oversight are inadequate
- When decision makers don't bear costs of poor outcomes they cause

**Keywords for Situations:**
Insurance, finance, corporate governance, agency problems, compensation systems, risk management, incentive design, accountability, oversight, principal-agent problems.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. **Identify Risk Separation:** Recognize when risk-takers are separate from cost-bearers.
2. **Assess Incentive Alignment:** Evaluate whether people face consequences of their actions.
3. **Design Accountability:** Create systems where decision-makers face costs of their choices.
4. **Implement Monitoring:** Create oversight to detect risky behavior when costs are separated.
5. **Align Interests:** Structure incentives so individual and collective interests align.
6. **Include Skin in the Game:** Ensure key decision-makers have personal exposure to outcomes.

**Coaching Questions:**
* "Is the decision maker insulated from negative consequences?"
* "How can I better align incentives to encourage appropriate risk-taking?"
* "Who bears the cost when this decision goes wrong?"
* "Are there monitoring systems to detect inappropriate risk-taking?"
* "How can I ensure accountability when risks and rewards are separated?"
* "What would change if the decision maker faced full consequences?"
