# m62_externalities.md

## Description
Externalities occur when someone either incurs a cost or receives a benefit without being compensated for or paying for it. Externalities are one form of market failure where costs spill over from one party to another. They can be positive (benefits spillovers) or negative (cost spillovers). Externalities are inefficient because scarce resources are not being used in a way that confers the most utility. To prevent negative externalities or encourage positive ones, additional costs or incentives are often needed for the responsible party.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
- When you're focusing on direct costs and benefits only, ignoring spillover effects
- In situations where externalities are already well-regulated or priced in
- When analyzing short-term impacts without considering long-term consequences
- In markets where externalities have already been internalized
- When the cost of addressing externalities exceeds the benefits gained

## Keywords for Situations
- Pollution and environmental damage
- Public education benefits
- Vaccination and herd immunity
- Traffic congestion effects
- Noise pollution from neighbors
- Technology network effects
- Infrastructure spillovers
- R&D knowledge spillovers
- Property value impacts
- Climate change effects
- Social media platform effects
- Financial system contagion

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify all parties affected by a decision, not just the direct participants
2. Assess both positive and negative spillover effects on third parties
3. Determine whether those affected are compensated for positive externalities
4. Evaluate whether those causing negative externalities are paying for the costs
5. Calculate the total social costs and benefits beyond private costs and benefits
6. Consider mechanisms to internalize the externalities (taxes, subsidies, regulations)
7. Evaluate the efficiency of different approaches to addressing externalities
8. Assess long-term consequences of unaddressed externalities

## Coaching Questions
- Who else is affected by this decision besides the direct participants?
- Are there benefits that others receive without paying for them?
- Are there costs that others incur without being compensated?
- How could we make sure those who benefit pay for them?
- How could we make sure those who cause costs bear them?
- What's the total social cost versus the private cost of this activity?
- Are there ways to turn negative externalities into positive ones?
- What mechanisms exist or could be created to address these spillovers?
- How do externalities affect the true profitability of this venture?
