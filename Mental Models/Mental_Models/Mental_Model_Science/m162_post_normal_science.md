## Mental Model = Post-Normal Science (PNS)

**Category = Science / Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
Post-normal science is an epistemological framework for problems where facts are uncertain, values are in dispute, stakes are high, and decisions are urgent (e.g., sustainability). It treats uncertainty and stakeholder narratives as central, requires relevance checks on both model and narrative, and shifts modeling from “predict and optimize” to “test narratives and explore scenarios.”

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When the problem is low-stakes, well-bounded, and reducible to standard methods.
- When “PNS” becomes an excuse for abandoning rigor instead of reframing how rigor is applied.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Sustainability; governance; uncertainty; stakeholder narratives; value conflicts; scenario analysis.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Identify uncertainties, stakes, and value conflicts explicitly.
2. Surface the competing narratives and who uses them (and why).
3. Check model relevance before model correctness (narrative quality first).
4. Use models to stress-test scenarios and boundaries, not to claim optimal futures.
5. Communicate results as conditional and decision-relevant, not “true”.

**Coaching Questions:**
- What uncertainties matter most for the decision?
- Which narratives are in conflict, and what values drive them?
- Are we using models to forecast, or to test the coherence of a story?
