## Mental Model = Maximum Energy Flux Principle

**Category = Science / Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
The maximum energy flux principle is the narrative that evolving systems tend toward configurations that maximize energy throughput/flux (within constraints). In the book’s framing, it aligns with EFT2: faster throughput can enable more complex behaviors and expansion of the domain of activities, but often increases resource consumption and environmental loading.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When constraints are changing (what was “maximizing” can become brittle).
- When you treat flux maximization as a policy prescription rather than a descriptive tendency.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Throughput; power; growth; complexity; EFT2; evolution; thermodynamics.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Define what “flux” means in the system (energy/time, throughput, power).
2. Identify constraints and boundary conditions.
3. Map how higher flux trades off with EFT1 (input per output) and stability.
4. Evaluate long-run viability: does flux maximization deplete constraints faster?

**Coaching Questions:**
- What are we maximizing: energy throughput, output throughput, or power?
- Which constraints will bind first if flux increases?
- What stability/adaptability is lost as throughput rises?
