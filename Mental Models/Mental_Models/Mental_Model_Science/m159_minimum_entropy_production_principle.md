## Mental Model = Minimum Entropy Production Principle

**Category = Science / Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
The minimum entropy production principle describes a tendency (under specific assumptions) for certain systems near a steady non-equilibrium state to minimize entropy production compatible with constraints. In the book’s framing, it aligns with EFT1: reducing input throughput per unit of maintained function can stabilize boundary conditions and reduce environmental loading.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When the system is not stable enough to approach a steady state.
- When you’re using it as a “driver of evolution” without acknowledging competing principles and scale issues.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Thermodynamics; dissipation; steady state; stability; EFT1; environmental loading.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Check whether a steady non-equilibrium state approximation is appropriate.
2. Identify constraints applied to the system and what “compatible” means.
3. Map “efficiency” to entropy/dissipation proxies carefully.
4. Use it as one narrative among others, not the only explanation.

**Coaching Questions:**
- Are we close enough to steady-state for this principle to apply?
- What constraints define the feasible minimum?
- What changes when the system evolves or scales up?
