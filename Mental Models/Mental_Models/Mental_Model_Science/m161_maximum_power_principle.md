## Mental Model = Maximum Power Principle

**Category = Science / Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
The maximum power principle is the idea that systems can evolve toward configurations that maximize usable power output (energy per unit time) under constraints. It helps explain why systems may accept lower ratio efficiency (EFT1) to achieve higher throughput/power (EFT2), especially under competitive pressure.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When “power maximization” is not the system’s selection pressure.
- When environmental sinks/sources impose hard limits that make power-seeking self-destructive.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Power; throughput; competition; evolution; efficiency trade-offs; constraints.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Define power in the system (useful work per unit time).
2. Identify selection pressures that reward power/throughput.
3. Make the EFT1 trade-off explicit (inputs per unit output may rise).
4. Check long-run viability under resource and sink constraints.

**Coaching Questions:**
- What selects for “more power” here: competition, survival, growth incentives?
- What efficiency is being sacrificed to get more power?
- Which constraints cap power seeking over the long run?
