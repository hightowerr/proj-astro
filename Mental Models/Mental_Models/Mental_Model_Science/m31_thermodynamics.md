# Thermodynamics

**Description:**
Thermodynamics is the study of energy, heat, work, entropy, and the behavior of matter. The laws describe fundamental principles: energy cannot be created or destroyed (conservation), entropy tends to increase (disorder increases), and absolute zero cannot be reached. In practical terms, thermodynamics explains that energy conversions are never 100% efficient and that systems tend toward equilibrium and increased disorder over time.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When expecting perfect efficiency in systems or processes
- When ignoring costs of energy conversion and transformation
- When designing systems without accounting for entropy and degradation
- When not considering the fundamental limits imposed by thermodynamic laws
- When assuming all energy inputs can be fully converted to useful work

**Keywords for Situations:**
Energy systems, efficiency, entropy, waste, resource utilization, systems design, engineering, economics, sustainability, environmental impact.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. **Apply Conservation Principles:** Recognize that energy/matter cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.
2. **Account for Losses:** Understand that all conversions involve losses and inefficiencies.
3. **Consider Entropy:** Plan for the tendency toward disorder and the need for maintenance.
4. **Optimize Efficiency:** Seek ways to minimize energy losses while understanding they can't be eliminated.
5. **Plan for Degradation:** Account for system decay over time due to entropy.
6. **Recognize Limits:** Accept that perfect efficiency is impossible and plan accordingly.

**Coaching Questions:**
* "What are the inevitable losses in this system or process?"
* "How can I minimize energy waste while understanding perfect efficiency is impossible?"
* "What happens to this system over time as entropy increases?"
* "Am I accounting for the full lifecycle costs including degradation?"
* "How do thermodynamic principles affect the sustainability of this approach?"
* "What are the fundamental limits constraining this system?"
