## Mental Model = Autopoiesis

**Category = Science / Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
Autopoiesis describes systems that reproduce and maintain themselves: their organization is sustained by processes that regenerate the components that make those processes possible. This implies circular causality and multi-scale dependencies: the “parts” and the “whole” co-produce each other over time.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When the system is engineered/closed and does not self-produce.
- When a linear approximation is sufficient for a short time horizon.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Living systems; self-organization; institutions; sustainability; circular causality.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Identify what constitutes the system’s boundary and identity.
2. Map the processes that maintain/reproduce that identity.
3. Trace circular dependencies (“this requires that, which requires this”).
4. Identify throughput requirements (energy/materials/information).
5. Test interventions for destabilizing self-maintenance loops.

**Coaching Questions:**
- What does this system do to keep being itself?
- Which circular dependencies must remain intact?
- What resource flows are required for self-maintenance?
