## Mental Model = Box’s Maxim

**Category = Science / Problem Solving**
**Description:**
Box’s maxim—“all models are wrong, but some are useful”—reframes modeling as decision support, not truth production. A model can be formally correct in its own terms yet useless if the narrative/boundary is irrelevant. Use the maxim to demand explicit purpose, scope, and relevance before trusting outputs.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When it’s used to excuse sloppy modeling or evade accountability (“everything is wrong anyway”).

**Keywords for Situations:**
Forecasting; indicators; policy models; scenario planning; model risk.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Define the decision the model is meant to support.
2. Check relevance: is the narrative/boundary meaningful for the decision?
3. Check assumptions and domain of validity.
4. Compare multiple models/narratives when stakes are high.
5. Communicate uncertainty and conditionality explicitly.

**Coaching Questions:**
- Useful for what decision, and under which boundary conditions?
- Which assumptions are doing the most work?
- What alternative narratives would change the conclusion?
