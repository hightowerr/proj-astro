## Mental Model = Impredicativity

**Category = Math / Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
Impredicativity occurs when a system cannot be described without self-reference—causal explanation becomes circular and scale-dependent. In adaptive systems, the “cause” can depend on the chosen time horizon (e.g., predators affect prey at one horizon; prey affects predators at another), making single-direction causality incomplete.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When the system is well-approximated by linear causality at one scale.
- When your goal is local mechanism isolation (but label it as partial).

**Keywords for Situations:**
Circular causality; multi-scale modeling; ecology; governance; systems analysis.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Identify the loop: which variables mutually determine each other?
2. Specify the scale/time horizon where each causal direction is meaningful.
3. Use multiple narratives/models rather than forcing reducibility.
4. Evaluate interventions for shifting the loop, not “breaking” it.
5. Prefer robust strategies that remain valid across plausible horizons.

**Coaching Questions:**
- Where is the chicken-and-egg loop in this system?
- What changes when you shift time horizon or system boundary?
- Which “causal story” is useful for the decision, even if partial?
