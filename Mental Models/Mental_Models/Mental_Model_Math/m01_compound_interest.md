**Category:** Math

## Description: Core concept explanation

Compound interest describes how gains accumulate on both the initial principal and all prior gains, producing exponential rather than linear growth. As a mental model, it applies to any system where improvements (or harms) are reinvested and begin generating further change. This “snowball effect” appears in money, skills, habits, relationships, and errors. Compounding can be positive or negative depending on whether the accumulated output strengthens or weakens the base.

---

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
* Systems with hard caps, saturation, or diminishing returns where exponential growth cannot continue.
* When gains are not reinvested, making the process closer to simple interest.
* Environments prone to shocks that can wipe out accumulated gains (injury, bankruptcy, reputational loss).
* Short time horizons where compounding cannot meaningfully operate and may distract from higher-leverage actions.

## Thinking Steps

1. **Identify the principal:** What is actually accumulating (money, skill, trust, code quality, errors)?
2. **Check reinvestment:** Are gains (or losses) being fed back into the system?
3. **Estimate rate and frequency:** How quickly and how often does change accrue?
4. **Consider the time horizon:** Think in years or decades, not days.
5. **Account for limits and risks:** Identify ceilings, diminishing returns, and potential resets.
6. **Design for positive compounding:** Put systems in place that automatically reinvest small gains.
7. **Mitigate negative compounding:** Locate accumulating harms and break the loop.

---

## Coaching Questions

* Where do you currently see positive or negative compounding, and what exactly is accumulating?
* What small daily or weekly action could meaningfully compound over a long horizon?
* Are you reinvesting gains, or withdrawing them and limiting growth?
* What negative compounding loops (debt, stress, technical debt, trust erosion) need to be halted or reversed?
* Over the next 10–20 years, which compounding processes would most change your life or organisation if protected and sustained?
