# Cognitive Dissonance

**Category:** Meaning

It's painful to hold two opposing ideas in our mind

**Description:**
Cognitive Dissonance describes the mental discomfort experienced when someone holds two or more contradictory beliefs, values, or attitudes. To reduce this discomfort, people often rationalize, deny, or change one of the conflicting thoughts. This model highlights how humans strive for internal consistency and how this drive shapes behavior, decision-making, and belief systems.

* **When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
  When evaluating others’ decisions or beliefs—assuming they act irrationally without understanding their internal reasoning may cause misjudgment. Avoid overusing the concept to justify every inconsistency; not all contradictions stem from dissonance.

* **Keywords for Situations:**

  * Post-purchase rationalization
  * Justifying mistakes or moral choices
  * Changing opinions after feedback
  * Resistance to new information
  * Habit and behavior alignment

* **Thinking Steps:**

  1. Identify the conflicting beliefs, actions, or values.
  2. Observe emotional or behavioral signs of discomfort or justification.
  3. Analyze how the person (or you) is attempting to resolve the tension—by changing belief, behavior, or perception.
  4. Evaluate whether this resolution improves or harms long-term understanding or integrity.
  5. Use the insight to design interventions—e.g., aligning messaging, feedback, or experiences to reduce dissonance constructively.

* **Coaching Questions:**

  * What belief or assumption feels at odds with your current action?
  * How are you rationalizing this decision or situation?
  * What would happen if you accepted both ideas as true temporarily?
  * Is the discomfort telling you something valuable or just protecting your ego?
  * How can you create alignment between your beliefs and behavior?
