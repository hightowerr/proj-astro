# Reason Respecting Tendency

**Description:**
Reason Respecting Tendency is the human inclination to comply more readily with requests when reasons are given, even if the reasons are not particularly compelling. This tendency stems from our natural love of accurate cognition and joy in mental activity. People want to understand why things happen and are more likely to accept explanations, even if those explanations are not meaningful. This bias can lead to compliance with requests when any reason is provided, regardless of its validity.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When accepting decisions or requests simply because a reason is provided
- When giving superficial reasons that seem to justify decisions but aren't substantial
- When being influenced by reasons that sound logical but aren't actually relevant
- When the presence of any reason makes you less critical of the underlying request
- When reason-giving becomes a manipulation tool rather than genuine explanation

**Keywords for Situations:**
Persuasion, influence, compliance, decision making, explanations, authority, cognitive biases, behavioral psychology, reasoning, critical thinking, logic.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. **Evaluate the Reason:** Assess whether the given reason is actually relevant and valid.
2. **Look Beyond Reasons:** Don't let a stated reason make you less critical of the underlying request or decision.
3. **Question Validity:** Ask whether the reason truly justifies the action being proposed.
4. **Seek Multiple Perspectives:** Get input from others to validate whether the stated reason is substantive.
5. **Separate Reason from Request:** Consider the request independently of the reason provided.
6. **Demand Quality:** Expect meaningful, relevant reasons rather than accepting any explanation.

**Coaching Questions:**
* "Is this reason actually relevant to the decision being proposed?"
* "Would I still make this decision if no reason had been given?"
* "Am I being influenced by the fact that any reason was provided?"
* "Does this reason truly justify the action being requested?"
* "What would I think of this request without the stated reason?"
* "Is the quality of the reason appropriate for the significance of the decision?"
