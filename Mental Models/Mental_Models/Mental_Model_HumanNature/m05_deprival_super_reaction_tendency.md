# Deprival Super Reaction Tendency

**Description:**
Deprival Super Reaction Tendency is a psychological phenomenon where people react much more strongly when something they have or almost have is taken away from them. This tendency combines loss aversion (the feeling that losses are more painful than gains are pleasurable) with the specific reaction to near-wins or almost-possessed rewards. When a person almost gets something they greatly want and has it jerked away at the last moment, they will react much as if they had long owned the reward and had it jerked away. The irrational reaction to loss in this scenario is significantly more intense than in typical loss situations.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When making decisions during times of loss or potential loss
- When the stakes are high and emotional reactions could be extreme
- When trying to understand others' reactions during disappointment
- When negotiating with someone who has recently experienced a loss
- When dealing with people who have made significant investments (time, money, effort) in something that's not working out

**Keywords for Situations:**
Loss aversion, gambling, near-misses, disappointment, addiction, trading, stock selling, emotional reactions, cognitive biases, behavioral economics, loss of control, frustration, anger, attachment to outcomes.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. **Recognize the Emotional State:** Identify when you or others are experiencing strong reactions to potential or actual loss.
2. **Separate Past from Present:** Acknowledge that what was lost or what might have been achieved no longer matters for current decisions.
3. **Focus on Future Value:** Evaluate decisions based on future outcomes rather than past investments or near-misses.
4. **Accept the Reality:** Acknowledge what has already occurred and move forward with the current situation.
5. **Avoid Doubling Down:** Resist the urge to risk more to recoup losses or achieve the almost-attained outcome.
6. **Detach Emotionally:** Create space between yourself and the loss to make rational decisions.

**Coaching Questions:**
* "Am I reacting more strongly because of what I almost achieved?"
* "Would I make the same decision if I had not come so close to success?"
* "What would I do if I had never started down this path?"
* "Am I doubling down to recoup losses or avoid accepting failure?"
* "What is the most rational next step regardless of what I've invested?"
* "How would I counsel someone else in this exact situation?"
