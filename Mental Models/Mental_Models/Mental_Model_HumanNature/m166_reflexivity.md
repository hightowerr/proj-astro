## Mental Model = Reflexivity (Change Yourself to Co-evolve)

**Category = Human Nature / Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
Reflexivity is the willingness to change yourself—values, priorities, behavior patterns—to co-evolve with other humans and the environment. It contrasts with externalization: trying to preserve identity via technology/markets (“silver bullets”) without reflecting on consequences and responsibility.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When you need immediate operational action and reflection can happen later.
- When “reflexivity” becomes a vague demand without concrete commitments.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Sustainability; behavior change; moral trade-offs; responsibility; denial; identity.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Identify the identity/comfort being protected by externalization.
2. Name the consequences being ignored or deferred.
3. Choose one priority shift that reduces system-level harm.
4. Commit to behavior changes and measurable constraints (budgets/caps).
5. Review outcomes and iterate values/choices as constraints evolve.

**Coaching Questions:**
- What am I trying to preserve about my identity here?
- What responsibility am I externalizing to “technology” or “markets”?
- What behavior change would materially shift system outcomes?
