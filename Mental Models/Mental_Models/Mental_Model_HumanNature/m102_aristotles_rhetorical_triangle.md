**Category:** Human Nature

---

**Description:**  
Aristotle’s Rhetorical Triangle is a framework for persuasive communication that balances three elements — **ethos**, **logos**, and **pathos**.  
- **Ethos** refers to the speaker’s **credibility** and trustworthiness — the perceived authority or moral character that makes an audience believe what is being said.  
- **Logos** represents **logic** and reasoning — the structure, coherence, and clarity of arguments presented to support a claim.  
- **Pathos** appeals to **emotion**, connecting with the audience through empathy, storytelling, and vivid examples.  

Together, these three pillars ensure that a message resonates intellectually, emotionally, and ethically with an audience. The model underscores that persuasion is not only about logic but about establishing trust and emotional relevance.

---

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**  
- When **precision and neutrality** are more important than persuasion (e.g. legal or scientific reporting).  
- When one element dominates excessively — pure **pathos** can feel manipulative; too much **logos** can feel cold; overemphasized **ethos** can appear self-important.  
- When communicating with highly skeptical or data-driven audiences who may resist emotional appeals.  
- When cultural or contextual differences make emotional or ethical cues easy to misinterpret.

**Keywords for Situations:**  
- Persuasive communication  
- Presentations and public speaking  
- Storytelling and marketing  
- Leadership and influence  
- Conflict resolution  
- Negotiation and stakeholder alignment  
- Teaching, coaching, or motivating teams  

---

**Thinking Steps:**  
1. **Define the goal** — what action, belief, or understanding you want to inspire.  
2. **Map the audience** — their values, motivations, and potential biases.  
3. **Balance the three appeals:**  
   - **Ethos:** Establish credibility through honesty, expertise, and transparency.  
   - **Logos:** Structure your message with clear reasoning and supporting evidence.  
   - **Pathos:** Connect emotionally through relatable stories, tone, and imagery.  
4. **Test alignment:** Ensure each appeal supports, rather than contradicts, the others.  
5. **Refine delivery:** Adjust tone, visuals, and pacing to maintain trust and engagement.  

---

**Coaching Questions:**  
- Which part of my message builds **trust** (ethos)?  
- Where does my audience experience **logic** (logos)?  
- How do I evoke genuine **emotion** (pathos) without manipulation?  
- Am I tailoring the balance of ethos, logos, and pathos to this particular audience?  
- How might cultural or contextual factors shift how each appeal is received?  
- What could weaken credibility or break emotional connection in this setting?  
- After hearing my message, what do I want my audience to **feel**, **understand**, and **do**?
