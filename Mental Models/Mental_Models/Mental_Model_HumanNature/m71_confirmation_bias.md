
# Confirmation Bias

**Description**: The tendency to search for, interpret, favor, and recall information in a way that confirms or supports one's preexisting beliefs or hypotheses. It is a major pitfall in problem solving, where individuals or teams "assert the answer" and then subconsciously look only for data that aligns with their prejudices, while ignoring or dismissing contradictory information. It is one of the most pervasive and difficult biases to overcome.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)**: This is a fundamental cognitive bias to be actively and continuously mitigated in any situation that requires objective analysis. It is not a tool to be used. Its effects are particularly dangerous when testing a hypothesis, conducting research, or making a high-stakes decision.

**Keywords for Situations**: Research, analysis, decision making, forming opinions, evaluating evidence, testing a hypothesis, political discussions, performance reviews.

**Thinking Steps**:
1.  State your hypothesis or belief clearly and acknowledge your preference for it.
2.  Instead of asking "What evidence supports this?", reframe the question to: **"What would prove my hypothesis wrong?"** or **"What is the strongest argument against my position?"**
3.  Actively and explicitly search for this disconfirming evidence with the same rigor you use to search for supporting evidence.
4.  Consider alternative hypotheses and try to argue for them as passionately as you argue for your own. This is often called the "method of multiple working hypotheses."
5.  In a group setting, assign someone the formal role of **"devil's advocate"** to argue against the prevailing opinion and surface contradictory evidence.

**Coaching Questions**:
*   "We've found a lot of evidence that supports our idea. Now, what have we found that contradicts it?"
*   "Let's spend the next 30 minutes trying to 'kill' our favorite idea. What are its biggest weaknesses and what is the strongest evidence against it?"
*   "What's the most charitable interpretation of the opposing viewpoint or the data that doesn't fit our theory?"
*   "I need someone to play devil's advocate for this discussion. Your only job is to challenge our assumptions and find flaws in our logic. Who wants to take that on?"
