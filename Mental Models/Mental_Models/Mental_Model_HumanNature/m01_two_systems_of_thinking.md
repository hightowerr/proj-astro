# Two Systems of Thinking

**Description:** 
This mental model, popularized by Daniel Kahneman, describes two systems of human thinking. System 1 operates automatically and quickly, with little or no effort and no sense of voluntary control. System 2 allocates attention to the effortful mental activities that demand it, including complex computations. System 1 is reflexive and intuitive, while System 2 is deliberate and rational. Most of our daily decisions are handled by System 1, but important, complex decisions should involve System 2. The model helps explain cognitive biases and the tendency to make quick judgments based on incomplete information.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When making high-stakes decisions that require deep analysis and rational thinking
- When dealing with complex financial decisions or investments
- When under stress or emotional pressure, which can impair System 2 thinking
- When the consequences of a wrong decision are significant and irreversible
- When facing novel situations that don't have familiar patterns for System 1 to recognize

**Keywords for Situations:**
Investment decisions, strategic planning, risk assessment, hiring decisions, conflict resolution, critical thinking, financial planning, business strategy, problem solving, cognitive biases, decision making, behavioral economics.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. **Identify the Thinking System:** Determine whether you're currently using System 1 (intuitive, automatic) or System 2 (deliberate, effortful) thinking.
2. **Pause and Assess:** When facing an important decision, deliberately slow down and engage System 2 thinking.
3. **Question Initial Impulses:** Notice your first intuitive response to a problem and ask whether it's based on sufficient evidence.
4. **Check for Cognitive Shortcuts:** Look for potential biases such as anchoring, availability heuristic, or framing effects that might be influencing System 1.
5. **Seek Disconfirming Evidence:** Actively search for information that contradicts your initial intuitive judgment.
6. **Apply Deliberate Analysis:** Use System 2 to carefully evaluate the evidence and alternatives before making a final decision.

**Coaching Questions:**
* "What is my first intuitive response to this situation?"
* "Could my initial impression be wrong? What evidence contradicts this view?"
* "Am I relying too heavily on gut feeling instead of careful analysis?"
* "What would I tell someone else in this situation?"
* "What are the potential risks of going with my first instinct?"
* "Have I allocated sufficient time and mental energy to think through this problem?"
* "What would someone who disagrees with me say about this decision?"
