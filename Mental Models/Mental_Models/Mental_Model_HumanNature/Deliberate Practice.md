Deliberate Practice is a structured method for improving performance by training at the edge of your ability with clear goals, immediate feedback, and focused repetition. 

It is characterised by:
- specific improvement goals  
- breaking complex skills into sub-skills  
- practice in the learning zone (beyond comfort, below panic)  
- high concentration and mental effort  
- rapid feedback and correction  
- iterative refinement  

Emphasises:
- It is “not inherently enjoyable” and requires intense focus.  
- It rewires underlying mental/physical representations.  
- Sub-skills should be isolated and trained deliberately.  
- Experts typically sustain only limited daily hours of true deliberate practice.  
- Long-term mastery emerges from repeating this cycle for years.
---

## **When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)**
- Activities done purely for fun or relaxation.  
- Domains where feedback is too slow or noisy to guide correction.  
- When practice becomes repetitive or automatic (entrenching errors).  
- When mental fatigue is high—quality drops quickly.  
- When constraints (environmental or structural) limit improvement more than skill.  
- When foundational knowledge is missing; deliberate practice sits on top of basics.

## **Thinking Steps**
1. **Define the Unit of Improvement**  
   Break the domain into sub-skills and choose one specific weakness.
2. **Set a Measurable Goal**  
   Define what improved performance should look like.
3. **Design a Drill in the Learning Zone**  
   Push slightly beyond current capability—enough to produce errors.
4. **Deep Focus Practice**  
   Short, intense, distraction-free sessions.
5. **Get Immediate Feedback**  
   From a coach, reviewer, instrument, recording, or environment.
6. **Correct & Experiment**  
   Adjust technique based on what the feedback reveals.
7. **High-Quality Repetition**  
   Repeat the corrected technique with full attention.
8. **Reflect on Outcomes**  
   Capture insights, analyse repeated errors, identify causes.
9. **Reset the Target**  
   Move to the next bottleneck and restart the cycle.

---

## **Coaching Questions**
- What exact sub-skill am I improving?  
- What metric shows I’m getting better?  
- Is this practice session actually challenging me? 
- How will I obtain feedback after each rep?  
- What mistakes am I repeating, and why?  
- What alternative technique could I test?  
- What did today’s practice teach me?  
- How am I managing recovery to protect quality?  
- Who can provide external critique to reveal blind spots?
