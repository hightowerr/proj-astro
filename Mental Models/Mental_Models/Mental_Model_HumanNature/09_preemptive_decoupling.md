# Preemptive Decoupling

## Description
Preemptive decoupling is a strategic response where incumbents open the floodgates themselves by preemptively decoupling two or more activities in their customer value chain. Instead of forcing customers to buy all components together, companies allow customers to pick and choose any subset of activities they want to perform with the incumbent. This approach involves doing the opposite of recoupling - it opens up the customer value chain rather than closing it. It's a minimally invasive alternative to recoupling that allows companies to coexist with changing customer behaviors rather than fighting against them. Often this requires rebalancing the business model to ensure value is captured at each point where value is created.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
Preemptive decoupling should be avoided when your business model fundamentally depends on value created through coupling activities together. Use caution when it might cannibalize your primary revenue streams without clear alternatives. Avoid this approach if you lack the operational flexibility to serve customers who want only specific activities. Be cautious when your cost structure doesn't allow for profitability with decoupled service delivery. Also avoid if decoupling would give competitors access to critical customer data or insights without reciprocal value.

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify which activities in your customer value chain customers might want to separate
2. Assess which activities you currently perform that create value but don't capture value
3. Determine how to capture value from previously uncaptured activities through rebalancing
4. Evaluate what new revenue sources might be needed to support decoupled services
5. Consider how to maintain customer relationships while offering more flexible services
6. Analyze how competitors might respond to your decoupling approach
7. Plan for the operational changes needed to support more flexible service delivery
8. Design metrics to measure success of the decoupled approach
9. Consider how to communicate these changes to customers without confusing them
10. Develop contingency plans if certain decoupled services don't achieve viability

## Coaching Questions
- What activities do customers currently perform with you that they might want to separate?
- How could you capture value from activities that currently only create value?
- What would motivate customers to continue using your services after decoupling?
- How might competitors use your decoupled services against you?
- What new revenue streams would be needed to support a decoupled model?
- How would your operational costs change with decoupled service delivery?
- What customer segments would be most receptive to decoupled services?
- How would you maintain customer loyalty in a more fragmented service model?
- What partnerships might be needed to support effective decoupling?
- How would you measure whether your decoupling strategy is successful?
