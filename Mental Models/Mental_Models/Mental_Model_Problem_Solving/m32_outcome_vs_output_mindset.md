## Mental Model = Outcome vs. Output Mindset

**Category = Problem Solving**
**Description:**
This mindset shifts the definition of success from "shipping code" (outputs) to "creating value" (outcomes). An output is a deliverable—a feature, a campaign, or a product release. An outcome is a change in human behavior that drives business results. Focusing on outcomes empowers teams with autonomy to find the best solution, reduces the risk of building things nobody wants, and aligns work with actual business impact rather than just a schedule.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- **Strict Compliance Projects:** When the "value" is legally defined by the presence of a specific output (e.g., "We must have this disclaimer checkbox by law"), the output effectively is the outcome.
- **Junior Teams:** Teams with very low maturity or domain knowledge might struggle with the ambiguity of an outcome. They may need traction metrics (a specific type of outcome) or closer guidance on outputs initially while they learn.
- **Pure Optimization:** When the relationship between the output and the outcome is already proven and 100% linear (e.g., "Moving this server to the cloud will save $X"), managing by output is acceptable.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Goal setting, OKRs, product strategy, autonomy, performance measurement, feature factory.

**Thinking Steps:**
1.  **Identify the Request:** Notice when a stakeholder asks for a specific feature or deliverable (an output).
2.  **Ask "Why?":** Interrogate the request to understand the underlying goal. Why do we need this feature? What customer behavior are we trying to change? (e.g., "We want a chatbot" -> "We want to reduce support costs").
3.  **Define the Outcome:** Translate the goal into a measurable change in behavior (e.g., "Decrease average handling time" or "Increase self-service resolution rate").
4.  **Negotiate the Metric:** Ensure the outcome is a "leading indicator" (predictive, influenceable) rather than a "lagging indicator" (like revenue, which takes too long to register).
5.  **Explore Options:** Once the outcome is agreed upon, give the team the freedom to explore *multiple* potential outputs (solutions) that might achieve that result.

**Coaching Questions:**
- "If we ship this feature on time and on budget, but nobody uses it, are we successful?"
- "What human behavior will change if we build this?"
- "Are we managing to a roadmap of features (outputs) or a roadmap of problems to solve (outcomes)?"
- "Is this metric something we can influence directly, or does it depend on too many other factors?"
