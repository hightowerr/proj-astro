# Mental Model: Pretotyping

## Description
Pretotyping is the practice of testing solutions quickly without building the actual product, focusing on finding ways to simulate the product and see if clients will buy it. Unlike prototyping, which involves actually building the solution, pretotyping focuses on validating demand and problem-solution fit quickly and inexpensively. This approach allows for rapid validation of ideas before committing significant resources to development, helping determine if there's real market demand for a solution to the identified problem.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
Use with caution when:
- The solution requires complex technical validation that can't be simulated
- The problem requires physical or safety testing that pretotyping can't address
- Stakeholders require a working prototype before buy-in
- The solution's technical feasibility is the main concern
- The market validation is less important than technical validation
- The target audience cannot engage with pretend solutions
- Regulatory requirements demand actual working solutions for testing

## Keywords for Situations
- When testing new business ideas or product concepts
- When trying to validate demand for potential solutions
- When facing resource constraints for full development
- When testing solutions in new markets or with new audiences
- When developing minimum viable products or services
- When trying to validate customer problems without building solutions
- When facing high uncertainty about market demand
- When seeking to quickly test multiple solution concepts

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify the core value proposition of your solution
2. Create a simulation or mock-up that demonstrates the value without full development
3. Test with potential users to validate demand and interest
4. Look for signs that people would actually pay for or use the solution
5. Gather feedback on the concept without revealing implementation details
6. Measure real interest (e.g., pre-orders, credit card information) rather than just opinions
7. Iterate based on feedback before building the actual solution
8. Validate problem-solution fit before committing to full development

## Coaching Questions
- How can I test this concept without building the actual solution?
- What would it take to create a simulation of the solution's value?
- How can I measure real interest rather than just asking for opinions?
- What's the fastest, cheapest way to validate this idea?
- How might I get customers to commit to using the solution before it exists?
- What aspects of the solution are essential to demonstrate value?
- How can I test the problem as well as the solution?
- What would convince someone to pay for this before it exists?
