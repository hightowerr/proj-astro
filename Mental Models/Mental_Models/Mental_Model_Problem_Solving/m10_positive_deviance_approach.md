# Mental Model: Positive Deviance Approach

## Description
The Positive Deviance Approach identifies and leverages successful behaviors or strategies that already exist within a community or system, rather than imposing external solutions. It's based on the observation that in every community, there are individuals or groups whose unique practices or behaviors enable them to find better solutions to problems than their peers, despite having access to the same resources and facing the same challenges. The approach focuses on discovering these positive deviants and understanding what makes them successful, then enabling the community to adopt these successful practices.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
Use with caution when:
- The community is not open to learning from peer examples
- The problem requires technical solutions that individuals cannot implement
- Time constraints don't allow for community discovery processes
- The positive deviants' solutions are not transferable to others
- External regulation or standards require specific solution approaches
- The community lacks the capacity to implement discovered solutions
- The problem is entirely external with no internal solutions possible

## Keywords for Situations
- When facing community or organizational challenges with persistent problems
- When external solutions have failed to address local issues
- When dealing with behavioral or cultural challenges
- When resources are limited for external solution implementation
- When trying to create sustainable behavior change
- When facing challenges in education, health, or social development
- When dealing with resistance to externally imposed solutions
- When trying to understand how some individuals succeed while others fail

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify the specific problem that needs to be addressed in the community
2. Identify positive deviants - individuals who have solved the problem despite limited resources
3. Investigate what these positive deviants do differently from their peers
4. Examine how these successful behaviors can be replicated by others
5. Facilitate community discussion about the successful practices discovered
6. Enable the community to adopt and adapt the successful practices
7. Monitor the spread of positive deviant behaviors throughout the community
8. Document and share the positive deviant solutions more broadly

## Coaching Questions
- Who in our community has solved this problem despite having the same constraints?
- What are these successful individuals doing differently from others?
- How can we learn from and replicate these successful approaches?
- What prevents others from adopting the successful practices we've identified?
- How can we enable the community to discover solutions themselves?
- What are the barriers to adopting the successful behaviors of positive deviants?
- How might we facilitate peer-to-peer learning of these successful practices?
- What would need to change for these positive deviant solutions to become widespread?
