## Mental Model = Experience Mapping

**Category = Problem Solving**
**Description:**
Experience Mapping is a technique to externalize and visualize a team's collective knowledge about a customer's journey. Instead of relying on verbal debates or written documents (which allow for vague interpretations), the team draws the customer's experience—what they think, feel, and do—step by step. This exposes gaps in understanding, aligns diverse perspectives (e.g., engineering vs. design), and creates a "current best guess" that can be tested against reality.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- **Treating as Truth:** The map is an inventory of *assumptions*, not facts. Do not make critical product decisions based solely on the map without validating it through customer interviews.
- **Groupthink:** Avoid starting with a group brainstorming session. This often leads to the loudest voice dominating or the group converging on a safe, average idea. Always start with individual work first.

**Keywords for Situations:**
Kickoff meetings, identifying knowledge gaps, cross-functional alignment, user research planning, removing ambiguity.

**Thinking Steps:**
1.  **Set the Scope:** specific enough to be concrete (e.g., "How do users entertain themselves with video?") but broad enough to capture the full context (not just "How do they use our app?").
2.  **Work Individually First:** Each team member (PM, Design, Eng) must draw their *own* version of the flow to capture their unique perspective and tacit knowledge. Drawing (even stick figures) forces specificity that words do not.
3.  **Share and Explore:** Walk through each person's map. Focus on the *differences* in understanding. Ask, "I see you included a step for X, why?" rather than debating who is right.
4.  **Co-Create a Shared Map:** Synthesize the individual maps into a single team map. Identify "nodes" (events/actions) and "links" (connections).
5.  **Identify the Gaps:** Mark areas where the team is unsure or disagrees. These become the primary research questions for the next set of customer interviews.

**Coaching Questions:**
- "Are we debating this verbally, or can we draw it to see if we're talking about the same thing?"
- "Does this map represent the 'happy path' or the actual messy reality of the customer?"
- "Whose perspective is missing from this map?"
- "What evidence do we have for this step, or is it just a hunch?"
