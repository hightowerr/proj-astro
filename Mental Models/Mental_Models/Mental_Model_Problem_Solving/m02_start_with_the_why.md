# Start With The Why

## Description
Start With The Why is based on Simon Sinek's Golden Circle model, a visual representation of his Theory of Value Proposition. The Golden Circle comprises three concentric circles representing the What, How, and Why of things as we move deeper into the center of the model. The "Why" represents the purpose or motivation for which something is created. The "How" describes the processes that lead to realization of the thing's purpose, and the "What" consists of the results of processes, the outward manifestations of actions taken. This model suggests that focusing immediately on the Why (rather than the How or What) taps into the part of our brains that governs our behavior, leading to more effective communication and inspiration.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This approach should be used with caution when time constraints don't allow for deep exploration of purpose, or in crisis situations where immediate action is needed without lengthy explanation of rationale. It may also be less effective in highly technical fields where process and results matter more than purpose, or with audiences who are primarily interested in practical outcomes rather than underlying motivations. Avoid this approach when working with stakeholders who are primarily motivated by immediate results rather than long-term vision.

## Thinking Steps
1. Define the fundamental purpose of your organization, product, or initiative - why does it exist?
2. Identify the core beliefs and values that drive your actions and decisions
3. Articulate the purpose in simple, emotional terms that resonate with your audience
4. Connect your activities back to the fundamental purpose - how do daily actions support the why?
5. Test whether your message starts with inspiration rather than features or processes
6. Ensure your team understands and can communicate the why effectively
7. Make sure your products, services, and policies align with your stated purpose
8. Validate that your why differentiates you from competitors
9. Check that your why is authentic and sustainable over time
10. Embed the why consistently across all communications and touchpoints

## Coaching Questions
- Why does your organization/product/service exist beyond making money?
- What would happen if your organization disappeared? What would be lost?
- How do your core values guide your daily decisions and operations?
- What inspires your team members most about their work?
- How do customers' deepest needs connect to your fundamental purpose?
- What would you do if you knew you couldn't fail?
- How does your 'why' differentiate you from competitors?
- What would happen if you focused on what you do before explaining why you do it?
- How can you communicate your purpose more effectively to your audience?
- What evidence shows that your actions align with your stated 'why'?
