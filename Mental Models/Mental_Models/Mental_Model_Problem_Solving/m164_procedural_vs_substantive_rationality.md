## Mental Model = Procedural vs Substantive Rationality

**Category = Problem Solving / Systems Thinking**
**Description:**
Substantive rationality assumes we can model the world well enough to compute optimal actions. Procedural rationality focuses on using a decision process that remains valid under uncertainty and limited knowledge—especially in governance and sustainability contexts where models are partial and narratives are contested.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- When “procedural” becomes a substitute for making real choices.
- When the domain is stable and optimization is genuinely feasible (engineering problems with tight bounds).

**Keywords for Situations:**
Governance; sustainability; bounded rationality; scenario planning; decision hygiene.

**Thinking Steps:**
1. Identify what can be known vs what is deeply uncertain.
2. Make narrative choices explicit (boundary, scale, values).
3. Use models to test scenarios and constraints, not to claim optima.
4. Prefer robust/option-preserving actions over brittle “best guesses”.
5. Establish monitoring and update rules (how the process learns).

**Coaching Questions:**
- What would make “optimal” meaningless here?
- What process would keep us from fooling ourselves with false precision?
- How will we update beliefs and actions as the system adapts?
