# Mental Model: Learning Experiments

## Description
Learning Experiments are deliberate attempts to do things differently from how you normally do them, in order to shake things up and learn something new about a problem. Unlike regular testing which focuses on validating existing hypotheses, learning experiments are designed to generate new insights and perspectives by changing your approach. They involve trying something novel specifically to observe what happens and what can be learned, rather than to achieve a predetermined outcome. This approach helps break out of established patterns of thinking and behavior when facing persistent challenges.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
Use with caution when:
- The situation involves safety-critical systems or operations
- Changes could have serious negative consequences for stakeholders
- The problem requires immediate resolution rather than learning
- Time constraints don't allow for experimentation
- The cost of failure would be unacceptably high
- The experiment could harm relationships or organizational trust
- The team lacks psychological safety to try new approaches

## Keywords for Situations
- When facing persistent problems that aren't responding to standard approaches
- When trying to understand complex problems with multiple contributing factors
- When facing challenges that seem to require innovative solutions
- When existing approaches have repeatedly failed to solve a problem
- When trying to understand user behavior or needs that aren't clear
- When seeking to break out of conventional solution patterns
- When the problem seems to be understood but solutions aren't working
- When facing "wicked problems" that resist traditional analysis

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify your current approach to the problem that might be limiting insights
2. Design an experiment that's different from your usual approach
3. Determine what you hope to learn from the experimental approach
4. Plan specific observations you'll make during the experiment
5. Execute the experiment while maintaining awareness of what's happening
6. Document unexpected observations or insights that emerge
7. Analyze what the experiment reveals about the problem
8. Iterate with new experiments based on what you've learned

## Coaching Questions
- What would happen if we approached this problem completely differently?
- How might we design an experiment to reveal something new about this situation?
- What alternative approaches have we not yet tried?
- What do we hope to learn by doing something different?
- How can we create a safe space to try new approaches?
- What would change if we focused on learning rather than solving immediately?
- How might we observe our problem in a new way?
- What experiments could we run that would reveal hidden aspects of the issue?
