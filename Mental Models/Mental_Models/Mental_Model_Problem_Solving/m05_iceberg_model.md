# Iceberg Model

## Description
The Iceberg Model is a framework for understanding and solving complex problems by recognizing that most of the problem exists beneath the surface, unseen. Like an iceberg, only about 10% of a problem is visible at the surface while 90% remains hidden. The model identifies four levels of problems: (1) The Event Level - immediate symptoms or observable occurrences, (2) Patterns of Behavior - recurring events that show trends over time, (3) Systems and Structures - the underlying organizational processes, rules, and policies that create the patterns, and (4) Mental Models - the beliefs, values, and assumptions that shape system structures. This model helps to look beyond immediate symptoms to address root causes rather than just treating surface-level problems.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
This model should be used with caution when time constraints don't allow for deep investigation of underlying causes. It may be inappropriate for simple problems that only require addressing the visible symptoms. Avoid using it when resources are limited and you only have capacity to address immediate issues. It's also less effective when dealing with one-time, isolated incidents that aren't part of a larger pattern, or when stakeholders are only interested in quick fixes rather than sustainable solutions.

## Thinking Steps
1. Identify the visible event or symptom that's immediately apparent (tip of the iceberg)
2. Look for patterns of behavior - is this happening repeatedly? What are the trends?
3. Investigate the systems and structures that might be causing these patterns
4. Examine the mental models, beliefs, and assumptions that created the current systems
5. Determine which level your current solution efforts are targeting
6. Consider interventions at deeper levels for more sustained impact
7. Develop actions to address the mental models and structures, not just events
8. Plan for monitoring changes at all levels of the iceberg
9. Evaluate how addressing deeper levels might prevent future surface-level problems
10. Create feedback mechanisms to capture changes at each level

## Coaching Questions
- What are you seeing on the surface that concerns you most?
- What patterns do you notice if you look back over the past 3-6 months? 
- What system structures or processes might be contributing to these patterns?
- What underlying beliefs or assumptions might be driving these systems?
- Are you addressing symptoms or root causes in your current approach?
- What would change if you tackled this issue at a deeper level?
- How might addressing the system structures prevent future occurrences?
- What beliefs or mental models would need to shift to create lasting change?
- What evidence do you have that this is a pattern rather than an isolated event?
- How can you intervene at the deeper levels to create more sustainable outcomes?
