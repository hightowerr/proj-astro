# Mental Model: Problem Validation

## Description
Problem Validation is the practice of testing your problem framing through real-world testing before investing time in solutions. Like a doctor running tests to confirm a diagnosis before starting treatment, good problem solvers work to confirm that they have framed the problem correctly before switching back into solution mode. This mental model prevents investment in solving the wrong problem by validating the problem statement with stakeholders and real-world evidence before proceeding with solution development.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
Use with caution when:
- Time constraints require immediate action without validation
- The problem is already well-validated and clearly understood
- Stakeholders are not available or willing to participate in validation
- The validation process would be more expensive than attempting a solution
- Safety concerns require immediate response without validation
- The problem is obvious and uncontested among all stakeholders
- Resources are too limited to allow for validation activities

## Keywords for Situations
- When facing significant investment decisions based on problem solutions
- When stakeholders have different understandings of the problem
- When solutions have failed despite apparent problem identification
- When facing challenges that seem obvious but may be misunderstood
- When dealing with customer problems that may be misdiagnosed
- When implementing solutions that will affect many people
- When encountering resistance to proposed solutions
- When facing recurring problems that haven't been properly understood

## Thinking Steps
1. Write down your current problem framing
2. Describe the problem to the stakeholders involved
3. Get outsiders to help validate your problem understanding
4. Devise a hard test that reveals genuine stakeholder interest
5. Consider pretotyping the solution to validate the problem simultaneously
6. Compare your understanding with real-world evidence
7. Confirm that the problem is significant enough to solve
8. Validate your approach with people who have different perspectives

## Coaching Questions
- How can I test whether I've identified the right problem?
- What would happen if I described this problem to the actual stakeholders?
- How might I create a hard test to validate whether this is a real problem?
- What evidence would prove that my problem framing is incorrect?
- Who else might have a different understanding of this situation?
- How can I validate my assumptions with real-world data?
- What would happen if I solved this problem - would it matter?
- How might I test the solution and problem simultaneously?
