# Mental Model: Rethink the Goal

## Description
Rethink the Goal challenges the assumption that the stated goal is necessarily the right one to pursue. While most goals enjoy immunity from scrutiny, this mental model questions whether we're pursuing the right objective. It shifts focus from "How do we overcome this obstacle?" to "Are we pursuing the right goal?" and "Is there a better goal to pursue?" This approach can lead to radical breakthroughs by finding alternative paths to achieve what we really care about rather than focusing on traditional or assumed objectives.

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
Use with caution when:
- The goal is clearly defined and non-negotiable
- Stakeholders have explicitly agreed on the goal and changing it would cause conflict
- Time constraints don't allow for goal reevaluation
- The goal has legal or regulatory requirements
- The goal is set by higher authority with no flexibility
- There's already strong commitment and momentum toward the current goal
- The goal is simple and obviously appropriate for the situation

## Keywords for Situations
- When progress toward a goal is consistently blocked or difficult
- When pursuing goals that seem obviously right but aren't working
- When stakeholders question the value of achieving a stated goal
- When facing resource constraints that make traditional goal-achieving difficult
- When encountering unexpected resistance to goal achievement
- When goals are set without clear connection to higher-level objectives
- When success metrics don't align with desired outcomes
- When the path to goal achievement seems unnecessarily complex

## Thinking Steps
1. Write down the current goal as stated
2. Identify the higher-level goals that this goal is meant to achieve
3. Question whether the stated goal is the best way to achieve higher objectives
4. Consider alternative goals that might achieve the same higher-level outcomes
5. Map the goal hierarchy from immediate goal to ultimate desirable outcomes
6. Examine if current goals are too narrow or self-imposed
7. Consider if there are better goals to pursue that serve the same purpose
8. Validate that the goal hierarchy makes logical sense and is worth pursuing

## Coaching Questions
- Why is this goal important to achieve?
- What will happen once we achieve this goal?
- Are there other ways to achieve the same outcome?
- What if the stated goal is preventing us from achieving what we actually want?
- What is the real benefit we're trying to achieve?
- Are we so focused on the goal that we've forgotten the purpose?
- What would happen if we pursued a completely different goal?
- How might someone with different priorities approach this situation?
