## Description: Core concept explanation

The STAR method is a simple structure for answering behavioural interview questions using four parts: **Situation, Task, Action, Result**. You briefly explain the context, your responsibility, what you did, and what happened. It keeps your answers focused on what you contributed and the impact you created. It’s designed for questions asking for real examples, such as “Tell me about a time when…”.

---

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)

- The question doesn’t ask for a past example.
- You risk sounding scripted or rehearsed.
- You try to force STAR into a question that needs a direct explanation, not a story.
- You overload the Action section with unnecessary detail and lose the interviewer.
- You ignore conversational flow and deliver a rigid monologue.

---

## Keywords for Situations

- Behavioural interviews
- Competency-based questions
- “Tell me about a time when…”
- Examples involving conflict resolution, teamwork, leadership, delivery under pressure
- Explaining how you solved a problem or achieved a goal
    

---

## Thinking Steps

1. **Check the question type.** If it asks for a specific moment (“a time when…”), use STAR.
2. **Choose a clear, relevant example.** Pick one where your role and impact are easy to understand.
3. **Situation:** Give the minimal context needed to understand the setup.
4. **Task:** State the goal or responsibility you owned.
5. **Action:** Describe the key steps you took. Focus on what _you_ did.
6. **Result:** Explain what changed because of your actions. Use numbers if possible.
7. **Tie it back.** Link why this example shows the skill the interviewer cares about.

---

## **Coaching Questions**

- What was happening, and why did it matter?
- What were you responsible for?
- What did you do, step by step?
- What changed as a result of your actions?
- How does this story show the strength the interviewer asked about?
