## **Description: Core concept explanation**
The Challenge-Action-Result (CAR) model is a concise structure for turning job duties into clear achievement statements. It breaks an experience into:  
- the **Challenge** you faced,  
- the **Action** you took, and  
- the **Result** delivered.  

The value lies in shifting focus from tasks to outcomes. Instead of generic responsibilities, each CAR statement demonstrates how you created impact. It works especially well for resumes, applications, and performance reviews where the goal is to communicate contribution in a measurable, evidence-based way.

---

## **When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)**
- **No clear outcome:** If there isn’t a meaningful result, the statement becomes weak or forced.  
- **Inflating impact:** Don’t exaggerate. Stick to what you actually did.  
- **Getting too wordy:** CAR helps with clarity, not length. Keep the final line short.  
- **Wrong format:** Some documents (e.g., academic CVs) focus on lists of outputs, not short stories. Use CAR only where it strengthens the message.

---

## **Keywords for Situations**
- resume writing, CV bullet points  
- job applications, cover letters  
- interview preparation, behavioural questions  
- performance reviews, self-evaluations  
- career coaching, professional communication, storytelling  

---

## **Thinking Steps**
1. **Find the main win**  
   What was the positive outcome you helped create?

2. **Explain the challenge**  
   What problem or situation made action necessary?

3. **State what you did**  
   What steps did you personally take?

4. **Show the result**  
   What happened because of your action? Use numbers if you have them; be honest if you don’t.

5. **Make it short**  
   Combine the three parts into a clear, single line.

---

## **Coaching Questions**
- **Challenge:** What problem or need was I dealing with?  
- **Action:** What exactly did I do to fix or improve it?  
- **Result:** What changed afterward, and how can I show that?  
- **Relevance:** Why does this matter for the role I want?  
- **Storytelling:** Can I explain this in one or two simple sentences without jargon?
