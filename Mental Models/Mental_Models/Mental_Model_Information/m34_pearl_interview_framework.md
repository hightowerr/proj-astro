## Description: Core concept explanation
The PEARL interview framework helps structure behavioral interview answers by breaking a story into five parts: **Problem, Epiphany, Action, Result, Learning**.  
Unlike STAR, PEARL emphasises the *insight* that changed your approach — the “epiphany.” This highlights how you think, not just what you did. The model works best when you need to show judgment, initiative, or growth through a real experience.

---

## When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)
- The example has **no meaningful insight** — you followed a standard process with no turning point.  
- The story does **not contain a clear problem**, stakes, or decision.  
- The interviewer asks for **hypothetical**, **technical**, or **fact-based** answers rather than experience-based ones.  
- Time-boxed answers where adding “epiphany” might make the response too long.

## Thinking Steps
1. **Problem**

   * Pick a clear example where your role and impact are easy to understand.
   * State the situation and the core challenge.
   * Make the stakes visible so the interviewer sees why it mattered.

2. **Epiphany**

   * Identify the key insight that changed your direction.
   * Make sure this insight is meaningful — not something anyone would have done by default.
   * Show how this insight shaped your approach.

3. **Action**

   * Explain the steps you took, focusing on decisions and trade-offs.
   * Highlight the parts you personally drove.
   * Keep the actions logically tied to the epiphany.

4. **Result**

   * Describe what changed because of your actions.
   * Use measurable or observable outcomes where possible.
   * Keep the result connected to the original problem.

5. **Learning**

   * Extract the lesson or principle you took away.
   * Note what you'd do differently next time.
   * Tie it back to the skill the interviewer is assessing and show how the learning shaped later behaviour.


---

## Coaching Questions
- What was the core problem, and why did it matter?  
- What insight changed how you approached it?  
- Which actions did *you* take, and why were they the right ones?  
- What measurable or observable results followed?  
- What did the situation teach you, and how have you applied that learning since?
