---
model: Turkey Problem (and Inverse Turkey)
category: ["Information", "Risk"]
detectors:
  - Long histories of stability are used as evidence that a system is safe
  - Models extrapolate from repeated past outcomes without considering hidden fragility
  - Opportunities are dismissed because they haven’t happened yet
triggers:
  - Risk assessment after prolonged calm
  - Evaluating “safe” strategies, institutions, or products with tail risk
  - Innovation and search problems where success is rare
failure_modes:
  - Confusing absence of evidence with evidence of absence
  - Overconfidence from repeated confirmation
  - Ignoring that the system’s payoff is asymmetric (a single event can dominate)
aliases:
  - Induction Trap
  - Inverse Turkey Error (missing upside)
core_mechanics:
  - Repeated positive observations can precede a sudden catastrophic revision
  - Stability can be a sign of hidden risk accumulation
  - The mirror error is missing opportunities because they are rare and not yet observed
---

### Description

The Turkey Problem is the inductive trap where repeated “good days” increase confidence right up until a single event changes everything. The system looks safe because outcomes were stable, but the exposure is fragile and asymmetric. The Inverse Turkey error is the mirror: assuming something will *never* succeed because it hasn’t succeeded yet (especially in search and innovation).

---

### When to Avoid (or Use with Caution)

- When the environment is truly stationary and the downside is bounded (rare).
- When you’re using the model to justify paranoia without distinguishing likely from ruinous.

---

### Keywords for Situations

Induction, hidden fragility, tail events, “it’s never happened,” stability trap, rare opportunities.

---

### Thinking Steps

1. **Write the exposure, not the story**
   What happens on the worst day? Can one event dominate outcomes?
2. **Search for the Thanksgiving scenario**
   Identify the plausible mechanism of sudden reversal and the conditions that trigger it.
3. **Check for suppressed variability**
   Has stability been engineered (overstabilization) in a way that could increase tail risk?
4. **Use downside caps**
   Apply barbell/optionality to keep exposure survivable if the regime changes.
5. **Avoid inverse-turkey blindness**
   In search problems, keep small, repeated trials so rare upside can be discovered.

---

### Coaching Questions

- What single event could negate the whole history of “it worked before”?
- Where is the hidden fragility that stability is masking?
- If the regime changes tomorrow, what breaks first?
- What small trials could reveal upside that we currently assume doesn’t exist?
