**Category: Information**

**Description: Core concept explanation**  
Jargon consists of specialized terms used within a profession or group. It improves speed and precision for insiders but creates barriers for outsiders. The barrier typically arises from the curse of knowledge: experts forget what it is like not to know. When the audience is mixed or unfamiliar with the domain, jargon reduces clarity, slows decision-making, and weakens trust. Clear communication requires identifying audience knowledge, removing unnecessary technical language, and defining unavoidable terms.

**When to Avoid (or Use with Caution):**
- Cross-functional work where different domains use different vocabularies  
- Presentations to executives or investors who need rapid comprehension  
- Resumes or job applications screened by generalists  
- Business cases requiring cross-department approval  
- Customer-facing material where unclear language reduces trust  
- Crisis or incident communication where clarity is essential  
- Teaching or training where learners lack foundational context

**Thinking Steps:**
1. **Audience analysis**: Identify what the audience actually knows.  
2. **Jargon audit**: Flag acronyms, technical terms, group-specific language.  
3. **Necessity test**: Keep a term only if it is essential for precision.  
4. **Translation decision**: Replace with plain language or add a brief definition.  
5. **External validation**: Test with someone outside the field to confirm understanding.  
6. **Iterative refinement**: Apply a simple-language test to ensure clarity.  

**Coaching Questions:**
- Who must understand this message, and what do they already know?  
- Could a non-expert restate the message accurately?  
- Is a term used because it is necessary or because it signals expertise?  
- Can the same idea be expressed with everyday words?  
- Has this been tested with someone from another department?  
- What assumptions about shared vocabulary might be wrong?  
- What risk arises if parts of the message are misunderstood?
