# Mental Models Selected for Order Summary Logic Problem

This document outlines the selection process and rationale for the mental models chosen to analyze the order summary and upsell logic problem.

## Selection Funnel Summary

From an initial list of over 280 available mental models, a broad scan identified approximately 10 potentially relevant models related to systems thinking, problem-solving, and strategic decision-making. After a deeper evaluation of the top candidates, the following three models were selected for their high relevance and complementary nature in addressing the specific challenges outlined in the problem diagnosis.

## Final Ranked List of Models

1.  **Bottlenecks**
2.  **First-Principle Thinking**
3.  **Leverage**

## Rationale for Selection

### 1. Bottlenecks

*   **Why it was chosen:** The problem diagnosis clearly points to the desktop user interface as a **bottleneck**. The inability to know the user's fulfillment preference upfront constrains the entire system's ability to provide an accurate order summary and relevant upsell offers. This model was chosen because its core focus is on identifying and addressing the single greatest constraint that limits a system's performance.
*   **Reference from model:** The model's thinking steps, such as "Identify the Constraint" and "Elevate the Bottleneck," directly apply to finding a solution for the desktop UI challenge.

### 2. First-Principle Thinking

*   **Why it was chosen:** The current situation involves a web of complex, interconnected business rules (Argos Plus vs. Argos Pay, eligibility, stock status) and platform-specific implementations (mobile vs. desktop). First-Principle Thinking is essential for cutting through this complexity. It forces a deconstruction of the problem to its fundamental truths, rather than reasoning by analogy (e.g., "the app does it this way").
*   **Reference from model:** The process of asking "Why?" repeatedly to challenge every assumption will be critical in questioning why the desktop experience is the way it is and what is truly necessary to achieve the primary goal of 100% accuracy.

### 3. Leverage

*   **Why it was chosen:** The primary goal is to achieve accuracy, but the solution must also be efficient. The principle of Leverage focuses on finding the point where the smallest effort can create the largest impact. This is crucial for prioritizing potential solutions, whether they involve a small UI change, a modification to a backend API, or a simplification of the business logic.
*   **Reference from model:** The coaching question, "If I could only change one thing to get the biggest result, what would it be?" perfectly frames the strategic choice that needs to be made to solve this problem effectively.
