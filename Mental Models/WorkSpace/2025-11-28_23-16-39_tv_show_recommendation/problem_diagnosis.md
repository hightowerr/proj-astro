# Problem Diagnosis: TV Show Recommendation

## The User
- A TV show enthusiast with a well-developed personal ranking of 26 shows.
- Has consumed a significant amount of content across multiple genres.
- Is actively seeking new viewing experiences based on established preferences.

## The Goal
To discover new TV shows that align with a specific taste profile and personal ranking system.

## The Obstacles
- Lacks a systematic way to match personal preferences with new recommendations.
- Finds community recommendations (e.g., on Reddit) to be subjective and not personalized.
- Has an extensive list of already-watched shows, making it difficult to identify what to watch next.

## The Root Cause
There is no centralized system available to the user that can analyze a personal ranking and suggest shows based on demonstrated preferences.

## The Feeling
Overwhelmed by the vast number of options without a clear pathway to discover relevant content.

## Context & Constraints
- **Platform Limitations:** Reddit recommendations are community-based, not algorithmically personalized.
- **Time Constraints:** An already extensive list of shows to potentially watch limits discovery time.
- **Subjectivity:** The nature of entertainment preferences makes generic recommendations less effective.
- **Exclusion List:** The need to avoid shows already watched.
- **Geographic Factors:** Availability of certain shows may be limited across different platforms.

## Final Problem Statement
TV show enthusiasts with established preferences lack a personalized recommendation system that can analyze their specific taste profile (demonstrated through personal rankings) and suggest relevant new content, leading to inefficient discovery processes and potential dissatisfaction with generic recommendations.
