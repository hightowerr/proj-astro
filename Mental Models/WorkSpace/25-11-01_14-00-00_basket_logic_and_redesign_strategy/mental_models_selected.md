# Mental Models Selected for Basket Logic and Redesign Strategy

From an initial list of 8 candidate models, 3 were selected to form the analytical framework for this strategic review. The focus is on providing clarity for the upcoming prioritization and design discussions.

## Final Ranked List of Models

1.  **m32_bottlenecks.md**
2.  **m51_trade-offs.md**
3.  **m21_leverage.md**

---

## Rationale for Selection

### 1. m32_bottlenecks.md

*   **Why it was chosen:** The transcript explicitly identifies a new, critical constraint: limited team capacity. The statement, "we haven't got the capacity to be able to do that...the basket redesign," makes it clear that the team itself is the primary **bottleneck** for any major strategic initiative. Analyzing all decisions through this lens is essential.
*   **Reference from file:** The model's thinking steps, particularly "Identify the Constraint" and "Subordinate Everything Else," provide a framework for accepting this capacity limit and pacing all other plans accordingly, preventing the team from over-committing.

### 2. m51_trade-offs.md

*   **Why it was chosen:** The upcoming meeting to prioritize three major redesigns (Basket, Arrange Delivery, Confirm & Pay) is a classic scenario requiring deliberate **trade-offs**. Given the capacity bottleneck, the team cannot do everything. This model provides a structured way to evaluate the options and their opportunity costs, forcing a conscious choice about what to do and, more importantly, what *not* to do.
*   **Reference from file:** The model's coaching question, "If we choose to do this, what are we choosing *not* to do as a result?" is the central question that must be answered in the prioritization meeting. The model forces a realistic discussion, moving from a wish list to a committed plan.

### 3. m21_leverage.md

*   **Why it was chosen:** When resources are constrained and trade-offs are necessary, identifying points of high **leverage** is the only way to make significant progress. This model provides the constructive lens for the analysis. It allows us to ask which of the competing initiatives (a full redesign, a smaller feature like Tagstarr, or fixing the subtotal logic) provides the most strategic impact for the development effort invested.
*   **Reference from file:** The model helps answer the core question of the redesign meeting: where should the team apply its limited force to get the biggest result? It shifts the conversation from "we can't do everything" to "what is the most impactful thing we *can* do?"
