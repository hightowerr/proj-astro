# Reasoning using Leverage

This document applies the "Leverage" mental model to identify where the team can apply its limited resources to achieve a disproportionate output.

## Step 1 & 2: Identify the Fulcrum and Desired Outcome

*   **Desired Outcome:** To improve the bottom-of-funnel customer experience and business metrics, despite having severely limited engineering capacity.
*   **The System's Fulcrum:** The fulcrum is the upcoming prioritization meeting. The decision made in that meeting is the single point where the team can apply a small amount of focused energy (a single, clear decision) to dictate the entire team's output for the next cycle, thus creating a massive impact.

## Step 3: Brainstorm Levers

Given the constraints, the team needs to find the highest-leverage actions. The transcript discusses several potential levers:

1.  **The Full Redesign (as a lever):** The engineers' argument for a "rebuild from scratch" is a leverage-based argument. They believe that while the initial effort is high, the long-term payoff (increased development velocity, easier bug fixing, ability to ship more features in the future) provides massive leverage over time. It's a lever that is hard to push initially but has a huge force multiplier.

2.  **Fixing the Subtotal Logic (as a lever):** The subtotal issue for Argos Pay and Fulfilment Pass is a point of negative leverage (friction). Fixing it is a high-leverage activity because it unblocks *multiple* strategic initiatives at once. A single fix provides a disproportionate benefit by enabling progress on several fronts.

3.  **The Prioritization Meeting (as a lever):** As identified above, the meeting itself is the highest-leverage event in the short term. A single, clear decision made here will prevent months of wasted effort from context switching.
    *   **Evidence:** The pain of context switching is felt acutely: "The worst thing in the world is context switching honestly it seems to get worse and worse". A decision that eliminates this provides enormous leverage by reclaiming that lost capacity.

4.  **The "Tagstarr" Feature (as a potential lever):** The idea of adding social proof messaging is presented as a potentially high-leverage, low-effort feature.
    *   **Evidence:** "seems like it's potentially something that we could look at doing it seems to be pretty easy to integrate". If a small, easy-to-integrate feature can significantly impact conversion, it represents a very high-leverage activity.

## Step 4: Assess the Upside and Downside

| Lever | Upside (Positive Leverage) | Downside (Negative Leverage) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Full Redesign** | Massive long-term increase in team velocity and capability. | Consumes all available capacity for an extended period, killing all other initiatives. High risk of failure/delays. |
| **Fixing Subtotal Logic** | Unblocks two major, strategic projects (Argos Pay, Fulfilment Pass). | Might be a "throwaway" effort if a full redesign is chosen shortly after, making the initial effort feel wasted. |
| **Prioritization Meeting** | Creates focus, eliminates context switching, and directs all team energy to a single point, maximizing impact. | A poor or unclear decision will amplify confusion and waste. A decision to do "a little bit of everything" would be negative leverage. |
| **Tagstarr Feature** | Potential for a quick, measurable win that boosts conversion and team morale. | Could be a distraction. Could add clutter and create a negative "Temu-like" user experience if not implemented carefully. |

## Step 5 & 6: Apply with Measure & The Human Element

The analysis points towards a clear, leveraged path forward for the meeting:

1.  **Use the meeting as the primary lever.** The highest-leverage action is to force a decision.
2.  **Decide on the core Trade-off:** The decision should be between the two most leveraged, competing strategies:
    *   **Strategy A (Long-Term Leverage):** Commit to the Full Basket Redesign.
    *   **Strategy B (Short-Term Leverage):** Commit to a series of high-leverage incremental features, starting with **(1)** fixing the subtotal logic (unblocks other work) and **(2)** exploring a small A/B test of Tagstarr (potential quick win).
3.  **Reject Negative Leverage:** The "combination" or "chunking up" approach must be rejected as it's a known source of negative leverage (context switching).

By framing the discussion around "leverage," the team can move from a feeling of being resource-constrained to a more empowered position of strategically directing their limited, precious resources to the point of maximum impact.
