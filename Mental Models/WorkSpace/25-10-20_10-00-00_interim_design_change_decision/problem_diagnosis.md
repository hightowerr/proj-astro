# Problem Diagnosis: Interim Design Change Decision

## Core Problem
The user, a Product Manager, is facing pressure to implement a "small" UI design change for the sign-in section on the empty trolley page.

## Key Factors
- **The Change:** A visually "nicer" sign-in section proposed by a designer.
- **The Conflict:** The PM believes the change is low-value and lacks a clear problem statement, while a designer and their manager are advocating for its implementation in the short term.
- **PM's Concerns:**
    1.  **No Validated Problem:** There is no evidence that the current design causes user friction.
    2.  **Redundant Effort:** The change will likely be replaced by an upcoming, larger basket redesign.
    3.  **Resource Cost:** The work involves development, QA, and potential analytics complexity, diverting resources from more impactful work.
- **Stakeholder Pressure:** The designer's manager has diplomatically pushed for the change, suggesting it's a good "interim" improvement and questioning the need for testing.
- **The Decision:** Should the team invest resources in this small, non-validated change now, or should they push back and bundle it with the future, more strategic redesign?
- **User's Feeling:** The PM feels resentful about being pushed to do something they believe is not the right decision.
