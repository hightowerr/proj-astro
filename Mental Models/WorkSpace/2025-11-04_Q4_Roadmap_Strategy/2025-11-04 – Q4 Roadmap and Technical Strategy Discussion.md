# 2025-11-04 – Q4 Roadmap and Technical Strategy Discussion

> [!goal] **Goal**
> To align on the Q4 roadmap, prioritize key initiatives (Wishlist Sync, Basket Redesign, Next.js migration), and address tactical issues related to ongoing projects like Argos Pay and Missed Offers.

> [!summary] **TL;DR**
> - Wishlist sync is a top priority for next quarter, driven by the Digital Catalogue project and the need to fix data loss issues.
> - A Next.js migration for the basket is being considered to unlock personalization for the Basket Redesign, potentially displacing "Save for Later".
> - The Argos Pay side drawer is on track for a February launch.
> - The "Missed Offers" A/B test is starting next week, with a plan to use the SP endpoint for both basket and side drawer to ensure consistency.
> - Basket subtitle and accuracy issues are acknowledged as an accepted risk for now, with potential UI workarounds.

## Context (Minto Pyramid Principle)

- **Situation** — The team is planning the roadmap for the next quarter, balancing strategic initiatives with ongoing project commitments.
- **Complication** — There are several large, competing priorities: syncing wishlists, redesigning the basket, migrating to Next.js, and delivering existing projects like Argos Pay. The backend team has a heavy workload.
- **Key Questions** — What is the MVP for wishlist sync? Should we prioritize the Next.js migration over "Save for Later"? How do we de-risk the "Missed Offers" A/B test?
- **Objective** — To make clear decisions on the Q4 priorities, define the scope of the wishlist MVP, and agree on the next steps for the Next.js discovery and other active projects.
