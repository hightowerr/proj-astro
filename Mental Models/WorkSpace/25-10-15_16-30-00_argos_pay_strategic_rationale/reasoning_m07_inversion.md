# Reasoning via Inversion

**Applied Model:** [m07_inversion.md](m07_inversion.md)

**Goal:** To understand the strategic necessity of Argos Pay by inverting the problem and identifying the guaranteed negative consequences of inaction.

---

### Thinking Steps Applied

**1. Clearly Define the Goal:**
The overarching goal is to achieve sustainable business growth, increase sales, and build long-term customer loyalty.

**2. Invert the Problem:**
Instead of asking "How do we achieve this goal?", we ask, "What would guarantee that we **fail** to grow, lose market share, and damage customer loyalty?"

**3. Brainstorm All Potential Failure Points (The Cost of Inaction):**
By choosing *not* to modernize the credit offering and launch a solution like Argos Pay, the business would guarantee the following failures:

*   **Guaranteed Loss of Market Share:** Competitors who offer seamless, modern affordability solutions (e.g., integrated Buy Now, Pay Later) will systematically capture customers at the point of purchase. The business would essentially be sending its cash-flow-conscious customers directly to competitors.

*   **Guaranteed Brand Decay:** Continuing to support a "historic," "not modern" product with a "hugely high" APR makes the entire brand appear outdated, unfair, and out of touch. This negative perception would inevitably tarnish the overall brand image, not just the financial services arm.

*   **Guaranteed Reduction in Sales & Basket Size:** Without an attractive and easy-to-use affordability option, customers will increasingly self-censor. They will abandon carts with large items, remove products to meet a budget, or delay purchases indefinitely. This directly translates to lower conversion rates and a lower average basket value.

*   **Guaranteed Erosion of Customer Loyalty:** The existing credit product creates a high-friction, negative experience. By maintaining it, the business is actively damaging a customer relationship at a critical touchpoint, encouraging them to shop elsewhere for a smoother experience next time.

*   **Guaranteed Strategic Handicap:** The source material notes that Argos Pay is intended to "multiply" other strategies. Without it, all other initiatives aimed at growth, retention, and increasing basket size are fundamentally handicapped. The business would be fighting for growth while ignoring a major lever.

**4. Create an "Avoidance List":**
Based on the failure points above, a simple "avoidance list" emerges. To succeed, the business must:
*   AVOID losing customers at checkout to competitors with better financing.
*   AVOID being perceived as an outdated or unfair brand.
*   AVOID forcing customers to abandon or shrink their baskets due to payment friction.
*   AVOID creating negative experiences that destroy loyalty.
*   AVOID hobbling other strategic growth initiatives.

**Conclusion from Inversion:**
Doing nothing is not a neutral act; it is a guaranteed path toward brand decay and commercial decline. The "why" of Argos Pay is therefore not just about capturing an opportunity; it is a **critical defensive maneuver to avoid a predictable and certain failure mode.** It is the necessary, non-stupid action required to reverse a course of guaranteed negative outcomes.
