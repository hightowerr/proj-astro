# Mental Model Selection

**Analysis:** To understand the strategic rationale ("the why") for the Argos Pay initiative.

**Selection Funnel:** From an initial list of over 100 models, 3 were deeply evaluated, and the final 3 were selected.

---

### Final Ranked Selection

1.  **First-Principle Thinking**
2.  **Inversion**
3.  **Trust**

---

### Rationale for Selection

1.  **First-Principle Thinking:**
    *   **Reason:** The user's request is to understand the fundamental "why." This model is specifically designed to break down a concept to its most basic, undeniable truths. It will allow us to move past assumptions (e.g., "we need a new credit product") and build the strategic case from the ground up (e.g., "customers need to acquire goods, sometimes their cash flow is limited, providing a mechanism to bridge this gap in a positive way builds loyalty and increases purchasing power").
    *   **Reference:** Its `Thinking Steps` directly map to this process: Deconstruct the problem, challenge assumptions, and reconstruct from the ground up.

2.  **Inversion:**
    *   **Reason:** To fully grasp why Argos Pay is necessary, it's crucial to understand the alternative. By inverting the problem and asking, "What would happen if we *didn't* create Argos Pay?", we can clearly articulate the risks and opportunity costs of maintaining the status quo. This directly addresses the strategic urgency.
    *   **Reference:** Its `Description` highlights its use for clarifying complex, high-stakes problems, which this initiative represents.

3.  **Trust:**
    *   **Reason:** The source document explicitly states, "we know that when people choose credit options they want trust." A financial product is not just a utility; it's a relationship. This model is essential for framing the 'why' not just as a financial decision, but as a strategic move to build a core customer relationship asset.
    *   **Reference:** Its `Keywords for Situations` (relationships, investment, leadership) and `Thinking Steps` (Assess stakes, evaluate incentives) are directly applicable to launching a new financial service.
