# Reasoning via the Trust Model

**Applied Model:** [m76_trust.md](m76_trust.md)

**Goal:** To understand why building **Trust** is a fundamental pillar of the Argos Pay strategy and a core part of its "why."

---

### Thinking Steps Applied

This analysis adapts the model's steps to evaluate the strategic importance of building customer trust with a financial product.

**1. Assess the Stakes (From the Customer's Perspective):**
*   **Upside of Granting Trust:** The customer gains immediate access to desired goods, coupled with a clear, predictable, and fair payment plan. This reduces their financial stress and helps them budget effectively. A trusted relationship makes them feel safe and respected by the brand.
*   **Downside of Misplaced Trust:** If the product is not trustworthy, the customer risks being exploited by hidden fees, confusing terms, or a negative impact on their credit score. This would lead to financial and emotional distress, shattering their trust not just in Argos Pay, but in the entire Argos brand, likely permanently.

**2. Evaluate Incentives & Reputation:**
*   **Incentive to be Trustworthy (The New Model):** By creating a fair and transparent product, the business's incentive shifts to long-term value creation. A customer who trusts Argos Pay will make more frequent and larger purchases over their lifetime. Trust itself becomes a competitive advantage and a driver of repeat business. The incentive is to build a loyal, valuable customer relationship.
*   **Incentive to be Untrustworthy (The Old Model):** The legacy card, with its "hugely high" APR, operates on a conflicting incentive structure. It profits from high interest payments, which often come at the expense of the customer's financial well-being. This prioritizes short-term extraction of value over the long-term relationship, actively destroying trust.
*   **Reputation:** The strategy for Argos Pay is to leverage the positive reputation of the core Argos brand to launch a new financial relationship, while simultaneously shedding the negative reputation of the old, untrustworthy product.

**3. Establish Clear Expectations (The Foundation of the New Product):**
The primary failure of the legacy product is its failure to set fair and clear expectations. The very foundation of the Argos Pay strategy must therefore be to reverse this. To be considered trustworthy, the product **must** be built on:
*   **Transparency:** No hidden fees. Simple, plain-language terms.
*   **Predictability:** The customer must know exactly what they will pay and when.
*   **Fairness:** The cost of credit must be perceived as reasonable and competitive.

The goal of a "seamless" journey, as mentioned in the source text, is about embedding these clear expectations at every step, removing ambiguity and anxiety.

**4. Start Small (How to Earn Trust Incrementally):**
The principle of "start small" is key to earning trust. The design of Argos Pay can incorporate this by allowing customers to first use the service for smaller, lower-risk purchases. A positive, simple, and transparent experience on a small transaction builds the customer's confidence to use the service for larger, more significant purchases in the future. This incremental approach is a core reason for the success of modern BNPL services.

**Conclusion from the Trust Model:**
The "why" of Argos Pay, when viewed through the lens of Trust, is a fundamental strategic pivot. It is a decision to **stop extracting value through a low-trust, high-friction financial product and to start creating value by building a high-trust, low-friction relationship.**

The strategy recognizes that in the modern market, trust is a tangible asset. By investing in a fair and transparent affordability solution, the business is not just facilitating a single transaction; it is making a deliberate investment in earning the customer's long-term loyalty and business. The ultimate "why" is to build trust, because trust is what drives sustainable growth.
