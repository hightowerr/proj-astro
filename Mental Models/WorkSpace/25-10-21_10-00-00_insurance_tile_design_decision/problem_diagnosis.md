# Problem Diagnosis: Insurance Tile Design Decision

The user is facing a design decision regarding the presentation of insurance options (Term Care and Monthly Care) within a shopping basket.

**Current Situation:**
- Previously, two separate panels existed for Term Care and Monthly Care.
- These were consolidated into a single tile to reduce clutter.
- The current single tile's messaging ("get protection with monthly care cancel any time") emphasizes Monthly Care, which is the cheaper option.
- Both options (Term Care and Monthly Care) are accessible via a side drawer that opens after clicking the single tile. The side drawer provides more detailed information and allows customers to select their preferred product.

**The Dilemma:**
- A new designer, citing **advertising standards** as the basis for compliance concerns, suggests reverting to two separate cards. The concern is that presenting only one option (Monthly Care) might be seen as favoring it over Term Care.
- The user's gut feeling is that one tile is sufficient, provided the messaging on that tile is adjusted to be more neutral or inclusive of both options. The user argues that the customer journey remains the same regardless of one or two tiles, as both ultimately lead to the same side drawer for detailed selection.

**Additional Information from Questionnaire:**
- **Compliance Basis:** Advertising standards.
- **A/B Testing/User Research:** No existing data beyond Term Care performance.
- **Business Objective:** Increase Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).
- **Customer Understanding:** "Okay, not great" familiarity with insurance products.
- **Messaging Flexibility:** It is possible to rephrase the single tile's messaging to be more neutral and inclusive.

**Key Questions to Address:**
- How to balance user experience (reducing clutter) with compliance requirements (advertising standards)?
- What is the most effective way to present two distinct but related insurance options without overwhelming the user or violating compliance guidelines, while also considering CLV and customer understanding?
- How to evaluate the impact of design choices on customer behavior and conversion for both insurance products, especially given tracking issues with Monthly Care?
- What are effective strategies for rephrasing the single tile's messaging to be neutral and inclusive?
