# Draft Workshop Plan: Basket Redesign Research Questions

**Objective:** To align stakeholders (Trading, Product, Design) and generate a prioritized list of research questions to de-risk the basket redesign.

**Duration:** 1 Day

**Facilitator:** UX Research Team

---

## Agenda

### **Session 1: The Fundamentals (Morning - 90 mins)**

#### **Activity 1: What is a Basket? (First Principles)**

- **Objective:** To establish a shared, fundamental understanding of the basket's core purpose, moving beyond current implementation.
- **Time:** 30 mins
- **Materials:** Whiteboard, markers.
- **Process:**
  1. The facilitator asks the group: “From first principles, what is the absolute core purpose of an e-commerce basket?”
  2. All answers are written on the whiteboard.
  3. The group discusses and refines the answers into a single, agreed-upon sentence. (e.g., “To help users confidently and easily complete their intended purchase.”)

#### **Activity 2: Friction & Confidence Mapping**

- **Objective:** To surface all the positive and negative aspects of the current basket experience from the diverse perspectives in the room.
- **Time:** 60 mins
- **Materials:** Sticky notes (two colors, e.g., green for confidence, pink for friction), pens, a large wall or whiteboard.
- **Process:**
  1. **(15 mins) Silent Brainstorming:** Each participant individually writes down moments in the current basket journey that create **friction** (on pink notes) and moments that build **confidence** (on green notes).
  2. **(30 mins) Mapping & Clustering:** Participants place their sticky notes on the wall, grouping similar ones together. The facilitator helps to create high-level themes for the clusters (e.g., “Clarity of Costs,” “Ease of Editing,” “Unwanted Distractions”).
  3. **(15 mins) Review & Discuss:** The group briefly discusses the emerging themes, paying special attention to the largest clusters of friction points.

---

### **Session 2: The “Clutter” Debate (Morning - 45 mins)**

#### **Activity 3: Value vs. Distraction**

- **Objective:** To reframe the subjective “clutter” debate into a more objective discussion about user value, using the lens of stakeholder incentives.
- **Time:** 45 mins
- **Materials:** The clustered sticky notes from the previous activity.
- **Process:**
  1. **(10 mins) Acknowledge Incentives:** The facilitator openly acknowledges the different goals of each team (e.g., “Trading needs to drive revenue, Design needs to create a great user experience. These goals can sometimes feel like they are in tension. Let’s explore that.”).
  2. **(25 mins) Reframe the Clusters:** The group revisits the “Unwanted Distractions” cluster (and any related friction points). For each item, the group discusses: “What is the intended user value here? What is the intended business value? Where is the disconnect?”
  3. **(10 mins) Identify the Core Tension:** The facilitator summarizes the discussion, aiming to define the core tension not as “clutter vs. clean,” but as a specific, researchable problem (e.g., “How do we balance the need to present relevant offers with the user’s need for a clear path to checkout?”).

### **Session 3: From Problems to Questions (Afternoon - 105 mins)**

#### **Activity 4: “How Might We…” Generation**

- **Objective:** To turn the prioritized friction points and core tensions into actionable, optimistic research questions.
- **Time:** 60 mins
- **Materials:** Sticky notes, pens.
- **Process:**
  1. **(15 mins) Introduction to HMWs:** The facilitator explains the “How Might We…” format.
  2. **(30 mins) Silent HMW Generation:** Working from the key themes and the core tension identified earlier, each participant writes as many HMW questions as they can, one per sticky note.
  3. **(15 mins) Share and Cluster:** Participants share their HMWs, and the group clusters similar questions.

#### **Activity 5: Prioritization & Next Steps**

- **Objective:** To leave the workshop with a clear, prioritized set of research questions and a plan for what happens next.
- **Time:** 45 mins
- **Materials:** Dot stickers or a 2x2 matrix on a whiteboard (e.g., User Impact vs. Business Impact).
- **Process:**
  1. **(20 mins) Dot Voting:** Each participant is given 3-5 dots to vote on the HMW questions they believe are most critical to answer for the redesign to be successful.
  2. **(15 mins) Review & Finalize:** The group reviews the top-voted HMWs. These now form the core research questions for the project.
  3. **(10 mins) Assign Next Steps:** The facilitator confirms who is responsible for taking the prioritized questions and developing a formal research plan, and by when.
