### Mental Model Selection Report

**1. Selection Process Summary:**

From an initial scan of model categories, `SysThinking`, `General`, and `Economics` were identified as most relevant. A candidate list of approximately 10 models was generated based on filename relevance to the core problems of prioritization, risk, and resource constraints. From this list, 3 models were selected for a full-text "deep dive" evaluation. The final selection consists of these 3 models.

**2. Final Ranked List of Selected Models:**

1.  **Bottlenecks (Theory of Constraints)**
2.  **Inversion**
3.  **Opportunity Cost**

**3. Rationale for Selection:**

*   **1. Bottlenecks (Theory of Constraints):**
    *   **Reason:** Chosen because the user's data indicates a severe and explicit constraint: **80% of the team's capacity is consumed by unplanned work and "firefighting."** This model provides a direct, proven algorithm for dealing with such a situation.
    *   **Reference:** The model's `Description` states it is for identifying "a choke point in a system that limits flow and determines the system's overall capacity." The `Thinking Steps` (Map, Identify, Exploit, Subordinate, Elevate) provide a clear roadmap for the team to first stabilize and then improve its throughput.

*   **2. Inversion:**
    *   **Reason:** Chosen to address the high-stakes risk of failure during the Black Friday peak, which the user identified as having a "severe revenue loss" impact. Instead of only planning for success, this model forces a rigorous analysis of what could cause failure.
    *   **Reference:** Its `Keywords for Situations` include "project planning, risk management," which is precisely what is needed. The `Thinking Steps`—specifically "Invert the Problem: Ask, 'What would guarantee failure?'"—will focus the team on the two most critical failure points: service instability and missing the Argos Pay deadline.

*   **3. Opportunity Cost:**
    *   **Reason:** Chosen to provide a powerful framing device to communicate the true cost of the current situation to stakeholders. The 80% capacity loss isn't just a number; it represents all the valuable projects that are being implicitly abandoned.
    *   **Reference:** The model's `Description` highlights its use in fighting "Narrow Framing." The coaching question, "if you say 'yes' to this project, what are you implicitly saying 'no' to?" perfectly captures the trade-offs the team is currently being forced to make without explicitly acknowledging them. It makes the cost of inaction tangible.
