# Problem Diagnosis: Aligning on Development Philosophy (Updated)

## 1. Core Problem

The conflict between the Product Manager (user) and the Engineering Manager is a clash between two fundamentally different operating models: a **Project-Driven model** and an **Outcome-Driven (OKR) model**. The EM is incentivized to deliver large, committed projects on time (e.g., "Quick checkout"), while the PM's ambition is to shift the team to an OKR-driven approach where success is measured by moving metrics, making A/B testing and iteration critical activities.

## 2. Key Issues

*   **Conflicting Operating Models:**
    *   **EM (Project-Driven):** Success = completing previously started work and hitting large project deadlines. A/B tests are viewed as a resource drain and a distraction from committed projects.
    *   **PM (Outcome-Driven):** Success = achieving a Key Result (OKR). A/B tests are a primary tool for learning and discovery, where even failures provide value.
*   **Incentive Misalignment:** The EM's incentives ("hitting large project deadlines") are directly at odds with the PM's desire to allocate resources to iterative A/B testing, which is perceived as new, uncommitted work.
*   **Resource Contention:** This conflict is not theoretical; it is causing practical "issues of resource and unclear priorities," as seen in the "Quick checkout project." The EM is likely protecting resources for the large project, while the PM is trying to fund experimentation.
*   **Lack of a Shared Framework:** While a roadmap with "strategic and tactical" items exists, it is not effectively resolving this conflict. There is no shared agreement on how to budget or prioritize resources between large committed projects and smaller iterative experiments.
*   **Unstated Strategic Debate:** The surface-level disagreement about A/B tests is masking a deeper, unstated debate about whether the team should function as a "feature factory" (project-driven) or an "outcome-oriented" team (OKR-driven).

## 3. Desired Outcomes

*   **Short-term:** Create a shared framework with the Engineering Manager that explicitly allocates resources and provides clear prioritization criteria for both large projects and iterative experiments.
*   **Long-term:** Successfully transition the team's operating model from purely project-driven to a hybrid model that embraces outcome-oriented goals (OKRs) and uses A/B testing as a key tool for achieving them.
