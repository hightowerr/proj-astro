# Applying Scarcity & Social Proof to Generate A/B Test Ideas

This document applies the principles of Scarcity and Social Proof to generate a low-design A/B test idea for the basket, focusing on an "order-summary nudge" as mentioned in the kickoff document.

---

### Introduction: Leveraging Behavioral Nudges

Scarcity (people value things more when they're in limited supply) and Social Proof (users adapt their behaviors based on what others do) are powerful psychological motivators. They can be leveraged with simple, text-based tweaks to encourage users to complete their purchase by increasing perceived value, urgency, or confidence.

---

### A/B Test Idea: Social Proof Nudge in Order Summary

*   **Analysis:** The Social Proof model suggests that users are influenced by the actions and behaviors of others. In an e-commerce basket, this can be leveraged by subtly indicating that other customers are also interested in the items in the basket or have recently purchased them. This creates a sense of validation and can encourage completion of the purchase. This is a simple, text-based tweak that fits the "low-design" criteria.

*   **Hypothesis:** By adding a small, dynamic text message to the order summary or near the primary Call-to-Action (CTA) button (e.g., "**25 others have this item in their basket right now**" or "**Y people bought this in the last 24 hours**"), we can leverage social proof to increase user confidence and urgency, leading to a higher conversion rate.

*   **Primary Metric:** Click-through rate on the primary CTA / Overall conversion rate.

*   **Guardrail Metrics:** Returns rate (to ensure the nudge isn't leading to impulsive, regretted purchases), Average Order Value (AOV).

---

### Alternative Idea: Scarcity Nudge (Low-Stock Alert)

*   **Analysis:** The Scarcity model states that people value things more when they're in limited supply. A low-stock alert in the basket or order summary can create urgency, encouraging users to complete their purchase before the item is gone. This is also a a simple text-based tweak, though it requires accurate real-time inventory data.

*   **Hypothesis:** By displaying a clear "Low Stock" alert (e.g., "**Only 3 left!**") next to items in the basket that are running low, we can leverage scarcity to create a sense of urgency, leading to a higher conversion rate for those specific items.

*   **Primary Metric:** Conversion rate for items with low-stock alerts.

*   **Guardrail Metrics:** Customer support contacts about out-of-stock items (to ensure the alerts are accurate and not causing frustration).

---

### Conclusion

Both Social Proof and Scarcity offer powerful, low-design nudge opportunities. The Social Proof nudge is recommended as a primary candidate due to its potentially broader applicability across all basket items without requiring complex real-time stock integration for every single product in the initial sprint.
