# Mental Models Selected for A/B Test Backlog Generation

This document outlines the mental models selected to generate a backlog of A/B tests for the Basket, as per the task "Define A/B test backlog (1–3 low-design tweaks) for next sprint; add metrics & guardrails."

## Selection Summary

From an initial list of potentially relevant models (including Friction, Leverage, Scarcity, Social Proof, Cognitive Load), a combination of three has been selected. The goal is to generate concrete, high-leverage, low-design test ideas suitable for a single sprint.

---

## Final Ranked List & Rationale

### 1. Friction & Cognitive Load

*   **Rationale for Selection:** The task requires identifying "low-design tweaks." The concepts of Friction (unnecessary effort) and Cognitive Load (mental effort) are perfect for this. They focus our attention on finding and removing small obstacles in the user journey—such as confusing copy, unclear buttons (affordance), or redundant steps—which are ideal candidates for simple A/B tests.

### 2. Leverage

*   **Rationale for Selection:** To ensure the "low-design tweaks" have a meaningful impact, we must apply them at the right points. The Leverage model forces us to ask, "Where can a small change produce the biggest result?" This will help prioritize which tweaks to focus on, such as those on the highest-traffic pages or at the largest drop-off points in the basket funnel.

### 3. Scarcity & Social Proof

*   **Rationale for Selection:** The source document explicitly mentions generating "order-summary nudges." Scarcity (e.g., "Only 3 left!") and Social Proof (e.g., "50 people have this in their basket") are classic, powerful behavioral nudges. They are perfect for generating simple, often text-only, A/B test ideas that require minimal design or engineering effort but can have a significant impact on conversion.
