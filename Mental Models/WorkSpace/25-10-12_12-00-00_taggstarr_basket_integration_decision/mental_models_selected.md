# Mental Model Selection & Rationale

## Selection Process Summary

From an initial scan of 98 mental models, a candidate list of 15 potentially relevant models was created based on the problem diagnosis. A deeper evaluation of 5 of the most promising candidates was then conducted by analyzing their full content. The final selection of 3 models was made based on their direct applicability to the core challenges of the Taggstarr integration decision: managing constraints, mitigating risks, and understanding the core psychological driver.

---

## Final Ranked Selection

1.  **Bottlenecks (m32)**
2.  **Inversion (m07)**
3.  **Social Proof (m84)**

---

## Rationale for Selection

### 1. Bottlenecks

*   **Why it was chosen:** You explicitly ranked "Legal Approval" as your number one constraint. This model provides a rigorous framework for identifying and managing the primary bottleneck in a system. Instead of passively waiting for legal approval, we can use this model to structure a proactive strategy to "exploit" and "elevate" this constraint, ensuring it doesn't derail the project.
*   **Link to Diagnosis:** Directly addresses the "Internal Process" and "Constraint Hierarchy" sections of the problem diagnosis.

### 2. Inversion

*   **Why it was chosen:** Your biggest fear is using messaging that is "off-brand." The Inversion model is the perfect tool for tackling this risk head-on. By asking, "What would guarantee this experiment damages our brand?" we can identify all potential failure points (e.g., confusing messaging, negative legal interpretation, poor UX) and create a clear plan to avoid them.
*   **Link to Diagnosis:** Directly addresses the "Primary Risk" identified in the questionnaire and provides a structured way to think about risk mitigation.

### 3. Social Proof

*   **Why it was chosen:** The entire premise of using Taggstarr is to leverage the psychological principle of Social Proof. This model is essential for analyzing the core mechanic of the proposed solution. It will help us to not only understand *why* it might work but also to design the experiment and messaging in a way that maximizes the positive effects of social proof while avoiding potential negative consequences like appearing manipulative.
*   **Link to Diagnosis:** Provides the theoretical foundation for the entire "Experiment Design" and helps to ensure the "Impact on user experience" is positive.
