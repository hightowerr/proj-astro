# Reasoning via Minto Pyramid Principle

This document uses the Minto Pyramid Principle to create a clear and persuasive communication structure for presenting your basket redesign plan to your key stakeholders (George, Head of Product, Trading).

## The SCQA Introduction

This is the concise, "light touch" story you will use to open your 15-minute conversations. It sets the stage and gets straight to the point.

*   **(S) Situation:** We are in the discovery phase for the basket redesign and have gathered dozens of ideas and pain points from workshops and stakeholders.
*   **(C) Complication:** In our low-engagement culture, we cannot analyze every idea perfectly or get everyone in a room. We risk getting stuck in analysis paralysis, and we need a way to focus our design and engineering efforts on what matters most.
*   **(Q) Question:** How should we prioritize our work for the basket redesign to ensure we solve the most important problems first?
*   **(A) Answer:** We should focus first on a small number of high-impact "Quick Wins" to build momentum, while simultaneously de-risking our high-impact "Big Bets."

## The Pyramid Structure

This pyramid is the logical argument that supports your answer. It is what you will walk them through.

### Level 1: The Governing Thought (The "Answer")

**Our plan is to first deliver two 'Quick Wins' to immediately improve the customer experience, while beginning discovery on two 'Big Bets' that will drive the core goal of improving checkout efficiency.**

### Level 2: The Key Line (The Main Arguments)

This governing thought is supported by two main arguments:

1.  **We will start with two low-effort, high-impact Quick Wins.**
2.  **We will concurrently start discovery on two high-effort, high-impact Big Bets.**

### Level 3: Supporting Points (The Specifics from Your 2x2)

This is where you plug in the specific items you identified in your questionnaire. This makes the plan concrete.

**1. We will start with two low-effort, high-impact Quick Wins:**
    *   **Add reviews to the product card:** This is a simple change that can increase customer confidence and reduce basket abandonment.
    *   **Add empty trolley recommendations:** This is a known pattern to help users who have hit a dead end, directly supporting basket-building.

**2. We will concurrently start discovery on two high-effort, high-impact Big Bets:**
    *   **Make it easy for users to get full value from offers:** This directly addresses a key frustration and is a major lever for improving checkout efficiency and CSAT.
    *   **Fix localisation:** This is a foundational issue that, once solved, will improve the experience for a large segment of users and prevent future problems.

### The "So What?"

This structure allows you to present a clear, decisive plan in minutes. It shows you have a process, you have made choices, and you are focused on impact. It transforms your story map from a confusing list of ideas into a strategic tool, and it gives your stakeholders a simple, concrete proposal to react to, making their feedback much easier to obtain.
