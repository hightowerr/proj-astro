# Mental Models Selected for Lister App Proposition Analysis

This document records the selection process and rationale for choosing the mental models to analyze the "Lister" Shape Up pitch.

## 1. Selection Funnel

An initial scan of model categories in `Mental_Models/` suggested candidates from `SysThinking`, `HumanNature`, and `Problem_Solving`. A deeper evaluation of specific files in these directories led to a candidate list of approximately nine relevant models. From this list, the following three were selected for their direct applicability to the strategic questions posed by the Lister pitch.

## 2. Final Ranked List of Models

1.  **Jobs to be Done (m0137_jobs_to_be_done.md)**
2.  **Feedback Loops (m30_feedback_loops.md)**
3.  **Pretotyping (m23_pretotyping.md)**

## 3. Rationale for Selection

### **Model 1: Jobs to be Done (JTBD)**
- **Reason:** The Lister pitch is built on the assumption that users have a "job" of expressing their identity through ranked lists and discovering new content via that identity. JTBD is the ideal model to formalize and challenge this assumption. It shifts the focus from product features (list-making) to the user's underlying goal (e.g., "Help me feel understood," "Help me discover things that align with my unique taste").
- **Reference:** The model's focus on user motivation and progress is a direct fit for analyzing a consumer application proposition.

### **Model 2: Feedback Loops**
- **Reason:** The Lister pitch's "core value moment" is explicitly described as a feedback loop: "My list changes → my recommendations change." The success of the entire system depends on this loop being positive and reinforcing. This model provides the tools to analyze the dynamics of that loop: what fuels it (new lists, updated ranks), what it produces (better recommendations), and what could inhibit it (poor recommendations, small catalogue).
- **Reference:** The model's concepts of "reinforcing loops" and "balancing loops" are essential for evaluating the viability of the core engine of the Lister app.

### **Model 3: Pretotyping**
- **Reason:** The "thinnest slice" described in the Shape Up pitch is a perfect real-world example of a pretotype. Its stated goal is to prove that "people enjoy ranking" and that "simple similarity is powerful enough." The Pretotyping model allows us to analyze the *quality* of this proposed experiment. Does the P0 actually test the biggest unknowns? Could the hypothesis be tested even more cheaply?
- **Reference:** The model's focus on "testing the appeal and usage of a potential product before building it" directly maps to the goal of the Lister P0.
