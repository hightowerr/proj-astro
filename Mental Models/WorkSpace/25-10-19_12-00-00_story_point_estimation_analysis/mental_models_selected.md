## Mental Models Selected for Analysis

From an initial list of 20+ candidates, 7 were deeply evaluated, and the final 3 were selected based on their direct applicability to breaking down user stories, evaluating estimates, and addressing bottlenecks.

### Final, Ranked List of Chosen Models:

1.  **`m64_problem_disaggregation.md` (Decomposition)**
    *   **Rationale for Selection:** Chosen because the core of the user's request is to "break down the 12 stories." This model provides a structured approach to dissecting complex problems into smaller, more manageable parts, which is essential for accurate estimation, especially when detailed story information is limited.

2.  **`m06_probabilistic_thinking.md`**
    *   **Rationale for Selection:** Story point estimation is inherently uncertain. This model is selected to help understand the range of possible outcomes for the 20-point estimate, assess the confidence level, and account for variability. This is critical for a "bottleneck" A/B test where the accuracy of the estimate directly impacts project timelines and outcomes.

3.  **`m32_bottlenecks.md`**
    *   **Rationale for Selection:** The user explicitly stated that the estimation process is a "Bottleneck" for a high-leverage A/B test. This model will be used to identify the root causes of this bottleneck, analyze its impact on the project, and explore strategies to alleviate it, thereby improving the efficiency and reliability of future estimations.
