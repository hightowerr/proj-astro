# Analysis Report: A Framework for Validating Basket Customer Evidence

### Executive Summary

This report outlines a structured, three-part framework to guide the task of "Validate/refresh customer evidence for existing Basket problems." The goal is to de-risk the upcoming redesign by ensuring it is focused on solving real, validated user problems. 

The recommended approach is a sequence of three mental models:
1.  **Jobs to be Done (JTBD):** First, frame the entire research effort around what users are fundamentally trying to accomplish.
2.  **Confirmation & Survivorship Bias:** Second, apply these critical lenses to all existing and new evidence to ensure objectivity and completeness.
3.  **5 Whys:** Finally, for each validated problem, use this technique to drill down to the actionable root cause.

Executing this process will produce a prioritized list of user-centric problems, ready to inform a successful and impactful design phase.

---

### Problem Statement

The user wants to validate and refresh customer evidence related to known problems with a shopping basket/cart. The ultimate goal is to inform a redesign of the basket that solves real user problems, which is expected to improve the conversion rate. The task originates from a "Basket Redesign Research Kickoff" meeting, which implies that some "existing problems" and "customer evidence" may already be documented. The core of the task is to apply a structured thinking process to ensure this foundational research is robust, complete, and free from bias.

---

### Individual Model Analysis

#### Model 1: Jobs to be Done (JTBD)
*   **Rationale for Selection:** To shift focus from the basket's features to the customer's underlying motivation and what they are trying to accomplish.
*   **Analysis & Findings:** The analysis produced a research plan to identify the core jobs of the basket (e.g., "complete a purchase confidently," "use as a temporary holding place," "validate final cost"). The plan outlines specific research actions, such as analyzing user workarounds and interviewing for desired outcomes, to validate these jobs.

#### Model 2: Confirmation & Survivorship Bias
*   **Rationale for Selection:** To ensure the validation process is objective and avoids common research pitfalls.
*   **Analysis & Findings:** The analysis provides a concrete plan to counter these biases. To fight Confirmation Bias, the plan recommends actively seeking to disprove existing hypotheses and assigning a "devil's advocate." To fight Survivorship Bias, it mandates prioritizing research on users who *abandoned* their carts (the "silent graveyard") over those who were successful.

#### Model 3: 5 Whys
*   **Rationale for Selection:** To provide a simple, effective tool for moving from a validated problem to an actionable root cause.
*   **Analysis & Findings:** This model is positioned as the final step in the analysis of a specific problem. The reasoning document provides a worked example showing how to start with a crisp problem (e.g., "60% drop-off at shipping page") and ask "Why?" repeatedly to uncover a systemic root cause (e.g., "Initial requirements prioritized data capture over user friction"), which then leads to specific design countermeasures.

---

### Synthesis & Integrated Insights

The three selected models work together as a cohesive, end-to-end research framework:

1.  **JTBD provides the STRATEGIC FOCUS:** It answers the question, "What should we even be looking at?" by centering the entire effort on the user's struggle and desired progress. It defines what "real user problems" actually are.
2.  **Bias Analysis provides the CRITICAL LENS:** It answers the question, "How can we be sure what we're seeing is true?" It forces the team to challenge their assumptions and look for the data they are missing, not just the data they have.
3.  **5 Whys provides the TACTICAL TOOL:** It answers the question, "Now that we've found a real problem, what do we do about it?" It connects the validated problem to a specific, actionable root cause that the design team can solve.

This integrated process ensures that the team doesn't just validate a list of features, but builds a deep, empathetic, and objective understanding of the user's context, which is the foundation for a successful redesign.

---

### Actionable Options & Recommendations

It is recommended that the team adopt the following 3-step research plan to validate and refresh customer evidence:

**Step 1: Frame the Research with Jobs to be Done.**
*   Execute the research plan outlined in `reasoning_jobs_to_be_done.md`.
*   **Objective:** To define and validate the 2-3 core "Jobs" that customers hire the basket to do. All existing problems should be mapped back to their impact on these jobs.

**Step 2: Stress-Test All Evidence with Bias Analysis.**
*   Execute the research plan outlined in `reasoning_confirmation_and_survivorship_bias.md`.
*   **Objective:** To ensure all existing and new data is examined for both disconfirming evidence (to fight confirmation bias) and evidence from non-completing users (to fight survivorship bias).

**Step 3: Perform Root Cause Analysis with 5 Whys.**
*   For the top 3-5 validated problems that emerge from the steps above, conduct a 5 Whys session as outlined in `reasoning_five_whys.md`.
*   **Objective:** To produce a clear, actionable root cause and a specific countermeasure for each high-priority problem, providing a direct input for the design team.
