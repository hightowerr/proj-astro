# Mental Model Selection

From an initial list of over 100 candidate models, the following three were selected for their direct relevance to the core challenges presented in the Argos Pay strategy note.

## Final Ranked List of Chosen Models

1.  **Trust (`m76_trust.md`)**
2.  **Friction and Viscosity (`m17_friction-and-viscosity.md`)**
3.  **Leverage (`m21_leverage.md`)**

## Rationale for Selection

*   **Trust:** This was the top-ranked model because the source text explicitly states, "we know that when people choose credit options they want trust." The entire success of the Argos Pay proposition hinges on overcoming customer skepticism of credit products. This model provides the perfect framework for analyzing how to build and maintain that trust.

*   **Friction and Viscosity:** The source text emphasizes that the journey "should be seamless" and not "thrown in their faces." This model directly addresses that requirement by providing a framework for identifying and minimizing the resistance (friction) in the user journey, ensuring the path to adoption is as smooth as possible.

*   **Leverage:** The note describes Argos Pay as a way to "multiply" other strategies and "helps people build that bigger baskets." This is the language of leverage. This model is essential for analyzing how a well-implemented Argos Pay can act as a force multiplier, using a single intervention point (the basket) to create an outsized impact on multiple business goals (AOV, sales growth, retention).
