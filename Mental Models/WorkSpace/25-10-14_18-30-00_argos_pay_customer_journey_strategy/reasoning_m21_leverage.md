# Reasoning with Leverage (m21)

Applying the "Leverage" mental model to the Argos Pay integration within the basket journey.

## 1. Identify the System's Fulcrum
- The **basket page** is the point of maximum leverage. It is the final moment of consideration where a customer evaluates the total cost of their intended purchase and decides whether to proceed or abandon. A small intervention here can have a massive impact on the final outcome (conversion).

## 2. Determine the Desired Outcome
- The primary goal is to increase Average Order Value (AOV) and overall revenue, by converting users who might otherwise abandon their purchase due to the total cost.

## 3. Brainstorm Levers
- The **Argos Pay offer** is the lever itself. It's a tool that can be applied at the fulcrum (the basket) to achieve the desired outcome.
- **How to apply the lever:** The way we present the offer determines how much force is applied. A simple text link is a small lever; a prominent banner with estimated monthly payments is a much larger one.

## 4. Assess the Upside and Downside
- **Upside:** By successfully addressing affordability concerns at the critical moment, we can increase conversion rates for large baskets, boost AOV, and increase overall revenue.
- **Downside (Leverage magnifies losses):** A poorly applied lever can backfire spectacularly. If the offer is confusing, untrustworthy, or creates friction, it can decrease overall conversion by distracting or annoying users who were otherwise ready to buy. This could lead to a net loss in revenue.

## 5. Apply with Measure
- The correct approach is to start with a small, calculated application of leverage. 
- An A/B test that introduces Argos Pay only for baskets over a certain value, using a simple and clear UI, is a measured first step. We must measure not only the adoption of Argos Pay but also the overall conversion rate to ensure the lever isn't causing negative second-order effects.
