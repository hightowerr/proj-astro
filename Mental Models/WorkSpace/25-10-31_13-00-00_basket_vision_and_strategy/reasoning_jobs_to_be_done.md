# Reasoning: Jobs-To-Be-Done (JTBD) Analysis

## JTBD Application

Following the Problem Disaggregation, the JTBD framework is used to reframe the identified themes. Instead of focusing on a list of problems, we focus on the underlying "job" the user is trying to accomplish within each theme. This perspective is crucial for developing a user-centric product vision. The core Job Story format used is: **When… (situation), I want to… (motivation), so I can… (expected result)**.

### Theme 1: Clarity & Confidence

*   **Core Job Story:**
    *   **When** I am reviewing my potential purchase,
    *   **I want to** be certain that all information presented to me—stock, location, fulfillment, and final price—is accurate and reflects my choices,
    *   **so I can** feel confident and trust the system enough to proceed to checkout without hesitation or worry.

*   **Analysis:** This is the most fundamental job. If the user cannot trust the information, all other features and incentives become irrelevant. The system must eliminate ambiguity and surprises. The high number of problem statements in this theme indicates this job is currently being performed very poorly.

### Theme 2: Efficiency & Control

*   **Core Job Story:**
    *   **When** I am managing my order,
    *   **I want to** easily and quickly adjust items, quantities, and complex fulfillment options (like mixed delivery/collection) in one seamless flow,
    *   **so I can** feel in control and efficiently build the exact order I need without getting frustrated.

*   **Analysis:** This job is about reducing friction. Users feel frustration when the tool (the basket) is clunky, slow, or doesn't support their desired workflow (e.g., multi-location orders). The goal is to make the process of refining an order feel effortless.

### Theme 3: Value & Incentive

*   **Core Job Story:**
    *   **When** I am about to spend my money,
    *   **I want to** clearly see the value I'm receiving and feel like I'm getting the best possible deal (through promotions, discounts, or loyalty rewards),
    *   **so I can** feel great about my purchase and be motivated to complete it now.

*   **Analysis:** This job is about the emotional and financial justification of the purchase. It's not just about offering discounts, but about clearly communicating the value and making the user feel smart and rewarded for their choice to shop here. This directly combats purchase hesitation.

### Theme 4: Focus & Guidance

*   **Core Job Story:**
    *   **When** I have decided on my items and am ready to check out,
    *   **I want to** be guided clearly toward completing my purchase without irrelevant distractions or overwhelming clutter,
    *   **so I can** finish my order quickly and not feel pressured into unwanted actions.

*   **Analysis:** This job highlights the tension between user goals and business goals. While the business may want to upsell, users in the final stages of checkout prioritize speed and focus. The basket must intelligently balance these needs, guiding the user to the finish line while providing helpful, relevant suggestions, not distracting clutter.

### Theme 5: Personalization & Inspiration

*   **Core Job Story:**
    *   **When** I am considering my purchase,
    *   **I want** the shopping experience and the suggestions I see to feel tailored to my needs, context, and style,
    *   **so the basket can** become a helpful and inspiring tool for discovery and planning, not just a boring holding pen.

*   **Analysis:** This is a higher-order job that can only be addressed once the foundational jobs (like Clarity and Efficiency) are met. It represents a desire for the basket to be more than a utility—to be a smart, adaptive partner in the shopping process. This is where significant differentiation and delight can be created.
