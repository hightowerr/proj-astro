# Problem Diagnosis: Basket Vision and Strategy

## Core Task

The user has provided a comprehensive list of customer problem statements related to the e-commerce basket experience. The primary task is to analyze and synthesize these statements to develop a long-term product strategy and vision for the basket. The ultimate goal is to improve the basket-to-checkout ratio by creating a more accurate, efficient, and trustworthy user experience.

## Key Objectives

1.  **Thematic Analysis:** Identify the strong, recurring themes from the list of problem statements.
2.  **Structural Organization:** Organize the problem statements in a way that clarifies their relationships, overlaps, and distinctions (e.g., using a MECE framework).
3.  **Strategic Insights:** Identify leverage points, bottlenecks, and the best way to think about solving these problems strategically.
4.  **Vision Development:** Formulate a long-term vision for a redesigned basket experience that addresses the identified problems and aligns with the North Star metric.

## Input Data

A list of over 70 user-centric problem statements, such as:
*   "I can’t see stock availability before changing options, so I waste time selecting something I can’t buy."
*   "The basket feels cluttered with too many competing features, obscuring the checkout path..."
*   "I want to plan my order across multiple locations — pick some items up now, others later, and get the rest delivered — all in one basket."
*   "If I can’t see or apply promo codes easily in the basket, I might delay or look elsewhere."
*   "I want the basket to feel smarter — showing relevant suggestions based on what’s in it."

## Desired Outcome

A structured analysis that provides a clear strategic framework for the future of the basket, enabling the product team to think about long-term vision and design, rather than isolated fixes.
