# Reasoning: Problem Disaggregation Analysis

## Problem Definition

The overarching problem is to structure over 70 user problem statements into a coherent framework to inform a new product vision for the e-commerce basket, with the goal of improving the basket-to-checkout ratio.

## Logic Tree Disaggregation: Thematic Groups

After analyzing the provided problem statements, they can be disaggregated into five high-level, MECE (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive) themes. This structure forms the first "cleave" of the problem space.

### Theme 1: Clarity & Confidence
*(Problems related to accuracy, trust, and having a clear understanding of the order, including availability, pricing, and fulfillment.)*

*   I can’t see stock availability before changing options, so I waste time selecting something I can’t buy.
*   I don’t learn that items are unavailable until I reach the basket, forcing me to backtrack and rework my order.
*   I can’t see which stores have stock until I enter a postcode, making it harder to find where an item is available.
*   If my location isn’t accurate or easy to update, I’ll keep seeing wrong availability, which undermines the whole experience.
*   I can’t tell which payment options are available until checkout, so I’m unsure how I’ll pay.
*   I can’t easily see key details — basket contents, delivery price, next best action — which slows me down.
*   I want to see delivery costs upfront to decide how I want to get my order.
*   I get frustrated when complex rules stop me from buying what I want, how I want — and it’s unclear why.
*   I’m unsure if I successfully added installation or recycling, which makes me worry before checkout.
*   Seeing a “Flexible Credit Available” message when it doesn’t apply feels misleading.
*   If fulfilment and availability aren’t accurate and seamless, I won’t trust the experience.
*   I want clear confirmation that my changes — like quantity or delivery updates — have saved.

### Theme 2: Efficiency & Control
*(Problems related to the user's ability to manage and modify their basket with ease and control, including UI/UX, flow, and multi-location orders.)*

*   I want to plan my order across multiple locations — pick some items up now, others later, and get the rest delivered — all in one basket.
*   If I can’t easily manage a basket with mixed delivery and collection items, I’ll get confused or abandon my order.
*   I want the basket designed mobile-first so it feels easy to use and consistent.
*   I want the experience designed with mobile in mind first, so it feels intuitive on any device.
*   I want the basket to be fully accessible so everyone can use it easily.
*   I want to adjust item quantities quickly with plus/minus buttons instead of a dropdown.
*   I want simpler side drawers that don’t feel cluttered when I’m managing my basket.
*   If the web experience doesn’t match the simplicity of the app, the redesign will fall short.
*   I want to save items to my wishlist without having to sign in first.
*   I want the next step toward buying — like Apple Pay — to be instantly clear and seamless.
*   I want to quickly review my trolley from anywhere — a mini basket in the header would help.
*   I’d prefer a simpler, on-page basket that lets me review and adjust items without leaving the page.
*   I want the fulfilment and basket steps combined into one smooth flow.
*   I don’t think having fewer steps automatically makes the experience better — clarity matters more.

### Theme 3: Value & Incentive
*(Problems related to feeling rewarded, getting a good deal, and being motivated to complete the purchase, including promotions, discounts, and loyalty.)*

*   I sometimes spot a better offer right after ordering, which makes me want to cancel or return my purchase.
*   If I can’t see or apply promo codes easily in the basket, I might delay or look elsewhere.
*   I don’t feel motivated to complete my purchase because there aren’t enough compelling offers or incentives.
*   I want to feel rewarded for shopping — linking my in-store and online activity would make it more valuable.
*   If I leave my basket or hesitate to add items, I’d like a discount or incentive to complete my purchase.
*   If multi-buy promotions aren’t shown clearly, I might miss the deal or get confused about what to add.
*   I want to see all relevant promotions at basket so I can maximise savings before checkout.
*   I want clear reminders of why buying from Argos is worth it — like better prices or benefits.
*   I want my savings to be clearly shown so I can see the value I’m getting.
*   I’d like the option to get extra value when returning — e.g., more store credit — so I feel rewarded.
*   I want to see when I’ve hit the maximum discount available so I know I’m getting the best deal.

### Theme 4: Focus & Guidance
*(Problems related to feeling overwhelmed, distracted, or pressured, including clutter, upselling, and irrelevant recommendations.)*

*   When an item or option is out of stock, I’m not guided to suitable alternatives, leaving me at a dead end.
*   I feel overwhelmed by too many choices and information, making me more likely to abandon my purchase.
*   I feel pressured by constant upselling, which distracts me from completing my purchase.
*   I see too many calls to action competing for my attention, making it hard to focus on finishing my order.
*   The basket feels cluttered with too many competing features, obscuring the checkout path and making results hard to measure.
*   I want the basket to focus on my chosen items, not be crowded with add-ons and promotions.
*   There’s too much happening on the page, and I can’t quickly spot what matters most.
*   The interface feels gimmicky with too much gamification, making it harder to focus on completing my order.
*   I’m not sure the basket is the right place to push me to add more items — it might be better done earlier.
*   I’m not sure the basket is the right place to promote care plans — many already have insurance.
*   I’d rather see one clear option to explore care for all items instead of separate CTAs.
*   I feel like the basket pushes too many extras, making it harder to focus on my purchase.
*   I can’t easily see my basket contents because it’s crowded with too many cross-sells.
*   If simple purchases don’t have a streamlined basket, the redesign will fail for most customers.

### Theme 5: Personalization & Inspiration
*(Problems related to the basket feeling generic and not tailored to the user's needs or context, including smart suggestions and a more creative or inspiring experience.)*

*   I want to be notified when out-of-stock items in my basket are available again.
*   I shouldn’t have to enter my postcode — the site should automatically show nearby stores with stock based on my location.
*   I want the site to automatically suggest the best way to get all my items together — delivery or the nearest store with full stock.
*   I want my location to be visible and editable from anywhere on the site so availability and delivery options stay accurate.
*   I want the basket to feel smarter — showing relevant suggestions based on what’s in it.
*   I want recommendations focused on essentials — like showing batteries for a toy — not irrelevant add-ons.
*   I’d like to see how many other customers add Argos Care for the same item to feel confident adding it too.
*   I want to choose a payment plan directly from my basket and have it carry to checkout.
*   I get confused when recommended products don’t match eligibility rules — like finance offers.
*   When my basket is empty, I want personalised suggestions based on what I’ve browsed or bought.
*   I often use the basket to compare options, so it should help me weigh choices easily.
*   I’d love the basket to feel like a fitting room — a space to try out options virtually.
*   I want the basket to feel like a creative look board where I can visualise items together.
*   I want to build my basket through inspiration — curated looks, trends, and personalised recs — and save to wishlist.
*   I want personalised options shown on the product card so I can see what suits me quickly.
*   I want each brand’s basket experience to reflect its own identity — not one-size-fits-all.
*   I want personalised recommendations in the basket so it feels tailored to me.
*   I want helpful support like Trevor integrated into the basket so I can get help without leaving.
*   I want the basket to adapt to my context — showing fewer prompts when my basket is complex.

## Hypotheses and Core Problem Areas

This disaggregation reveals several core tensions and hypotheses:

1.  **The Foundational Bottleneck is "Clarity & Confidence":** Many issues in other themes (e.g., managing a mixed basket, feeling good about a purchase) are dependent on first having accurate, real-time information about availability, fulfillment, and cost. This theme is the bedrock of a functional basket.
2.  **"Focus & Guidance" vs. "Value & Incentive":** There is a direct conflict between the desire for a clean, focused path to checkout and the business need to upsell, cross-sell, and show promotions. The current implementation is causing users to feel overwhelmed and distracted.
3.  **The Basket's Role is Ambiguous:** Is the basket a simple holding pen for checkout, or is it a dynamic workspace for planning, comparison, and inspiration? The problem statements show users want it to be both, but the current design serves neither role well, leading to feature clutter and a lack of a clear primary function.
4.  **Personalization is Key to a "Smarter" Basket:** Many requests for a "smarter" experience boil down to the system understanding the user's context (location, past purchases, current basket contents) and tailoring the experience accordingly.

This structured breakdown provides a clear foundation for the next steps of the analysis: reframing these problems as Jobs-To-Be-Done and synthesizing them into a coherent product vision.
