# 2025-11-10 – Ticket Grooming & Prioritization Process

> [!goal] **Goal**
> To clarify the distinction between getting tickets ready within a workstream and the actual prioritization of work for a sprint.

> [!summary] **TL;DR**
> 
> - The focus of a workstream discussion is to get as many tickets "ready for grooming" as possible.
> - This does not determine their priority.
> - Actual priority is decided during Sprint Planning, where tickets are pulled from the "Sprint Ready" column.
> - The "Sprint Ready" column itself should be kept prioritized to avoid confusion.

## 1) Context (Minto Pyramid Principle)

- **Situation** — A question was raised about the priority of tickets being discussed in a workstream meeting.
- **Complication** — There was a concern that if low-priority items are made "sprint ready" while high-priority items are not, developers might pick up the wrong work.
- **Key Questions** — When does prioritization happen? How do we ensure developers work on the most important tasks?
- **Objective** — To confirm that prioritization is a function of Sprint Planning, not workstream grooming, and to agree that the "Sprint Ready" backlog must be actively prioritized.

## 2) Structured Tasks

- [ ] **Task:** Ensure the "Sprint Ready" column in Jira is always sorted by priority.
  - **L/N/O:** L
  - **Reasoning:** This is a high-**Leverage** (`m21_leverage.md`) process improvement. It prevents developers from accidentally picking up lower-priority work and removes ambiguity, which reduces friction and improves team velocity.
  - **Owner:** @PM
  - **Blockers:** None.
  - **Impact:** 1st order: The backlog is correctly sorted. 2nd order: The team's effort is consistently focused on the most valuable work, maximizing impact.
