# Questionnaire: Securing Architectural Support

To help you navigate this organizational challenge, please provide answers to the following questions. Your responses will clarify the power dynamics and communication channels needed to unblock your project.

1.  Besides the weakly recommended "Steve Jones", are there any other architects in the company, even if they are in different domains? Is there a directory or an organization chart you can consult?
2.  Who is the manager or head of the architecture team? Engaging them directly might be more effective than searching for an individual contributor.
3.  What is the standard, formal process for requesting an architectural review at your company? Does a form, a ticketing system (like JIRA), or a specific meeting exist for this purpose?
4.  Have you prepared a concise document (a one-pager or a short slide deck) that outlines the problem, the proposed solution (migrating to DPMS), and the specific request for architecture? Having a clear proposal makes it easier for a busy architect to engage.
5.  Who are the senior stakeholders who have a vested interest in the basket's performance (e.g., Head of Product, Head of Engineering for this division)? Their influence could be used to prioritize this request with the architecture team.
