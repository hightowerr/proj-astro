# Problem Diagnosis: Securing Architectural Support (Updated)

## 1. Core Problem

The project to migrate the basket's data source to DPMS is blocked. While the team requires architectural sign-off to proceed confidently, they are unable to secure it. The root cause appears to be a **mismatch in perceived importance**: the architecture team likely views this task as a "lower-level activity" that falls below their threshold for strategic engagement, while the development team sees it as a critical, risky change requiring expert approval.

## 2. Key Issues

*   **Perceived as Low-Level:** The core obstacle is the belief that architects are focused on "sophisticated buy vs. build" decisions, and this data source migration is not strategic enough to warrant their time.
*   **The "Approval Paradox":** The team is caught in a loop. They feel they cannot proceed without architectural approval to de-risk the work, but they cannot get this approval because the task is not deemed important enough by the architects.
*   **Lack of a Clear Path:** There is no known formal process or clear point of contact for getting this type of "mid-level" architectural guidance, leading to organizational uncertainty.
*   **No Proactive Proposal:** The team has not yet created a concise proposal or "deck" to clearly and quickly communicate the problem and the need for architectural input, relying instead on informal conversations.

## 3. Desired Outcomes

*   **Short-term:** Reframe the problem in a way that highlights its strategic importance, making it compelling enough to secure the attention of an architect.
*   **Long-term:** Establish a clear and appropriate level of engagement with the architecture team to get the DPMS migration reviewed, approved, and unblocked.
