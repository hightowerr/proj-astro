# Reasoning File 1: Framing

This document applies the mental model "Framing" to the problem of securing architectural support.

## Analysis using Thinking Steps

### 1. Identify the Core Message

The core message is: "We need an architect to review and approve our plan to migrate the basket's promotional data source to DPMS."

### 2. Determine the Desired Perception

The goal is to shift the architects' perception of this project from a **low-priority chore** to a **high-value, strategic opportunity** that is worthy of their limited time and expertise.

### 3. Choose the New Frame

The current, ineffective frame is: "We have a small, tactical problem and need a rubber stamp so we don't get into trouble." This signals low importance and administrative burden.

We must replace it with a powerful **"Strategic Enabler"** frame. This is not a single frame, but a combination of several, presented together in a concise document or a 3-slide deck.

*   **Frame 1: The "De-Risking" Frame (Loss Aversion)**
    *   **Old:** "We had a bug with a missing data field."
    *   **New:** "The recent A/B test failure was a warning shot. It exposed a critical architectural vulnerability in our most important revenue-generating journey (the basket). Our project will eliminate an entire class of future risks by removing brittle, undocumented legacy data sources."

*   **Frame 2: The "Strategic Accelerator" Frame (Shared Values)**
    *   **Old:** "We need to connect to DPMS."
    *   **New:** "We want our team to be the pilot program—the first to successfully implement the company's official DPMS strategy. This project will create the blueprint and best practices for every other team to follow, accelerating the adoption of the new company-wide architecture."

*   **Frame 3: The "Business Enabler" Frame (Value)**
    *   **Old:** "We need to fix this to run an A/B test."
    *   **New:** "Our ability to optimize the basket—a key driver of conversion—is currently blocked by this technical fragility. This migration will unblock the product team, enabling rapid testing and iteration on promotions to directly impact business results."

### 4. Define the Boundaries

*   **What to Exclude from the Frame:** In the initial pitch, do not focus on the technical minutiae of the `is_coupon_eligible` field. This is the "low-level" detail that causes architects to disengage.
*   **What to Highlight in the Frame:** Emphasize the strategic words: **risk reduction**, **pilot program**, **blueprint**, **unblock business value**, and **accelerate strategy**.

### 5. Test the Frame

The immediate next step is to embody this new frame in a tangible asset. Create a one-page document or a 3-slide deck titled something like "De-Risking the Basket & Accelerating the DPMS Strategy." The document should end with a clear, time-boxed, and easy-to-accept request:

> **Ask:** A 1-hour review session with the appropriate architect to validate our proposed approach for this strategic pilot project.

This reframed proposal is no longer a request for a tedious sign-off; it's an invitation to collaborate on a high-visibility, strategic win.
